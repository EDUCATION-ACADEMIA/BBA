Planning that is a result of being forced to react to international pressure is known as

(A) Strategic planning

(B) Tactical planning

(C) ** Reactive planning

(D) Proactive planning

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A different approach to the problem of planning under uncertainty is taken in the reactive planning paradigm. In this approach, no specific sequence of actions is planned in advance. Just as for contingency planning, the planner is given a set of initial conditions and a goal.

Concept note-2: -Reactive planning refers to the control strategy where a robot maps directly from its state to its available actions. This contrasts with deliberative planning, where a robot carries out (often extensive) computation to select an action.

Concept note-3: -What is reactive vs proactive management? Reactive management is when leaders respond to crises and issues as they arise. Proactive management means anticipating needs and challenges so that you and your team are prepared to overcome them. Itâ€™s impossible to anticipate every circumstance.

Concept note-4: -Since nearly all agents using reactive planning are proactive, some researchers have begun referring to reactive planning as dynamic planning.
