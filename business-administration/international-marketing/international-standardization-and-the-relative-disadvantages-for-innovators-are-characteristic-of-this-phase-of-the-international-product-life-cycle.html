BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

INTERNATIONAL MARKETING

[SOURCES]
international standardization and the relative disadvantages for innovators are characteristic of this phase of the international product life cycle.

(A) pha0-domestic innovation

(B) phase1-foreign innovation

(C) phase2-mature

(D) pha3-replication around the world

(E) ** phase4-reverse innovation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The industry life cycle refers to the evolution of an industry or business through four stages based on the business characteristics commonly displayed in each phase. The four phases of an industry life cycle are the introduction, growth, maturity, and decline stages.

Concept note-2: -The theory, originating in the field of marketing, stated that a product life cycle has three distinct stages: (1) new product, (2) maturing product, and (3) standardized product. The theory assumed that production of the new product will occur completely in the home country of its innovation.

Concept note-3: -Growth Stage The second stage of the product life cycle is considered the key stage for many businesses and manufacturers in establishing “a product’s position in a market, increasing sales, and improving profit margins.”

Concept note-4: -The product life cycle is the progression of a product through 5 distinct stages-development, introduction, growth, maturity, and decline.
