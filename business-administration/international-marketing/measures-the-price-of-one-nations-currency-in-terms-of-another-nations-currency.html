Measures the price of one nation’s currency in terms of another nation’s currency

(A) interest rates

(B) ** exchange rates

(C) discount rates

(D) inflation rates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An exchange rate measures the price of one currency in terms of another. In turn, the terms of trade measure how many units of the foreign goods can one unit of the domestic good acquire.

Concept note-2: -An exchange rate is a relative price of one currency expressed in terms of another currency (or group of currencies).

Concept note-3: -A currency pair is the quotation of two different currencies, with the value of one currency being quoted against the other. The first listed currency of a currency pair is called the base currency, and the second currency is called the quote currency.
