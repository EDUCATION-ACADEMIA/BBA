An expatriate is a

(A) Person who has experienced trauma while traveling back and forth due to international job assignment.

(B) Person who has no willingness to adapt to a foreign culture he/she is assigned to work in.

(C) ** person temporarily or permanently residing in a country and culture other than his/her upbringing or legal residence

(D) All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An expatriate, or expat, is an individual living and/or working in a country other than their country of citizenship, often temporarily and for work reasons. An expatriate can also be an individual who has relinquished citizenship in their home country to become a citizen of another.

Concept note-2: -While an expatriate is defined as an individual who lives outside their native country, an immigrant is an individual who lives permanently in a foreign country.

Concept note-3: -’Expatriate’ originates from the Latin words ‘ex’ (’outside of’) and ‘patria’ (one’s country). It was initially defined as anyone who left their country or was exiled from it. During the European colonial period, ‘expatriates’ referred to colonial officers who travelled to overseas colonies.

Concept note-4: -An expat – which is short for expatriate – is simply someone who lives outside their native country (the country they were born in). They may be temporarily or permanently living there for a variety of reasons including work or retirement.
