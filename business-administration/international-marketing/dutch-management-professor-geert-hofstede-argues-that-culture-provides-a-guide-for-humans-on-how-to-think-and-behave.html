Dutch management professor Geert Hofstede argues that culture provides a guide for humans on how to think and behave.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to Dutch professor Geert Hofstede, culture is a figment of our imagination. According to the text, culture can trace its origins to geography, history, technology and political economy, and social institutions.

Concept note-2: -The Individualism/Collective Index, identified by Geert Hofstede, refers to the preference for behavior that promotes one’s self-interest. When a culture scores high in Individualism/Collectivism Index, individuals fail to identify with groups.

Concept note-3: -Hofstedes framework It is a structure which helps in knowing the differences related to culture of various countries or states. All the states have their own culture which they follow, and to some extent, it shows the behavior of individuals.

Concept note-4: -Countries that score high on masculinity are task oriented, whereas countries that score higher on femininity are person oriented. Uncertainty Avoidance (UA) addresses a society’s tolerance for uncertainty and ambiguity.
