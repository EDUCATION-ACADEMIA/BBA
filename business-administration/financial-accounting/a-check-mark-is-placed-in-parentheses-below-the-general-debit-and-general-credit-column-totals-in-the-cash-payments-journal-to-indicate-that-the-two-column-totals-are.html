A check mark is placed in parentheses below the General Debit and General Credit column totals in the cash payments journal to indicate that the two column totals are

(A) posted individually

(B) posted only as part of the column total

(C) ** not posted

(D) none of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A check mark in parentheses below a General Debit column total indicates that the total is not posted. The procedure of arranging accounts in a general ledger, assigning account numbers, and keeping records current is known as file maintenance. The column total of the General Credit column is posted.

Concept note-2: -Correct Answer: Option 4. Accounts Payable.

Concept note-3: -In the cash payments journal, a check mark is used in the Post Reference column to indicate the amount has been posted to the accounts payable account in the general ledger.

Concept note-4: -The controlling account in the general ledger that summarizes the debits and credits to the individual customer accounts is Accounts Receivable.
