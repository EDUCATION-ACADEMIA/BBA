BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING

[SOURCES]
A company using the accrual method of accounting performed services on account in August. The services were for $2, 000 and the company gave the customer credit terms that state the amount is to be paid to the company in September.In September when the company receives the $2, 000 from the customer, which account should the company debit?

(A) ** Cash

(B) Accounts Receivable

(C) Service Revenue

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Debits increase stockholders’ equity accounts, and vice versa for credits.

Concept note-2: -A credit entry increases liability, revenue or equity accounts-or it decreases an asset or expense account. Thus, a credit indicates money leaving an account. You can record all credits on the right side, as a negative number to reflect outgoing money.

Concept note-3: -When the cash is deposited to the bank account, two things also change, on the bank side: the bank records an increase in its cash account (debit) and records an increase in its liability to the customer by recording a credit in the customer’s account (which is not cash).

Concept note-4: -Credits increase liability, equity, and revenue accounts. Credits decrease asset and expense accounts.
