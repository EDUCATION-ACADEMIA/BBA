Overview of Theories of AccountingPrescriptive (normative) Theories7. On 1960s and 1970s this period is commonly referred as ____ of accounting research

(A) ** Normative period

(B) Parallel period

(C) Positive period

(D) Deductive period

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The period 1956-1970 is labeled the ‘normative period’, called the formative period because it was the period when accounting theory attempted to establish norms for best accounting practice.

Concept note-2: -What is Accounting Theory? An accounting theory is a notion that uses speculations, methodologies, and frameworks in the study of financial reporting (as well as how financial reporting principles are applied in the accounting industry).

Concept note-3: -Pragmatic accounting (1800-1955) This period is often referred to as the ‘general scientific period’. The emphasis was on providing an overall framework to explain what accountants do, based upon observation of practice.

Concept note-4: -The literature dealing systematically with normative accounting theory begins in the first decade of our century with two German scholars, Johann Friedrich Schar (1846-1924) and Heinrich Nicklisch (1876-1946).
