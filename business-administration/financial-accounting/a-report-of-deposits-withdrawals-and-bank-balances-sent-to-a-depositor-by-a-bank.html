Concept note-1: -A bank reconciliation statement is a summary of banking and business activity that reconciles an entityâ€™s bank account with its financial records. The statement outlines the deposits, withdrawals, and other activities affecting a bank account for a specific period.

Concept note-2: -A report of deposits, withdrawals, and bank balances sent to a depositor by a bank is called a Bank Statement.

Concept note-3: -Any deposits not yet recorded by the bank are deposits in transit and should be added to the balance shown in the bank statement.

Concept note-4: -The bank statement informs the account holder or depositor of the balance remaining in the account, as well as keeping track of the transactions for the account holder of what he or she spent or deposited into the account on what date and to whom it was paid or received.
