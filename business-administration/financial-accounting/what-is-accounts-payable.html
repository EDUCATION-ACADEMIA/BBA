What is accounts payable?

(A) Money that you owe someone else

(B) Money that you loaned to the bank

(C) ** Money that a business owes to another business

(D) Money that you invested in a company

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Accounts payable is the money a company owes its vendors, while accounts receivable is the money that is owed to the company, typically by customers. When one company transacts with another on credit, one will record an entry to accounts payable on their books while the other records an entry to accounts receivable.

Concept note-2: -Liabilities are debts or other obligations in which your business owes money, now or in the future. Assets are items of value that your business owns, such as real estate and equipment. Assets and liabilities are part of a business’s balance sheet and are used to judge the business’s financial health.

Concept note-3: -Accounts payable (AP) is a short-term debt and a liability on a balance sheet where a business owes money to its vendors/suppliers that have provided the business with goods or services on credit.

Concept note-4: -Liabilities are everything a business owes, now and in the future. They are found on the right side of a balance sheet. A common small business liability is money owed to suppliers i.e. accounts payable.

Concept note-5: -Accounts payable (AP) represents the amount that a company owes to its creditors and suppliers (also referred to as a current liability account). Accounts payable is recorded on the balance sheet under current liabilities.
