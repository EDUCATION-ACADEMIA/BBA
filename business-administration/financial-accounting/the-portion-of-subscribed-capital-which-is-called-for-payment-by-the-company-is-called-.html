[SOURCES]
The portion of subscribed capital which is called for payment by the company is called ____

(A) Paid-up Capital

(B) Subscribed Capital

(C) ** Called-up Capital

(D) Nominal Capital

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Part of capital of a company which is called-up only on winding up is called ‘reserve capital’.

Concept note-2: -The subscribed capital is the capital that is subscribed by the public and called up capital is the capital called up by the company. The difference between this two is uncalled capital. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-3: -Called up capital is that part of the subscribed capital which has been called up on the shares.

Concept note-4: -Paid-up capital is the portion of the subscribed capital for which the company has received payment from the subscribers. A company creates paid-up capital by selling its shares directly to investors in the primary market.

Concept note-5: -Also called paid-in capital, equity capital, or contributed capital, paid-up capital is simply the total amount of money shareholders have paid for shares at the initial issuance.
