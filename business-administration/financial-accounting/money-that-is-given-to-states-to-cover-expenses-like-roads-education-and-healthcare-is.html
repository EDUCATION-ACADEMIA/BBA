Money that is given to states to cover expenses like roads, education, and healthcare is

(A) appropriations

(B) ** grants-in-aid

(C) discretionary spending

(D) mandatory spending

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Grants-in-aid are payments in the nature of assistance, donations or contributions made by one government to another government, body, institution or individual. Grants-in-aid are given by the Union Government to State Governments and/or Panchayati Raj Institutions.

Concept note-2: -Grants-in-aid are given by the Union Government to State Governments and/or Panchayati Raj Institutions. Union Government also gives substantial funds as grants-in-aid to other agencies, bodies and institutions.

Concept note-3: -NOTE-(2) (a) The general principle of grant-in-aid is that it can be given to a person or a body which is independent of the Government. One Department of the Government cannot make a grant-in-aid to another Department of the same Government.

Concept note-4: -Definition: Demand for Grants is the form in which estimates of expenditure from the Consolidated Fund, included in the annual financial statement and required to be voted upon in the Lok Sabha, are submitted in pursuance of Article 113 of the Constitution.
