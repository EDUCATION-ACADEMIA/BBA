Overview of Theories of AccountingInductive Theories23. Are the theories of accounting, mostly being developed on the basis of observation which is what accountants actually did in practice.

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is Accounting Theory? An accounting theory is a notion that uses speculations, methodologies, and frameworks in the study of financial reporting (as well as how financial reporting principles are applied in the accounting industry).

Concept note-2: -The theory base of accounting consists of principles, concepts, rules and guidelines developed over a period of time to bring uniformity and consistency to the process of accounting and enhance its utility to different users of accounting information.

Concept note-3: -Accounting theory provides a guide for effective accounting and financial reporting. Accounting theory involves the assumptions and methodologies used in financial reporting, requiring a review of accounting practices and the regulatory framework.

Concept note-4: -It helps to build an approach towards accounting practices. It covers not only present scenario, but also covers future development in an economy. Thus, Accounting theory refers to the brief set of principles, doctrines, conventions, concepts related to accountancy, evolved by analysing the accounting practices.
