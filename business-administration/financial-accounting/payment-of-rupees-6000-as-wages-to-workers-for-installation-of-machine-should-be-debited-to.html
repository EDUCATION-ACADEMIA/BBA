Payment of rupees 6000 as wages to workers for installation of machine should be debited to

(A) Wages account

(B) Repairs account

(C) ** Machinery account

(D) Cash account

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Also any expense made in connection to fixed asset before commencement of business is regarded as capital expenditure, which means the respective expenses would be debited to fixed asset A/c. Therefore, payment of Rs. 6, 000 as wages to workmen for installation of a machine should be debited to machinery A/c.

Concept note-2: -Therefore, wages paid on installation of machinery is debited to Machinery Account.

Concept note-3: -Installation charges incurred on machinery is debited to installation expense account.

Concept note-4: -Any expenditure incurred on the carriage and installation of asset such as freight, transit expenses, installation expenses, wages etc, should be treated as capital expenditure and should be debited to the Asset A/c. This expenditure is non-recurring in nature and, thus, added to the value of the asset.

Concept note-5: -Wages paid for installation of machinery debited to wages account is an error of principle.
