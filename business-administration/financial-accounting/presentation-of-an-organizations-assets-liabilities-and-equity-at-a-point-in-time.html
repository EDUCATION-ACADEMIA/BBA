FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING

[SOURCES]
Presentation of an organization’s Assets, Liabilities, and Equity at a point in time

(A) ** Balance Sheet

(B) Income Statement

(C) Statement of Cash Flows

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Format of statement IAS 1 does not prescribe the format of the statement of financial position. Assets can be presented current then non-current, or vice versa, and liabilities and equity can be presented current then non-current then equity, or vice versa.

Concept note-2: -A balance sheet summarizes a company’s assets, liabilities and shareholders’ equity at a specific point in time (as indicated at the top of the statement). It is one of the fundamental documents that make up a company’s financial statements.

Concept note-3: -Order of liquidity is the presentation of assets in the balance sheet in the order of the amount of time it would usually take to convert them into cash. Thus, cash is always presented first, followed by marketable securities, then accounts receivable, then inventory, and then fixed assets.
