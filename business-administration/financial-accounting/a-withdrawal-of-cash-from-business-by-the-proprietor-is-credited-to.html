A withdrawal of cash from business by the proprietor is credited to

(A) Drawings A/c

(B) Capital A/c

(C) ** Cash A/c

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Withdrawal of cash by proprietor should be debited to drawing account in the books of the business. Same amount should be credited by the proprietor in cash account. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-2: -When a business owner withdraws cash for personal use, these funds come out this capital account. Proprietor withdrawal cash or other asset from business recorded as credit to cash and a debit to the proprietor draws account i.e. cash in hand to decrease.

Concept note-3: -The amount which the owner withdraw from business for personal use is called as drawings. It is shown as deduction from the amount of capital in the balance sheet.

Concept note-4: -Cash is withdrawn for personal use, therefore drawings account should be debited.
