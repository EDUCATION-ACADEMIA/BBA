Obligations (amounts owed) are reported on the statement of financial position and are referred to as ____

(A) Assets

(B) ** Liabilities

(C) Owner’s Equity

(D) Expenses

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer. => Obligations (amounts owed) are reported on the balance sheet and are referred to as . Obligations (amounts owed) are reported on the balance sheet and are referred to as liabilities.

Concept note-2: -Obligations (amounts owed) are reported on the balance sheet and are referred to as. liabilities.

Concept note-3: -Overview: The balance sheet-also called the Statement of Financial Position-serves as a snapshot, providing the most comprehensive picture of an organization’s financial situation. It reports on an organization’s assets (what is owned) and liabilities (what is owed).

Concept note-4: -Liabilities are the legal debts a company owes to third-party creditors. They can include accounts payable, notes payable and bank debt. All businesses must take on liabilities in order to operate and grow.

Concept note-5: -A company’s balance sheet, also known as a “statement of financial position, ” reveals the firm’s assets, liabilities, and owners’ equity (net worth).
