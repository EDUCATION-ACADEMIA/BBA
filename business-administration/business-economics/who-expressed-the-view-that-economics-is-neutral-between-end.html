Who expressed the view that “Economics is neutral between end”?

(A) ** Robbins

(B) Marshall

(C) Pigou

(D) Adam smith

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This concept was coined into existence by the British economist Lionel Robbins. Hence, economics is neutral among ends. Also, as long as an outcome is reached, it doesn’t matter, how it was reached. Robbins expressed the view that ‘Economics should be neutral between ends’.

Concept note-2: -Lionel Robbins was a British economist who proposed a very scientific definition on economics where he emphasized on setting up an effective relationship between the human wants and scarce means to maximize the satisfaction of most of the unlimited wants in the economy.

Concept note-3: -Marshall expressed the view that economics is the study of mankind in the ordinary business of life in his definition of economics.

Concept note-4: -The definition appears in the Essay by Robbins as: “Economics is the science which studies human behaviour as a relationship between ends and scarce means which have alternative uses."

Concept note-5: -Adam Smith is the Father of Economics. Q.
