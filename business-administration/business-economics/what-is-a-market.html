what is a market?

(A) where you can buy new dresses

(B) where you can text people back and fourth

(C) where you go to draw out money

(D) ** where buyers and sellers exchange any types of goods, services and information

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Key Takeaways. A market is a place where buyers and sellers can meet to facilitate the exchange or transaction of goods and services. Markets can be physical like a retail outlet, or virtual like an e-retailer. Other examples include illegal markets, auction markets, and financial markets.

Concept note-2: -Economic market structures can be grouped into four categories: perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, and monopoly.

Concept note-3: -A market is a medium which facilitates the exchange of goods and services between buyers and sellers. A market can be physical or virtual and is defined by the different goods and services exchanged in it.

Concept note-4: -Depending on their characteristics, markets can be classed as perfectly competitive, imperfectly competitive, or monopolistic.
