Macroeconomics is the study of ____

(A) individual building blocks in the economy

(B) the relationship between different sectors of the economy

(C) household purchase decisions

(D) ** the economy as a whole

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Macroeconomics is the study of whole economiesâ€“the part of economics concerned with large-scale or general economic factors and how they interact in economies.

Concept note-2: -Macroeconomics studies how the economy behaves as a whole, including inflation, price levels, rate of growth, national income, gross domestic product and changes in employment rates.

Concept note-3: -Macroeconomics looks at the economy as a whole. It focuses on broad issues such as growth of production, the number of unemployed people, the inflationary increase in prices, government deficits, and levels of exports and imports.

Concept note-4: -Macroeconomics is a branch of economics that deals with the structure, performance and behaviour of the overall economy.

Concept note-5: -Microeconomics studies the decisions of individuals and firms to allocate resources of production, exchange, and consumption. Microeconomics deals with prices and production in single markets and the interaction between different markets but leaves the study of economy-wide aggregates to macroeconomics.
