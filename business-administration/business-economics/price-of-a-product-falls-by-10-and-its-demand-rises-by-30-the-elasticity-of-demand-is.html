Price of a product falls by 10% and its demand rises by 30%. The elasticity of demand is:

(A) 1. 1%

(B) 2.5%

(C) ** 3. 3

(D) 4. 1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answers. Price of a product falls by 10% and its demand rises by 30%. The elasticity of demand is 3.

Concept note-2: -Solution(By Examveda Team) The elasticity of demand is 3.

Concept note-3: -Therefore, the ratio of 6/5 gives us 1.2, which indicates that the demand for the commodity is elastic. The elasticity of 1.2 is assertive of the fact that when the price of the commodity falls by 5% there is an increase in demand of the commodity by 1.2 units (following the inverse relationship by the law of demand).

Concept note-4: -So, if the price of a good increases by 10 percent and the quantity demanded decreases by only 5 percent, that good is said to have inelastic demand.
