This economist believes that economics is the not only the study analysing the economic problems but also suggesting ways to attain higher standard of living.

(A) ** Paul Samuelson

(B) J.M. Keynes

(C) Lionel Robbins

(D) Adam Smith

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"To be a good economist, you must be a good political economist, ” Samuelson said. “It’s not enough to know the diagrams of supply and demand and the mathematics of econometric regressions.

Concept note-2: -He introduced the concept of “revealed preference” in a 1938 article. His goal was to be able to tell by observing a consumer’s choices whether he or she was better off after a change in prices, and indeed, Samuelson determined the circumstances under which one could tell.

Concept note-3: -John Maynard Keynes (1883–1946), one of the greatest economists of the twentieth century, pointed out that economics is not just a subject area but also a way of thinking.

Concept note-4: -Economics is the study of mankind in the ordinary business of life.-Alfred Marshall.
