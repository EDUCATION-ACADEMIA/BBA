Which of the following actions would government take to increase economic growth:

(A) Decrease exports

(B) Increase taxes

(C) ** Increase government spending

(D) Decrease government subsidies

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Increased government spending is likely to cause a rise in aggregate demand (AD). This can lead to higher growth in the short-term. It can also potentially lead to inflation.

Concept note-2: -In response to the financial slowdown and its impact on the economy, the government plays a key role by increasing its spending in order to boost economic growth.

Concept note-3: -Which of the following is a reason a government might increase government spending? It is concerned that the unemployment rate is too high.

Concept note-4: -In the early stages of sustained growth, government has often provided the incentives for entrepreneurship to take hold. In some economies the development of transportation, power, and other utilities has been carried out by the government. In others the government has offered financial inducements and subsidies.
