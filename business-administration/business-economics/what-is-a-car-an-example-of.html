What is a car an example of?

(A) A Cost

(B) A serviceA Service

(C) ** A Product/Good

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Physical products include durable goods (like cars, furniture, and computers) and nondurable goods (like food and beverages).

Concept note-2: -Material goods are those which are tangible. They can be seen, touched and transferred from one place to another. For example, cars, shoes, cloth, machines, buildings, wheat, etc., are all material goods.

Concept note-3: -Consumer goods are products used by consumers. Capital goods include items like buildings, machinery, and tools. Examples of consumer goods include food, appliances, clothing, and automobiles.

Concept note-4: -Shopping Goods Shopping goods are commodities consumers typically spend more time researching and comparing before purchase. They can range from affordable items, like clothes and home decor, to higher-end goods like cars and houses.

Concept note-5: -Cars and other vehicles that are used in the transportation industry are also capital goods because they are used to provide services for customers. The IRS classifies capital goods as tangible assets because of their physical nature.
