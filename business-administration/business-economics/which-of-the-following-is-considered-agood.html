Concept note-1: -Types of soft drinks include lemon-lime drinks, orange soda, cola, grape soda, ginger ale, and root beer. Soft drinks may be served cold, over ice cubes, or at room temperature. They are available in many container formats, including cans, glass bottles, and plastic bottles.

Concept note-2: -Coffee, tea, milk, cocoa, and undiluted fruit and vegetable juices are not considered soft drinks. The term soft drink was originated to distinguish the flavoured drinks from hard liquor, or distilled spirits.

Concept note-3: -Ingredients. Soft drinks typically contain water, sweetener (8 12%, w/v), carbon dioxide (0.3 0.6% w/v), acidulants (0.05 0.3% w/v), flavorings (0.1 0.5% w/v), colorings (0 70 ppm), chemical preservatives (lawful limits), antioxidants (<100 ppm), and/or foaming agents (e.g., saponins up to 200 mg/mL).
