APC is equal to

(A) ** 1-APS

(B) 1 / APS

(C) 1-MPS

(D) ** Consumption / Income

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The formula for average propensity to consume is as follows: Average Propensity to Consume = Consumption/Total Disposable Income. Thus, abbreviated as APC = C / DI.

Concept note-2: -APC is the ratio of consumption to income. It is the proportion of income that is consumed. It is worked out by dividing total consumption expenditure (C) by total income (Y). MPC measures the response of consumption spending to a change in income.

Concept note-3: -The average propensity to consume (APC) is the ratio of consumption expenditures (C) to disposable income (DI), or APC = C / DI.

Concept note-4: -Average propensity to consume (APC) measures the percentage of income that is spent rather than saved.

Concept note-5: -The sum of the Average Propensity to Consume (APC) and Average Propensity to save (APS) is always equal to unity, i.e., APC + APS = 1. It is so because the money income can either be spent on consumption or it can be saved.
