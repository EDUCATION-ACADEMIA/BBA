What is macro economics?

(A) The study of major problems in economies

(B) Is the social science that studies the implications of individual human action, specifically about how those decisions affect the utilization and distribution of scarce resources

(C) The study of large economies

(D) ** The study of production, distribution and exchange of goods and services in a national or global context

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Macroeconomics is a branch of economics that studies how an overall economy-the markets, businesses, consumers, and governments-behave. Macroeconomics examines economy-wide phenomena such as inflation, price levels, rate of economic growth, national income, gross domestic product (GDP), and changes in unemployment.

Concept note-2: -Macroeconomics is the study of whole economiesâ€“the part of economics concerned with large-scale or general economic factors and how they interact in economies.

Concept note-3: -Economics is a social science that focuses on the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, and analyzes the choices that individuals, businesses, governments, and nations make to allocate resources.

Concept note-4: -The study of macroeconomics involves the study of the factors affecting the economy or society as a whole rather the individual factors. It is also known as aggregate economics.
