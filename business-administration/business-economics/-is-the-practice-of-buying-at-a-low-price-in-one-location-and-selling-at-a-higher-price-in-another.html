____ is the practice of buying at a low price in one location and selling at a higher price in Another

(A) 1. Barter System

(B) ** 2. Arbitrage

(C) 3. Free Trade

(D) 4. International Trade

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Arbitrage describes the act of buying a security in one market and simultaneously selling it in another market at a higher price, thereby enabling investors to profit from the temporary difference in cost per share.

Concept note-2: -Definition: Arbitrage is the process of simultaneous buying and selling of an asset from different platforms, exchanges or locations to cash in on the price difference (usually small in percentage terms). While getting into an arbitrage trade, the quantity of the underlying asset bought and sold should be the same.

Concept note-3: -Arbitrage means taking advantage of price differences across markets to make a buck. If a currency, commodity or security-or even a rare pair of sneakers-is priced differently in two separate markets, traders buy the cheaper version and then sell it at the higher price to make money.

Concept note-4: -A More Complicated Arbitrage Example Suppose you have $1 million and you are provided with the following exchange rates: USD/EUR = 1.1586, EUR/GBP = 1.4600, and USD/GBP = 1.6939. With these exchange rates, there is an arbitrage opportunity: Sell dollars to buy euros: $1 million ÷ 1.1586 = €863, 110.
