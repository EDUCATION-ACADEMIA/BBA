A set of firms emphasizing similar strategic dimensions and using a similar strategy is called a strategic leader.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Strategic groups. A set of firms emphasizing similar strategic dimensions and using a similar strategy is called a strategic group. The competition between firms within a strategic group is greater than the competition between a member of a strategic group and companies outside that strategic group.

Concept note-2: -A strategic group is a set of firms emphasizing similar strategic dimensions to use a similar strategy. The strategic groups can be useful for analyzing an industryâ€™s competitive, and diagnosing competition, positioning, and the profitability of firms within an industry.

Concept note-3: -Any of the three dimensions of strategy-value, imitation or perimeter-can be the starting point of a strategic initiative. Several sequences of value, imitation and perimeter are possible, and each typifies a strategic development.

Concept note-4: -A strategic group is a concept used in strategic management that groups companies within an industry that have similar business models or similar combinations of strategies.
