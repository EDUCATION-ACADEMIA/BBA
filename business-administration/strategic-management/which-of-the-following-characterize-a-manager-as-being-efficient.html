Which of the following characterize a manager as being efficient?

(A) They use a minimum amount of resources for the amount of outputs produced

(B) The devote a large amount of time to planning

(C) They achieve their goals

(D) ** They interview, select, and train people who are most suitable to fill open jobs.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: The correct answer is They use a minimum amount of resources for the amount of output produced.

Concept note-2: -To be an effective manager, you need to be confident in your abilities, experience, and decision-making skills. This doesn’t mean you have to be arrogant or feel that you’re better than your employees. But you’re in a management role for a reason, your confidence will empower your team to be the best they can be.

Concept note-3: -A good manager creates an environment of trust, respect, and positivity. This provides everyone with the basis for quality and productive work. A good manager makes each team member feel equally important and creates an atmosphere of cohesion within the team, independent of the workplace itself.

Concept note-4: -Which of the following characterize a manager as being effective? They use a minimum amount of resources for the amount of outputs produced. They devote a large amount of time to planning. They achieve their goals.

Concept note-5: -A powerful manager assumes responsibility for the organization’s most significant decisions and makes every attempt to get the best out of the team. A strong manager strives to work hard in every situation and set an example for the team by achieving more personally and demonstrating a high level of efficiency.
