Which of the following statements would represent the BCG matrix cash cow?

(A) a company that has a high growth rate and high market share

(B) a company that has a high growth rate and a low market share

(C) a company that has a low growth rate and a low market share

(D) ** a company that has a low growth rate and a high market share

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A star in the BCG matrix refers to a product that has high growth and a large size in the market. Option B is the right choice here. A. A cash cow can be understood s the category of products that has low growth but has a relatively huge market share.

Concept note-2: -A cash cow is also one of four quadrants in the BCG matrix, which looks at the value of different units within a corporation. Cash cows are part of mature, slow-growing industries, have a large chunk of the market share and require minimal investment to thrive.

Concept note-3: -In addition, there are four quadrants in the BCG Matrix: Question marks: Products with high market growth but a low market share. Stars: Products with high market growth and a high market share. Dogs: Products with low market growth and a low market share.

Concept note-4: -What is the BCG Matrix? The Boston Consulting groupâ€™s product portfolio matrix (BCG matrix) is designed to help with long-term strategic planning, to help a business consider growth opportunities by reviewing its portfolio of products to decide where to invest, to discontinue, or develop products.

Concept note-5: -Dogs-Dogs represent businesses having weak market shares in low-growth markets. They neither generate cash nor require huge amount of cash. Due to low market share, these business units face cost disadvantages.
