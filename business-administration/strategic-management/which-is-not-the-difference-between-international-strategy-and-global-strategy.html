Which is not the difference between International strategy and Global strategy?

(A) the degree of involvement and separation from the centre

(B) the degree in product standardization and adaptation in local business

(C) strategy integration and competitive moves

(D) ** B and C are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An international strategy involves the tactics adopted in different countries specific to the markets of those countries whereas, a global strategy is a concept that involves putting together plans that are unique for the worldwide market.

Concept note-2: -The only answer which does not fit an international strategy is deporting, which is the removal of a person from a country for the violation of immigration law.

Concept note-3: -Multinational corporations choose from among four basic international strategies: (1) international (2) multi-domestic, (3) global, and (4) transnational.

Concept note-4: -There are three main international strategies available: (1) multidomestic, (2) global, and (3) transnational (Figure 7.23 “International Strategy”).
