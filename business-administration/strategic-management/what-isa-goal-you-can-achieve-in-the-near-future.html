What is:A goal you can achieve in the near future

(A) a long-term goal

(B) ** a short-term goal

(C) an unrealistic goal

(D) a realistic goal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A short term goal is a goal you can achieve in 12 months or less. Examples include: Take a class. Buy a new television.

Concept note-2: -A short-term goal is something you want to do in the near future. The near future can mean today, this week, this month, or even this year. A short-term goal is something you want to accomplish soon. Something that will take you a long time to accomplish is called a long-term goal.

Concept note-3: -How long are short-term goals? They’re short-term needs that you can achieve today, this week, this month, or even this year. For example, you can set a career goal like completing a skill enhancement course or a short-term savings goal like setting aside money for an emergency fund.

Concept note-4: -TIP #1 – One of your short term goals should be to land a job with their company, and then quickly learn everything you need to know about the role, how your team operates, and the company’s policies and procedures. This short term goal, once achieved, will enable you to start contributing to the company’s objectives.

Concept note-5: -A short-term goal is any goal you can achieve in 12 months or less. Some examples of short-term goals: reading two books every month, quitting smoking, exercising two times a week, developing a morning routine, etc.
