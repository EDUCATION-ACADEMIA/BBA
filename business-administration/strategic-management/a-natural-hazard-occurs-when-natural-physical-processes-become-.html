A natural hazard occurs when natural physical processes become ____

(A) dormant and inactive.

(B) ** extreme and unpredictable.

(C) scary!

(D) faster than usual.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A hazard is any natural process, or even that poses a direct threat to the human environment. The event itself is not a hazard; instead, a process or event becomes a hazard when it threatens human interests.

Concept note-2: -They can be triggered by severe thunderstorms, tropical cyclones, large low pressure systems, monsoons, ice jams or melting snow. In coastal areas, storm surge caused by tropical cyclones, tsunamis, or rivers swollen by exceptionally high tides can cause flooding.

Concept note-3: -Earthquakes are by far the most unpredictable and highly destructive of all the natural disasters.

Concept note-4: -Earthquakes are one of the most unpredictable and damaging disasters. The scientific community has yet to find a way to predict when an earthquake will occur with enough time to evacuate areas. Systems exist, but they can only give a few minutes warning to residents that a disaster is on its way.
