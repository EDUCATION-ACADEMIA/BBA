Which is not an advantage of vertical integration?

(A) Enables the firm to input and output markets to competitors

(B) Enables the firm retain control over proprietary knowledge

(C) ** Enables the firm to internationalize quickly

(D) Reduces uncertainties in demand

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vertical integration is when a firm takes ownership of a firm in a different industry. Vertical integration is when a firm expands by gaining ownership of its suppliers or distributors.

Concept note-2: -Types of Vertical Integration. There are a number of ways that a company can achieve vertical integration. Two of the most common are backward and forward integration.

Concept note-3: -Backward integration is a form of vertical integration in which a company expands its role to fulfill tasks formerly completed by businesses up the supply chain. In other words, backward integration is when a company buys another company that supplies the products or services needed for production.

Concept note-4: -So, the correct option is ‘Stratospheric ozone depletion’.
