[SOURCES]
The ____ answers the question “What do we want to accomplish?”, whereas ____ answers the question “Why do we exist?”

(A) ** Vision statement; mission statement

(B) Short-term objectives; long term objectives

(C) Objectives; strategies

(D) Vision statement; Goals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An important question a mission statement should answer is, “What do we want to become?” Whereas the mission statement answers the question, “What do we want to become?” the vision statement answers the question, “What is our business?” In order to motivate a workforce effectively, both profit and vision are needed.

Concept note-2: -A vision statement should answer the basic question, “What do we want to become?” A clear vision provides the foundation for developing a comprehensive mission statement. Many organizations have both a vision and mission statement, but the vision statement should be established first and foremost.

Concept note-3: -What are mission and vision statements? A mission statement defines the organization’s business, its objectives, and how it will reach these objectives. A vision statement details where the organization aspires to go. Why does your company exist?

Concept note-4: -A vision statement answers questions about the future state of an organization. A mission statement, however, answers the question “what is our business?".
