Which of the following is NOT one of the characteristics of reward and incentive systems?

(A) ** They do not serve as means of influencing the culture of an organization.

(B) They focus efforts on high priority tasks.

(C) They motivate high levels of individual and collective task performance.

(D) They represent an effective control mechanism.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -’Reward system refers to all the monetary, non-monetary and psychological payments that an organisation provides for its employees in exchange for the work they perform.

Concept note-2: -The purpose of the reward system is to attract, retain, and motivate qualified employees and to maintain a pay structure that is internally equitable and externally competitive. Rewards have both surface and symbolic value. Rewards take the form of money, benefits, perquisites, awards, and incentives.

Concept note-3: -A fourth mechanism used by organizations in allocating rewards is distributive justice. Under this approach, employees receive (at least a portion of) their rewards as a function of their level of contribution to the organization. The greater the contribution (such as performance), the greater the reward.

Concept note-4: -The custodial model is based around the concept of providing economic security for employees – through wages and other benefits – that will create employee loyalty and motivation. The custodial model mainly focuses on the financial reward aspects of being employed by the organisation.
