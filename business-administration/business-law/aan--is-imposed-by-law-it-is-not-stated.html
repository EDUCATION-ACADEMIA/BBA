A/an ____ is imposed by law; it is not stated.

(A) limited warranty

(B) full warranty

(C) express warranty

(D) ** implied warranty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. Condition, as to free from encumbrances, is not an implied condition in a contract of sale. Conditions and warranties may be express or implied. Express conditions and warranties are which, are expressly provided in the contract.

Concept note-2: -An implied warranty is a guarantee that is not written down or explicitly spoken. Article 2 of the Uniform Commercial Code ("UCC") governs the sale of goods. An implied warranty is automatically presumed regarding the sale of goods or real property, which prevents a risk from transferring to the buyer.

Concept note-3: -Under the implied category are three major subtypes: the implied warranty of merchantability (only given by merchants), the implied warranty of fitness for a particular purpose, and the implied warranty of title.

Concept note-4: -Implied warranties are unspoken, unwritten promises, created by state law, that go from you, as a seller or merchant, to your customers. Implied warranties are based upon the common law principle of “fair value for money spent, ” There are two types of implied warranties that occur in consumer product transactions.
