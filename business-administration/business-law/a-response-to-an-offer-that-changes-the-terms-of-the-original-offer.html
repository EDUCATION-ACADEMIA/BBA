A response to an offer that changes the terms of the original offer

(A) contract

(B) ** counteroffer

(C) implied offer

(D) unilateral contract

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A counteroffer functions as both a rejection of an offer to enter into a contract, as well as a new offer that materially changes the terms of the original offer. Because a counteroffer serves as a rejection, it completely voids the original offer. Thus, the original offer can no longer be accepted.

Concept note-2: -Your response should be brief and relevant as you explain whether or not you’re able to accept their offer. Elements to include in your counter offer response are: A professional greeting. A message stating whether you accept the candidate’s offer. Your enthusiasm for them to join the team.

Concept note-3: -A conditional or qualified acceptance is an acceptance that adds to, or changes, the terms of the original offer. This is essentially a counteroffer. A conditional or qualified acceptance generally terminates the offeree’s power of acceptance.

Concept note-4: -Thank you for your interest in joining our company. We appreciate the time and effort you have put into the application process. After careful consideration, we have decided to accept your counter offer. We are happy to match your salary request of $ [X] per year.

Concept note-5: -Dear [Hiring Manager], Thank you for extending an offer at [company name] for the position of [position]. This is a very exciting opportunity, and I’m confident that I’ll be able to make a positive contribution to the team. I’d like to ask if there’s room to negotiate the base salary for the role.
