BUSINESS LAW

[SOURCES]
Your monthly payment in order to keep health insurance. Sometimes you pay a part of it and your employer pays a part of it.

(A) ** Premium

(B) Co-insurance

(C) Medicaid

(D) Coverage

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Indemnity insurance pays a portion of the bill-usually 80 percent, after the deductible has been met, although this may vary. You pay the remainder, usually 20 percent of the total bill. This is called coinsurance.

Concept note-2: -The amount you pay for your health insurance every month. In addition to your premium, you usually have to pay other costs for your health care, including a deductible, copayments, and coinsurance.

Concept note-3: -At present, there are four options for paying health insurance premium-monthly, quarterly, half-yearly and annually. Policyholders must go through the fine print when paying the health insurance premium in instalments, especially the monthly mode and the quarterly mode.

Concept note-4: -deductible. The amount you pay for covered health care services before your insurance plan starts to pay. With a $2, 000 deductible, for example, you pay the first $2, 000 of covered services yourself.

Concept note-5: -The amount of money charged by the insurer to the policyholder for the coverage set forth in the insurance policy is called the premium.
