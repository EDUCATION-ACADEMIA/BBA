Which agency controls the price of natural gas and electricity for interstate business transactions?

(A) Environmental Protection Agency

(B) ** Federal Energy Regulatory Commission

(C) Nuclear Regulatory Commission

(D) Department of Energy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) is an independent agency within the Department of Energy which regulates the interstate transmission of electricity, natural gas, and oil.

Concept note-2: -The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission is charged by Congress with evaluating whether interstate natural gas pipeline projects proposed by private companies should be approved.

Concept note-3: -The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission regulates interstate transmission of electricity, natural gas, and oil, and also regulates hydropower projects and natural gas terminals.

Concept note-4: -The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC), an independent agency within DOE, regulates the interstate transmission of natural gas, crude oil, refined petroleum products, and electricity, as well as the interstate sale of electricity at wholesale.

Concept note-5: -Our current commissioners are Chairman Richard Glick, Commissioner James Danly, Commissioner Allison Clements, Commissioner Mark C. Christie, and Commissioner Willie L. Phillips.
