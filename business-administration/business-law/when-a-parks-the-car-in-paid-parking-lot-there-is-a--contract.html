When A parks the car in paid parking lot, there is a ____ contract

(A) Bilateral

(B) Executory

(C) ** Implied

(D) Express

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pay on entry is a system configuration where the customer pays for parking as he enters the facility. It is often used for event parking.

Concept note-2: -Valet parking is actually the opposite of finding a parking space on your own: It means that someone else parks your car for you. This person is called a valet. The valet parking service is often offered at restaurants, hotels, stores and other businesses.

Concept note-3: -Implied acceptance is when you don’t directly say “yes” to an offer verbally or in writing, but you do something which implies you will. Implied acceptance can create an implied contract. If your actions clearly indicate that you have accepted their offer, but you haven’t vocalized this acceptance, it still counts.

Concept note-4: -Don’t use the word ‘parking’ to refer to a place where cars are parked. Instead, say car park in British English and parking lot in American English. We parked in the car park next to the theatre. The high school parking lot was filled with cars.
