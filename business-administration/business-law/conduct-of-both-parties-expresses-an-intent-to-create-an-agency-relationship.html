EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An implied agency is a formal agreement between the client or principal and the listing agent. It does not include any documentation or writing. An implied agency in real estate is established when the principal hires the agent to help him with the transactions and other relevant tasks involved in purchasing a home.

Concept note-2: -An agency relationship requires the consent of both parties, and their intention to create the relationship “must find expression in either words or conduct.” Many agency agreements authorize the A to exercise some degree of discretion in carrying out the A’s responsibilities.

Concept note-3: -A buyer representation agreement works the same way as a listing contract, but it establishes the express agency relationship between the broker as the agent and the buyer as the principal.

Concept note-4: -Express authority is the authority which the principal has expressly given to the agent whether orally or in writing. Implied authority (sometimes described as usual authority) is the authority of an agent to do acts which are reasonably incidental to and necessary for the effective performance of his duties.
