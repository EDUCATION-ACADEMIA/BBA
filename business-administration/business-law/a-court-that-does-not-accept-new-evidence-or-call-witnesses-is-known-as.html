A court that does not accept new evidence or call witnesses is known as?

(A) Probate court

(B) Small claims court

(C) Trial court

(D) ** Appellate court

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Supreme Court has original, appellate and advisory jurisdiction.

Concept note-2: -It is true that the general principle is that the appellate court should not travel outside the record of the lower court and cannot take any evidence in appeal. However, as an exception, Order 41 Rule 27 CPC enables the appellate court to take additional evidence in exceptional circumstances.

Concept note-3: -The appellate courtâ€™s task is to determine whether or not the law was applied correctly in the trial court. Appeals courts consist of three judges and do not use a jury.

Concept note-4: -Appellate jurisdiction refers to the authority of a court to rehear/review a case decided by a lower court. In India, appellate jurisdiction is vested in both the Supreme Court and High Courts. They may either overrule or uphold the judgments of lower courts.
