Craig returned a recently purchased stereo to ElectronicWarehouse for a full refund. Last week Craig’s credit card bill still showed a charge for the purchase. What law could help Craig correct this error?

(A) Disputed Charges Reform Act

(B) ** Fair Credit Billing Act

(C) Fair Credit Reporting Act

(D) Federal Trade Commission Act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Consumer credit refers to the use of debit cards for personal needs. Consumer credit dates back to colonial times when it was extensively used by farmers. Consumer credit allows businesses to be more efficient or more productive. Economists recognize consumer credit as a major force in the American economy.

Concept note-2: -The amendment prohibits creditors from taking actions that adversely affect the consumer’s credit standing until an investigation is completed, and affords other protection during disputes.

Concept note-3: -The Act requires creditors to give consumers 60 days to challenge certain disputed charges over $50 such as wrong amounts, inaccurate statements, undelivered or unacceptable goods, and transactions by unauthorized users.

Concept note-4: -The FCBA covers billing errors on “open-end” or revolving accounts. These include credit cards, charge cards and home equity lines of credit. However, the law doesn’t cover installment loans-like auto loans-that give you a set period of time to pay off your debt.
