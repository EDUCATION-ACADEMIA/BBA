The competition and consumer act 2010 restricts certain behaviours, which one isn’t on there?

(A) Exclusionary provisions

(B) Price fixing

(C) ** Changing production trends

(D) Exclusive Dealing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (CCA) is a national law that governs how businesses must deal with suppliers, competitors and customers, and covers aspects of business such as advertising and price setting. It applies to all businesses – both streetfront and online operations.

Concept note-2: -The Competition and Consumer Act 2010 Broadly, it covers: product safety and labelling. unfair market practices. price monitoring.

Concept note-3: -An anti-competitive practice is an action conducted by one or more businesses to make it difficult or impossible for other companies to enter or succeed in their market.

Concept note-4: -Anticompetitive practices include activities like price fixing, group boycotts, and exclusionary exclusive dealing contracts or trade association rules, and are generally grouped into two types: agreements between competitors, also referred to as horizontal conduct.
