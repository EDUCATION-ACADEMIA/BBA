When a consumer complaint is not resolved, the last resort for a consumer to contact with the complaint is:

(A) an appropriate regulatory agency.

(B) ** an attorney to file a lawsuit.

(C) a dispute resolution organization.

(D) small claims court.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the contrary, if there is deficiency in service like non appearance, abstaining from court, non filing of replies, not arguing the matter or any such act which is detrimental to the interest of the client, the advocate/CA can be held be held liable for compensation under the Consumer Protection Act.

Concept note-2: -The Act provides for a three tier Consumer Disputes Redressal Agencies. These are: District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission in the District, State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission at the state level and the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission at the national level.

Concept note-3: -Is there an appeal against decisions of CCPA? A person aggrieved by any order passed by the Central Authority may file an appeal to the National Commission within a period of thirty days from the date of receipt of such order.

Concept note-4: -The complaints can be of any types ranging from false bank charges, defective products, online shopping, defective services, fraudulent services etc.
