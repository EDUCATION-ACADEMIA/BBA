A failure to provide complete performance of a contract is a(n) ____ ? ____

(A) performance

(B) ** breach of contract

(C) discharge

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A breach of contract is a failure, without legal excuse, to perform any promise that forms all or part of the contract. This includes failure to perform in a manner that meets the standards of the industry or the requirements of anyexpress warranty or implied warranty, including the implied warranty of merchantability.

Concept note-2: -If the party does not fulfill his contractual promise, or has given information to the other party that he will not perform his duty as mentioned in the contract or if by his action and conduct he seems to be unable to perform the contract, he is said to breach the contract.

Concept note-3: -A breach of contract occurs whenever a party who entered a contract fails to perform their promised obligations. Due to the frequency of breaches of contract, a robust body of law has grown to resolve the ensuing disputes.

Concept note-4: -Generally speaking, there are four types of contract breaches: anticipatory, actual, minor and material.
