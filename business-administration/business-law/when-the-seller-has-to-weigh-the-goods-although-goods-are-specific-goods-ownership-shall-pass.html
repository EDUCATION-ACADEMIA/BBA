When the seller has to weigh the goods, although goods are specific goods, ownership shall pass:

(A) When weighed

(B) ** When agree to be weighed

(C) When the weighed and buyer as the notice

(D) When the goods are delivered to the buyer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: –Where there is an unconditional contract for the sale of specific goods in a deliverable state, the property in the goods passes to the buyer when the contract is made, and it is immaterial whether the time of payment of the price or the time of delivery of the goods, or both, is postponed. 21.

Concept note-2: -In a contract for the sale of specific goods, which is unconditional in nature, the goods are transferred from the seller to the buyer at the time of formation of the contract. However, the only precondition required for the transfer of property is the fact that the goods must be existing in a deliverable state.

Concept note-3: -Rule: The ownership/property in goods is said to be transferred from the seller to the buyer once the goods are in deliverable state and the notice for the same is given by seller to the buyer. Till the time it’s in non-deliverable state, then the ownership lies with the seller only.
