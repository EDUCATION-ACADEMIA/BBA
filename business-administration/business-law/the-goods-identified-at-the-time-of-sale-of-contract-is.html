The goods identified at the time of sale of contract is

(A) ** Specific Goods

(B) Future Goods

(C) Certain Goods

(D) Uncertain Goods

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1) Specific Goods: The goods which are specifically identified at the time of formation of the contract for sale of goods are called specific goods. In case of specific goods the contract of sale is in respect of only those goods and the same goods are to be delivered by the seller to the buyer and not the similar one.

Concept note-2: -Goods which are identified at the time of contract are known as Specific goods.

Concept note-4: -b) Specific goods are those that are identified at the time the contract of sale is made.

Concept note-5: -Specific Goods: These are those goods which are separately identified and agreed upon at the time of sale. Example: You decided to purchase a good day biscuit and you went to the shop, paid for it, and took the delivery.
