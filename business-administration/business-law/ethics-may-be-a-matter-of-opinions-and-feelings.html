Ethics may be a matter of opinions and feelings.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ethics may be a matter of opinions and feelings. The golden rule holds that you should “Do unto others before they do unto you.” The principle of good sportsmanship is an outgrowth of the greatest good principle. Laws make people do what is right.

Concept note-2: -At the heart of ethics is a concern about something or someone other than ourselves and our own desires and self-interest. Ethics is concerned with other people’s interests, with the interests of society, with God’s interests, with “ultimate goods", and so on.

Concept note-3: -Ethics examines the rational justification for our moral judgments; it studies what is morally right or wrong, just or unjust. In a broader sense, ethics reflects on human beings and their interaction with nature and with other humans, on freedom, on responsibility and on justice.

Concept note-4: -Both Plato and Aristotle offer the following helpful advice: those who have knowledge are not merely asserting something to be true; they have adequate reasons for doing so. Thus, mere opinion, since it is not supported with good reasons, is not only unstable, but it is also unteachable.
