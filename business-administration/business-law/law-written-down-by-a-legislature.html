BUSINESS LAW

[SOURCES]
Law written down by a legislature.

(A) ** statutory

(B) constitutional

(C) administrative

(D) common

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Statutory laws are laws set up by the legislature and written down, or codified, into legal books.

Concept note-2: -Statute law or Statutory law is a law that is created by the legislation, for e.g. the State Legislature. A statute is a formal act of the legislature in written form.

Concept note-3: -noun. the written law established by enactments expressing the will of the legislature, as distinguished from the unwritten law or common law. Also called: statute law.

Concept note-4: -Different statutory laws In this regard, the names of such laws include Civil Procedure Code 1908, Government of India Act (1919), Indian Penal code (1860), Indian Contract Act (1872).
