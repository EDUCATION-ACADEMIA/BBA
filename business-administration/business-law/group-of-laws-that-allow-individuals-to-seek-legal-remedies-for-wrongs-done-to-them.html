BUSINESS LAW

[SOURCES]
Group of laws that allow individuals to seek legal remedies for wrongs done to them.

(A) Criminal Law

(B) Business Law

(C) Case Law

(D) ** Civil Law

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Procedural law prescribes the means of enforcing rights or providing redress of wrongs and comprises rules about jurisdiction, pleading and practice, evidence, appeal, execution of judgments, representation of counsel, costs, and other matters.

Concept note-2: -The remedies under civil law have their strong nexus with the personal relief of what exactly the aggrieved party is looking for. The Declaration of title, temporary or permanent injunction, Specific performance, Monetary Damages all work on a different footing as compared to imprisonment or death sentence.

Concept note-3: -Put simply, the difference between the criminal remedies or sanctions and civil remedies is that “criminal law is meant to punish, while the civil law is meant to compensate.” International human rights law requires an effective remedy where an individual’s rights or freedoms have been violated.

Concept note-4: -Civil law is the part of a country’s set of laws which is concerned with the private affairs of citizens, for example marriage and property ownership, rather than with crime.

Concept note-5: -Civil law deals with the disputes between individuals, organizations etc., whereas Criminal Law is the body of law that deals with crime and the legal punishment for criminal offenses.
