Enforcable rules of conduct in a society, typicslly reflect the culture and circumstances that create them are called ____

(A) Code

(B) Equity

(C) ** Laws

(D) Common Law

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The laws, or enforceable rules of conduct in a society, do not reflect the culture and circumstances that create them. laws in this country are created at only the federal and state levels of government. The supreme court of the U.S is the final interpreter of the federal constitution.

Concept note-2: -2. A working definition of procedural law is the rules and principles that govern the behavior of courts and lawyer in dealing with disputes.

Concept note-3: -Ethics are defined as rules of conduct. When you exhibit acceptable practices in society and under governing bodies of instruction, you are delivering patterns of what is considered acceptable, good behaviour. Your moral reasoning can help in the decision-making process and selecting choices that work in your favour.

Concept note-4: -The law is a system of rules that a society or government develops in order to deal with crime, business agreements, and social relationships.

Concept note-5: -What Is Customary Law? Customary law is a set of customs, practices and beliefs that are accepted as obligatory rules of conduct by indigenous peoples and local com-munities. Customary law forms an intrinsic part of their social and economic systems and way of life.
