Contracts entered into as a result of misrepresentation or fraud are voidable by

(A) ** the injured party

(B) the UCC

(C) both a and b

(D) nonce of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A misrepresentation is a false statement of a material fact made by one party which affects the other partyâ€™s decision in agreeing to a contract. If the misrepresentation is discovered, the contract can be declared void. Depending on the situation, the adversely impacted party may seek damages.

Concept note-2: -A misrepresentation in contract law is a statement of fact that is not true and that persuades someone to enter into a contract. The contract does not have to be in writing, it can be a verbal agreement. The untrue statement can be made by an agent or employee on the part of their principal or employer.

Concept note-3: -Under what circumstances will an innocent misrepresentation by a party cause a contract to be voidable?-Courts generally hold that if it was reasonable for the misled party to have relied on the innocent misrepresentation, the contract is voidable.

Concept note-4: -DOLO CAUSANTE; Fraud used by one of the parties to a contract in SECURING THE CONSENT of the other party. It VITIATES CONSENT and is ground for annulment of the contract by the innocent party. It renders the contracts VOIDABLE.
