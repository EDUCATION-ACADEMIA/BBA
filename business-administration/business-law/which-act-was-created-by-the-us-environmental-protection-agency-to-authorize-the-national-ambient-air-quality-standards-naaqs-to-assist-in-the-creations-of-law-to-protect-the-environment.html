Which act was created by the US Environmental Protection Agency to authorize the National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS) to assist in the creations of law to protect the environment?

(A) ** Clean Air Act

(B) National Environmental Policy Act

(C) Energy Independence and Security Act

(D) Energy Policy Act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Authorized by the CAA of 1970, EPA officials established the National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS), which was the traditional centrepiece of CAA regulations. The NAAQS addressed six pollutants that threatened public health: sulfur dioxide, nitrogen dioxide, particulate matter, carbon monoxide, ozone, and lead.

Concept note-2: -The U.S. National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS, pronounced /ˈnæks/ naks) are limits on atmospheric concentration of six pollutants that cause smog, acid rain, and other health hazards. Concept note-3: -The Clean Air Act (CAA) (42 U.S.C. 7401 et seq.) is a comprehensive Federal law that regulates all sources of air emissions. The 1970 CAA authorized the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to establish National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS) to protect public health and the environment.

Concept note-4: -It aims to formulate a holistic national program of air pollution management and encourage cooperation and self-regulation among citizens and industries through market-based instruments.
