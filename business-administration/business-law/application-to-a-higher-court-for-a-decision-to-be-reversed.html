application to a higher court for a decision to be reversed

(A) ** appeal

(B) deposition

(C) discovery

(D) settlement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A High Court is at liberty to affirm, reverse or modify any judgment, decree or final order appealed from as the justice of the case may require.

Concept note-2: -4. the appellate court has jurisdiction to reverse or affirm the findings of the trial court. The first appeal is a valuable right of the parties and unless restricted by law, the whole case is therein open for rehearing both on questions of fact and law.

Concept note-3: -No revision is possible where the order has been made subject of an appeal to the CIT(A). Revision of the order u/s. 264 cannot be made where an appeal lies to CIT(A) but not made and the assessee has not waived his right of such appeal.

Concept note-4: -Reverse: This means that the Appellate Court decides that the decision of the lower court was wrong. When this happens, the Appellate Court vacates (cancels) the decision of the lower court.
