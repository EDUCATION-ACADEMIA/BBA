Will saw an advertisement where he could quit commuting to his job and make $2, 500 a month more. When he called the toll-freenumber, the job required a $5, 000 up-front investment for “equipment and customer lists.” Will was deceived by the ad and called a fraud hotline. What type of fraud did Will report?

(A) Lottery scam

(B) Price-fixing

(C) Spamming

(D) ** Work-at-home scheme

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Acceptance of an offer to form a unilateral contract need not be communicated to the offeror to be effective. A properly mailed acceptance takes effect when mailed, even if it never is received by the offeror. At an auction sale, a statement made by the auctioneer to draw forth bids constitutes an offer.

Concept note-2: -If no time for acceptance is specified in an offer, the offer terminates at the end of a reasonable time. True. In a bilateral contract, communication of acceptance is necessary.

Concept note-3: -An output contract is too vague to be a legally-enforceable agreement. An offer is effective only if it is communicated by the offeror in person. An offer gives the offeror the power to bind the offeree by contract. A revocation of an offer is ordinarily effective only when it is communicated to the offeree.

Concept note-4: -An offer can be accepted by the offeree or any third party. Acceptance may consist of words or conduct. Acceptance of an e-contract must show that an offeree voluntarily assented to the offer’s terms. A click-on agreement can indicate the acceptance of an online offer.
