The court that gets to decide what is constitutional ____

(A) ** Supreme Court

(B) Court of Appeals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The proceedings of the Supreme Court are conducted in English only. Supreme Court Rules, 1966 and Supreme Court Rules 2013 are framed under Article 145 of the Constitution to regulate the practice and procedure of the Supreme Court. SUPREME COURT REGISTRY.

Concept note-2: -Constitutional Courts (CCs) are specialized courts with jurisdiction over constitutional matters. In contrast to the American system of judicial review, CCs based on Kelsen’s Austrian model are the result of constitutional design.

Concept note-3: -Supreme Court of India came into existence on 26th January, 1950 and is located on Tilak Marg, New Delhi. The Supreme Court of India functioned from the Parliament House till it moved to the present building. It has a 27.6 metre high dome and a spacious colonnaded verandah.

Concept note-4: -SC is the highest judicial court in India. It upholds the rule of law and also guarantees and protects citizens’ rights and liberties as given in the Constitution. Therefore, the Supreme Court is also known as the Guardian of the Constitution.

Concept note-5: -It is the highest constitutional court and has the final decision in all legal matters. It also has the power of judicial review. The Chief Justice of India is the Head of the Supreme Court, which consists of a maximum of 34 judges, and has extensive powers in the form of original, appellate and advisory jurisdictions.
