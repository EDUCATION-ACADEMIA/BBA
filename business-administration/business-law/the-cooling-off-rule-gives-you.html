The cooling-off rule gives you

(A) the ability to cancel a contract for most purchases made in the seller’s regular place of business.

(B) protection from abusive telemarketers who try and sell you product over the phone.

(C) ** three business days to cancel contracts for most purchases made away from the seller’s regular place of business.

(D) one month to cancel contracts for most purchases made away from the seller’s regular place of business

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cooling-off Rule is a rule that allows you to cancel a contract within a few days (usually three days) after signing it. As explained by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), the federal cooling-off rules gives the consumer three days to cancel certain sales for a full refund.

Concept note-2: -The three-day cancellation rule is a federal consumer protection law within the Truth in Lending Act (TILA). It gives borrowers three business days, including Saturdays, to rethink their decision and back out of a signed agreement without paying penalties.

Concept note-3: -The Cooling-Off Rule gives you three days to cancel certain sales made at your home, workplace, or dormitory, or at a seller’s temporary location, like a hotel or motel room, convention center, fairground, or restaurant. The Rule also applies when you invite a salesperson to make a presentation in your home.

Concept note-4: -Check for the cooling-off period 14 days is the minimum cooling-off period that a seller must give you. Make sure you check the terms and conditions in case they’ve given you more time to change your mind-many choose to do so.

Concept note-5: -Businesses do not get cooling off periods when signing up to contracts at home, on a business premises or at a distance. Unless the contract you have with the company you are purchasing the product from states you have a cooling off period, you will not have one.
