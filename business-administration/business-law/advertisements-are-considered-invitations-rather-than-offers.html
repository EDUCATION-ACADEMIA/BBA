Advertisements are considered invitations rather than offers.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They are considered an invitation to begin negotiations. The reason for this is because if a contract was formed, no one could ever advertise without fear of being sued.

Concept note-2: -Generally, advertisements are not offers but invitations to treat, so the person advertising is not compelled to sell.

Concept note-3: -Advertisements Are Not Offers Generally, courts do not consider advertisements offers. Instead, they are an invitation to begin negotiations.

Concept note-4: -Courts and scholars uniformly recite the contract law rule familiar to all first-year students: An advertisement is not an offer.

Concept note-5: -An advertisement can either be an offer or an invitation to treat. However, it will be based on intention of parties.
