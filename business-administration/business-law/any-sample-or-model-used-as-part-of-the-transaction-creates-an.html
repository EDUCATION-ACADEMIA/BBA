Any sample or model used as part of the transaction creates a(n)

(A) ** express warranty

(B) limited warranty

(C) warranty of title

(D) full warranty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(c) Any sample or model which is made part of the basis of the bargain creates an express warranty that the whole of the goods shall conform to the sample or model.

Concept note-2: -An “express” warranty by a seller is created by: Any statement of fact or promise relating to the goods sold which becomes part of the basis of the bargain between the parties, creating a warranty that the goods will conform to the statement or promise.

Concept note-3: -An express warranty is created when a seller indicates that the goods will conform to: (1) All affirmations of fact or promises made about the goods. Examples: “ This car will go 100 miles per hour". “ This paint will not fade for at least 5 years".
