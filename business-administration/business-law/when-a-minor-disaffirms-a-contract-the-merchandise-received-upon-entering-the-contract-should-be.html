When a minor disaffirms a contract, the merchandise received upon entering the contract should be

(A) undamaged

(B) ** returned

(C) paid for

(D) ratified

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When a minor disaffirms a contract, all property that he or she has transferred as consideration can be recovered–even if it was subsequently transferred to a third party. Disaffirmance must be timely. The contract must be disaffirmed in its entirety.

Concept note-2: -People who can prove they lacked the capacity to enter a legally binding contract-intoxication, mental incompetence, etc.-can disaffirm a contract and therefore avoid any and all legal obligations set forth in the contract. In many cases, this can apply to minors.

Concept note-3: -A minor can disaffirm or avoid a contract by showing an intent not to be bound by it. Most contracts can be disaffirmed by a minor at anytime before reaching the age of majority or for a reasonable time after reaching majority.

Concept note-4: -Definition of ‘disaffirm’ 1. to deny or contradict (a former statement) Law. a. to refuse to abide by (a contract, agreement, etc.

Concept note-5: -Any person who is not of the age of majority is a minor. In India, 18 years is the age of majority. Below the age of 18 years does not have the capacity to enter into a contract. A contract or agreement with a minor is null from the beginning, and no one can sue them.
