EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The utilitarianism principle basically holds that an action is morally right if it produces the greatest good for the greatest number of people.

Concept note-2: -Utilitarianism is an ethical theory that determines right from wrong by focusing on outcomes. It is a form of consequentialism. Utilitarianism holds that the most ethical choice is the one that will produce the greatest good for the greatest number.

Concept note-3: -If you answered in the affirmative to these questions, then you might be a utilitarian, the moral system founded by English philosopher Jeremy Bentham (1748–1832) and encapsulated in the principle of “the greatest good for the greatest number.”

Concept note-4: -"The idea behind the greatest good for the greatest number for the longest time is that you do things for the greater public good and benefit and that that’s what matters.

Concept note-5: -Utilitarianism is a theory of morality that advocates actions that foster happiness and oppose actions that cause unhappiness. Utilitarianism promotes “the greatest amount of good for the greatest number of people."
