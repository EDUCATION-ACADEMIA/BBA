What is charter capital?

(A) The minimum amount of capital required by law for enterprises to do business

(B) Amount of capital contributed by members of the enterprise

(C) ** Amount of capital contributed or committed to contribute by members, shareholders or committed to contribute within a certain period of time and recorded in the company’s charter

(D) Sentence (B) and (C) are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Charter Capital means the total value of assets that are contributed or promised to be contributed by members/partners when establishing a limited liability company or partnership; or the total face value of shares that are sold or registered when establishing a joint-stock company.

Concept note-2: -Charter capital is defined as the capital contributed or undertaken to be contributed by the member/owner within a statutory period (usually 90 days from the issuance date of enterprise registration certificate).

Concept note-3: -In business law, contribution may refer to a capital contribution, which is money or assets given to a business or partnership by one of the owners or partners. The capital contribution increases the owner or partner’s equity interest in the entity.

Concept note-4: -A capital contribution is a contribution to the equity capital of a company, but is not made in exchange for shares issued to the contributor and it does not constitute a separate asset in its own right.

Concept note-5: -Cash or assets given to an entity in exchange for an equity interest or as part of an ongoing obligation, or capital commitment, to fund the entity.
