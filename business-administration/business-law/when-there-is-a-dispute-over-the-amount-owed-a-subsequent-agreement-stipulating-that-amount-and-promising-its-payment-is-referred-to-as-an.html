When there is a dispute over the amount owed a subsequent agreement stipulating that amount and promising its payment is referred to as a(n)

(A) ** accord

(B) satisfaction

(C) output contract

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Generally speaking, there are four types of contract breaches: anticipatory, actual, minor and material.

Concept note-2: -Accord and satisfaction refers to the agreement (accord) between two contracting parties to accept alternate performance to discharge a pre-existing duty between them and the subsequent performance (satisfaction) of that agreement. The new performance is called the accord.

Concept note-3: -A contractual dispute is usually when a party in a contract has a disagreement concerning its terms or definitions, whether it’s you or your client. If handled poorly, contractual disputes can be costly and time-consuming, end up in court and damage your business relationships and reputation.

Concept note-4: -An example of promissory estoppel might be applied in a case where an employer makes an oral promise to an employee to pay the employee a specified monthly or annual amount of money throughout the full duration of the employee’s retirement.
