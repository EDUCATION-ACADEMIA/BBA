When a debtor and a creditor disagree on the amount owed on a debt and agree to compromise, the debt is

(A) ** not paid legally.

(B) not binding legally.

(C) settled legally.

(D) partially settled.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Default by the Debtors Default in payment of debt requires legal action by the Creditor.

Concept note-2: -Any of the above can legally lend and borrow funds. In business, a creditor-debtor relationship is defined by a debt agreement (or contract) which explicitly states the legal obligations, responsibilities and binding rights of both parties.

Concept note-3: -Debt is an obligation that requires one party, the debtor, to pay money or other agreed-upon value to another party, the creditor.

Concept note-4: -1096 entitles the creditor to compel the debtor to make delivery or, if the thing is indeterminate or generic, to demand specific performance at the debtor’s expense. Art. 1098 provides that if the obligor does not do something that is was obliged to do then, it shall be enforced at its own expense.
