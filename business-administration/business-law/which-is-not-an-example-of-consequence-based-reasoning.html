Which is NOT an example of consequence-based reasoning?

(A) Driving the speed limit so that you don’t get a ticket.

(B) Slowing down when you drive past your aunt’s house so she won’t call your mom on you.

(C) Driving slow in really bad weather so that you don’t have an accident.

(D) ** Following rules of the road and signs because it is expected of us all.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Consequentialism is an ethical theory that judges whether or not something is right by what its consequences are. For instance, most people would agree that lying is wrong. But if telling a lie would help save a person’s life, consequentialism says it’s the right thing to do.

Concept note-2: -The most famous version of non-consequentialism is deontology, which holds that one has an absolute duty to obey certain rules. “Never kill an innocent person” or “never lie” are examples of such rules. Christianity is one form of deontology and the Ten Commandments represent one set of rules.

Concept note-3: -Deontology is a set of moral theories which place themselves opposite consequentialism. While consequentialism determines right actions from good ends, deontology asserts that the end and the means by which it is arrived upon are intrinsically linked. A good end will come about as a result of good or right means.
