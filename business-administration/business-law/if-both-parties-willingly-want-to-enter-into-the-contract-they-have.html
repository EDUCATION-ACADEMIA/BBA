If both parties willingly want to enter into the contract, they have

(A) misrepresentation

(B) ** genuine assent

(C) legality

(D) capacity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -➢ genuine agreement (genuine assent or mutual assent)→exists when consent is not clouded by fraud, misrepresentation, duress, undue influence, or mistake. ➢ If genuine agreement is missing, the victim may cancel or disaffirm the contract because the contract is voidable.

Concept note-2: -Example: agreeing to pay someone to commit a crime cannot be a contract Consideration The agreement must involve both sides receiving something of legal value as a result of the transaction.

Concept note-3: -Without genuine assent a contract typically is voidable. This means that, if the injured party desires, that party can cancel or avoid the contractual obligation. The party then has the legal right to get back what has already been put into the contract, which is called rescission.

Concept note-4: -A bilateral contract is an agreement between two parties in which each side agrees to fulfill their side of the bargain. Typically, bilateral contracts involve an equal obligation or consideration from the offeror and the offeree, although this need not always be the case.
