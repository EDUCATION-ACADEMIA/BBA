If a law conflicts with provisions of the Constitution, a court can declare that law

(A) criminal

(B) unusual

(C) ** unconstitutional

(D) unfair

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Article 13 of the Indian Constitution provides for invalidity of laws that are inconsistent with Fundamental Rights. The supreme court has the power to review legislative and administrative actions and declare them ultra vires(outside the scope of law) if they are inconsistent with the constitution.

Concept note-2: -If a law made by Parliament or the state legislatures violates any provision of the Constitution, the Supreme Court has the power to declare such a law invalid or ultra vires.

Concept note-3: -Law Made By A Legislature Is Valid Till It Is Declared Unconstitutional By A Court Of Law: Supreme Court.

Concept note-4: -The Judiciary has the power to set aside any law passed by the parliament if it intervenes in the Constitution of India. Any law passed by the legislature that contravenes the Constitution can be made null and void by the Judiciary.

Concept note-5: -Thus, we can say that the Supreme court can declare any law of legislature as invalid and can determine the constitutional validity of any law or action of the executive in the country when it is challenged, this is called as Judicial review.
