Gathering of jury members to discuss the case and reach a verdict, is called

(A) jury gathering

(B) collaboration

(C) ** deliberation

(D) grand jury

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After being charged, the jury goes into deliberation, the process of deciding whether a defendant is guilty or not guilty. During this process, no one associated with the trial can contact the jury without the judges and lawyers.

Concept note-2: -After hearing the jury instructions, the jury moves to the jury room to consider the case and reach its verdict. All the jury’s discussion of testimony and evidence takes place only when all the jurors are present, in the jury room-nowhere else.

Concept note-3: -Thus, we define a “deliberative process” as a process allowing a group of actors to receive and exchange information, to critically examine an issue, and to come to an agreement which will inform decision making (Fearon, 1998).

Concept note-4: -Verdict: The official decision or finding of the jury which is reported to the court. Voir dire: Means “to speak the truth” in French.

Concept note-5: -If used as a verb, it refers to the action of analyzing or carefully considering the evidence, facts, the law, or other matters. For example, a jury that deliberates on a verdict in a criminal case.
