How often are the Environmental Impact Statements updated?

(A) every day

(B) every month

(C) ** every week

(D) every year

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -i) To disclose significant environmental effects of proposed projects to decision-makers and the public. ii) To identify ways to avoid or reduce environmental damage. iii) To prevent adverse environmental impacts by requiring implementation of feasible alternatives or mitigation measures.

Concept note-2: -The time it takes to prepare an EIS ranges from 51 to 6, 708 days. On average, the time it takes for all federal entities to prepare an EIS is 3.4 years.

Concept note-3: -National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) of 1969 NEPA required federal agencies to evaluate their activities “significantly affecting the quality of the human environment” by issuing an Environmental Impact Statement (EIS).

Concept note-4: -The environmental impact statement (EIS) is a government document that outlines the impact of a proposed project on its surrounding environment. In the United States, these statements are mandated by federal law for certain projects.
