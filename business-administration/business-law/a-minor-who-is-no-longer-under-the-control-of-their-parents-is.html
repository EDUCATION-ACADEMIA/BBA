A minor who is no longer under the control of their parents is

(A) Shunned

(B) ** Emancipation

(C) Eschew

(D) All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Emancipation of minors is a legal mechanism by which a minor before attaining the age of majority is freed from control by their parents or guardians, and the parents or guardians are freed from responsibility for their child.

Concept note-2: -Emancipation is when a child (a minor) legally gets some of the rights of adults before reaching the age of 18. For example, signing contracts, choosing where to live, and enrolling in school.

Concept note-3: -Eligibility Criteria for Legal Separation from Parents A child can apply for emancipation if, The parents are abusive and they can’t take care of the child. The situation in the parent’s home is emotionally, morally, and physically harming. The child has reached financial independence and wants adult rights.

Concept note-4: -Currently, there are no laws in India that allow for emancipation. There’s a very straightforward reason for it, it’s clubbed with the general reason for why child rights don’t exist in India. Not to be confused with child ‘protection’ by the way. Child protection rights are very much prevalent in India.
