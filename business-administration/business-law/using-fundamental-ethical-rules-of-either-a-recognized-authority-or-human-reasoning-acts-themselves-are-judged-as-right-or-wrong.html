Using fundamental ethical rules of either a recognized authority or human reasoning, acts themselves are judged as right or wrong.

(A) Case Law

(B) ** Rule Based Ethical Reasoning

(C) Statutory Law

(D) Greatest Good

(E) Ethics

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -2) Rule-Based Ethical Reasoning-In this style of reasoning, acts are either right or wrong. For example, telling the truth is always right, and lying is always wrong. In rule-based ethics, good consequences do not justify wrong or bad acts.

Concept note-2: -Ethical reasoning helps determine and differentiate between right thinking, decisions, and actions and those that are wrong, hurtful and/or harmful-to others and to ourselves. Ethics is based on and motivated by facts, values, emotions, beliefs, emotions, and feelings.

Concept note-3: -Ends-based thinking: This principle states that you should do the action that provided the greatest good for the majority of persons. Also, you must be able to forecast the outcomes and the consequences 2. Rule-based thinking: Act only on the guideline you want everyone else to follow.

Concept note-4: -Ethics is deciding what is right or wrong in a reasoned, impartial manner.
