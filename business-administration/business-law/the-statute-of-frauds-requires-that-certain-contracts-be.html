The Statute of Frauds requires that certain contracts be

(A) completed within a year

(B) ** be in writing

(C) benefiting only one party

(D) free of stipulations

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Statute of Frauds: A statute which requires certain types of contracts to be in writing in order to be enforceable.

Concept note-2: -The Statute of Frauds can be satisfied by any signed writing that (1) reasonably identifies the subject matter of the contract, (2) is sufficient to indicate that a contract exists, and (3) states with reasonable certainty the material terms of the contract.

Concept note-3: -Contracts that are required to be in writing by the statute of frauds include: contracts for mortgage or lease of land and buildings.

Concept note-4: -Which of the following is true regarding the Statute of Frauds’ writing requirement? The writing must indicate the parties’ identities. Whose signatures are needed in order to satisfy the Statute of Frauds? Only the signature of the party against whom enforcement is being sought.
