The works of an author or artist are protected by

(A) ** copyright

(B) patent

(C) trademark

(D) servicemark

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is copyright? Copyright (or author’s right) is a legal term used to describe the rights that creators have over their literary and artistic works. Works covered by copyright range from books, music, paintings, sculpture, and films, to computer programs, databases, advertisements, maps, and technical drawings.

Concept note-2: -Copyright protects the rights of authors, i.e., creators of intellectual property in the form of literary, musical, dramatic and artistic works and cinematograph films and sound recordings.

Concept note-3: -Copyright is a right which subsists in a number of different kinds of works such as literary, dramatic, musical or artistic works, sound recording and cinematograph films. 1 The Copyright Act 1957 governs the law related to the copyrights in India.

Concept note-4: -First, copyright protects original works of authorship, including literary works like books, essays, articles, blogs, and poetry.

Concept note-5: -Copyright, a form of intellectual property law, protects original works of authorship including literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works, such as poetry, novels, movies, songs, computer software, and architecture.
