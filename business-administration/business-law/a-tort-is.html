A tort is:

(A) A civil wrong action in which breaches ones personal rights

(B) the breach of ones personal right and property

(C) The taking of someone stuff

(D) ** the infringement of the rights of one individual by another

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is there to make people respect the rights of other people around them. Hence, a tort is a violation of a right in rem which means that the right is vested in some person and is available against the world at large.

Concept note-2: -Tort is an infringement of an individual’s private or civil rights and a suit must be filed by the injured party. Duty of Care is owed to claimant by the defendant. Standard of care is required in a given case and if is not met by the defendant, thus it stands broken.

Concept note-3: -An infringement is a violation, a breach, or an unauthorized act. Infringement occurs in various situations. A harm to one’s right is an infringement. A violation of a statute is also an infringement.

Concept note-4: -Tort is a private wrong that contravenes the legal right of an individual or a group. The person who engages in tort is called “tort-feasor” or “Wrongdoer”. The place of trial for tort is Civil Court. Tort litigation is compoundable which means that the complainant can withdraw the suit filed by him.

Concept note-5: -A tort can occur when, under the law, one person owes another a duty of care but fails to fulfill that duty. Every person owes a duty to all other persons to use reasonable care to avoid causing injury to them or their property.
