The consent given will be considered to be valid consent if there is

(A) Coercion

(B) Undue influence

(C) ** Free consent

(D) Misrepresentation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Now Free Consent has been defined in Section 14 of the Act. The section says that consent is considered free consent when it is not caused or affected by the following, Coercion. Undue Influence.

Concept note-2: -13. ‘Consent’ defined.-Two or more persons are said to consent when they agree upon the same thing in the same sense.-Two or more persons are said to consent when they agree upon the same thing in the same sense."

Concept note-3: -For an agreement to be constituted as a Valid contract consent is very important and pure consent is not accepted it must be free consent that is free from misrepresentation, fraud, Mistakes, and undue influence.

Concept note-4: -Free Consent means the absence of any kind of coercion, undue influence, fraud, misrepresentation or mistake.
