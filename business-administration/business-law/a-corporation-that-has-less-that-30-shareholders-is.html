A corporation that has less that 30 shareholders is

(A) LLC

(B) C Corporation

(C) ** Close

(D) S Corporation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They are limited to no more than about 30 shareholders, and there are often significant restrictions on the ability of existing owners and shareholders to transfer or sell stock in the company.

Concept note-2: -The special statutory close corporation statutes require that there be a limited number of shareholders (under 30 or, in some states, under 50), and that certain transfer restrictions appear on the stock certificates.

Concept note-3: -S corporations are corporations that elect to pass corporate income, losses, deductions, and credits through to their shareholders for federal tax purposes.

Concept note-4: -A Close Corporation is a Corporation which sets certain limitations on the sale, holding and transfer of its shares of stock. In Delaware, a Close Corporation is limited to thirty shareholders.

Concept note-5: -Ernst & Young, PricewaterhouseCoopers, SC Johnson, Hearst Corporation, and Chick-Fil-A, and Hobby Lobby are other well-known U.S. closed corporations. Some examples of a non-U.S. closed corporation are Sweden’s IKEA, Germany’s ALDI and Bosch, and Denmark’s LEGO.
