It refers to a bad Filipino culture and often practiced by some Filipino workers.

(A) Risk-taker

(B) Long-term thinking skills

(C) ** Mañana Habit

(D) Strategic planning

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is Mañana Habit? Mañana Habit is the regular tendency or practice of putting off until tomorrow what can be done today. It tends to occur subconsciously, like most of our procrastination problems. It is a way of thinking and doing acquired through previous repetition of task-avoidance experiences.

Concept note-2: -This habit shows our culture’s lack of sense in time which is most true especially to students. This is a social problem because most Filipinos are under an illusion that they are dealing with important urgent tasks but realize in the end that they are just wasting their time.

Concept note-3: -Mañana Habit is synonymous to procrastination in the Filipino Culture. It originates from the Spaniards, and it remains prominent today.

Concept note-4: -Filipinos are fond of saying “bukas na lang” or “sa susunod na lang” due to laziness. This arises from a slack mentality that a problem will eventually go away by itself. Filipinos tend to possess this mentality especially when they feel insecure. In a way, crab mentality also promotes an unhealthy competition.
