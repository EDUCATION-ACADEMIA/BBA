It is the degree to which a society values assertiveness and feelings of material success versus concern for relationships.

(A) Uncertainty Avoidance

(B) ** Masculinity-Femininity

(C) Time Orientation

(D) Power Distance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Masculinity-femininity is the degree a society values assertiveness and materialism versus feelings, relationships, and quality of life.

Concept note-2: -In a masculine culture, men are expected to be assertive, competitive, and focused on material success. Women are expected to be nurturing and focused on people and quality of life. In contrast, Hofstede says a feminine culture or feminine society is one where gender roles are more fluid.

Concept note-3: -Masculinity is seen to be the trait which emphasizes ambition, acquisition of wealth, and differentiated gender roles. Femininity is seen to be the trait which stress caring and nurturing behaviors, sexuality equality, environmental awareness, and more fluid gender roles.

Concept note-4: -Collectivism refers to the degree to which a society focuses on the relationship of the group as a whole.

Concept note-5: -According to Hofstede’s model, masculinity prizes “achievement, heroism, assertiveness, and material rewards for success.” Femininity, in contrast, prefers “cooperation, modesty, caring for the weak and quality of life.” Short-Term vs. Long-Term Orientation (LTO).
