Invention refers to the development of

(A) an existing idea/product/process

(B) an existing product/process/idea

(C) ** a totally new idea/product/process

(D) the alteration of out of date ideas/products/processes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Invention is the creation of new products or processes. These can be completely novel and untried or may be derived from previous products, but with sufficient new features or technical attributes, that a firm can patent the design or copyright ideas included in the development.

Concept note-2: -1. Invention is the generation of a new idea aimed at solving a specific technical problem. Innovation can be seen as a process of interaction and feedback during the various stages of product development. Not all inventions are commercialized, so it is clear that not all inventions result in innovation.

Concept note-3: -Product development typically refers to all stages involved in bringing a product from concept or idea through market release and beyond. In other words, product development incorporates a productâ€™s entire journey.

Concept note-4: -The invention process can vary from invention to invention, but the typical invention process involves the following steps: (1) documentation, (2) confidentiality, (3) patent search, (4) patent application and (5) selling or licensing.

Concept note-5: -An idea is generally theoretical in nature and needs to be proved either with a solution or a prototype, . An invention is the extension of the idea that is developed. For an idea to become an invention, it needs to follow the path of most resistance.
