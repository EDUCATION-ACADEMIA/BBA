The four factors contributing to the External (Operating) Business Environment include:

(A) Owners/Management, Employees, Organisational Culture, Organisational Structure

(B) Customers, Clients, Suppliers, Distributors

(C) Employees, Employers, Customers, Clients

(D) ** Customers, Competitors, Suppliers, Interest Groups

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The success of a company depends on its ability to adapt. Micro environmental factors include suppliers, customers, marketing intermediaries, financiers, and public perceptions.

Concept note-2: -A PEST analysis (or STEP analysis) invites you to list all the relevant external forces using four headings: Political, Economic, Sociological and Technological. These are useful headings; it doesnâ€™t matter that some items might be both political and economic (eg taxation and exchange rates).

Concept note-3: -External factors include political, economic, sociocultural, technological, environmental, and legal factors. These are factors that happen outside of the company but still, impact its ability to do business. Internal factors include culture changes, management changes, and employee morale.
