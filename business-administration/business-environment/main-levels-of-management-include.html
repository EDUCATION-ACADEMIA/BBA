Main levels of management include:

(A) Top Level or Executive

(B) Mid Level or Middle

(C) First Line or Supervisory

(D) ** All the Above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Management levels are the divisions between degrees of authority and responsibility in a company. The typical management levels are top-level management, mid-level management and first-line management. These levels determine the duties of various manager positions, including who they report to and who reports to them.

Concept note-2: -The four most common types of managers are top-level managers, middle managers, first-line managers, and team leaders. These roles vary not only in their day-to-day responsibilities, but also in their broader function in the organization and the types of employees they manage.

Concept note-3: -The three levels of management in most organizations are top-level management, mainly responsible for overseeing all operations, middle-level management, responsible for executing plans and policies, and low-level management, responsible for direct task execution and deliverables.
