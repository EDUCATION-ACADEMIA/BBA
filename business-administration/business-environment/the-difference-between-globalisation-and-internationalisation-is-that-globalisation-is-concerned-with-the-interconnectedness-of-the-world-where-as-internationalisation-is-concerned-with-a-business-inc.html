The difference between globalisation and internationalisation is that globalisation is concerned with the interconnectedness of the world where as internationalisation is concerned with a business increasing its operations over several countries.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Put simply, globalization is a process used by a company to bring its business to new locales, whereas internationalization refers to the act of designing a product or service in a way that makes the expansion into international markets a simpler process. Globalization and internationalization go hand in hand.

Concept note-2: -Internationalization emphasizes the relationship between and among nations, people, cultures, institutions, systems while globalization stresses the concept of worldwide flow of economy, ideas, culture, etc.

Concept note-3: -International trade happens whenever a business in one country sells to customers in another. Globalization is a political program to eliminate obstacles to international trade, creating a level playing field.

Concept note-4: -Globalization is about the interconnectedness of people and businesses across the world that eventually leads to global cultural, political and economic integration. It is the ability to move and communicate easily with others all over the world in order to conduct business internationally.
