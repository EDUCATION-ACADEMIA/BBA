____ is a market structure with a small number of firms, none of which can keep the others from having significant influence.

(A) Duopoly

(B) ** Oligopoly

(C) Monopoly

(D) Monopolistic Competition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oligopoly markets are markets dominated by a small number of suppliers. They can be found in all countries and across a broad range of sectors. Some oligopoly markets are competitive, while others are significantly less so, or can at least appear that way.

Concept note-2: -An oligopoly is a market structure with a small number of firms, none of which can keep the others from having significant influence.

Concept note-3: -Oligopoly, in which a market is by a small number of firms that together control the majority of the market share. Duopoly, a special case of an oligopoly with two firms. Monopsony, when there is only one buyer in a market.

Concept note-4: -Rather, they are oligopolies. Oligopoly arises when a small number of large firms have all or most of the sales in an industry.

Concept note-5: -Oligopoly = A market structure characterized by barriers to entry and a few firms.
