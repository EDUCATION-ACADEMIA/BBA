What do you call the data accumulated for CRM?

(A) Customer popsicle

(B) ** Customer lifecycle

(C) Customer business cycle

(D) Customer circle of life

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cumulative value of all the funds a customer spends with a company is known as their “customer lifetime value (CLV)” or “customer life cycle value”.

Concept note-2: -In customer relationship management (CRM), customer lifecycle is a term used to describe the progression of steps a customer goes through when considering, purchasing, using and maintaining loyalty to a product or service.

Concept note-3: -Customer data includes the behavioral, demographic, and personal information businesses collect from their audience. Brands collect customer data to better understand, communicate, and engage with target customers.

Concept note-4: -Customer Lifecycle Stage: Reach, Acquisition, Conversion, Retention.

Concept note-5: -CRM systems start by collecting a customer’s website, email, telephone, and social media data-and more-across multiple sources and channels.
