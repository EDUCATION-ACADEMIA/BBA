Which warnings are included with Disciplinary

(A) Refusing a qualified order 3 (three) times in a row from superiors despite having been warned

(B) Not achieving the work targets set by the Company

(C) Lack of control and supervision of subordinates resulting in delayed or constrained work

(D) ** Making trouble or noise or commotion so as to disturb the peace of work

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Statutory minimum disciplinary procedures Your employerâ€™s disciplinary procedure should include the following steps: a letter setting out the reasons or reasons why they are considering disciplinary action. a meeting to discuss the issue. a disciplinary decision.

Concept note-2: -A written warning is used, by an employer and in the form of a document, to warn an employee of their actions in the workplace, general conduct, and the potential consequences if the employee does not remedy their behavior (with regards to employment).

Concept note-3: -A written warning is a formal warning that the employer can give the employee at the end of the disciplinary procedure. A first or final written warning should say: what the misconduct or performance issue is. the changes needed, with a timescale.
