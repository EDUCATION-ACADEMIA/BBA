The name of the trade union that represents oil workers in Trinidad and Tobago

(A) PSA

(B) JITUM

(C) TTUTA

(D) ** OWTU

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Seamen and Waterfront Workers Trade Union. Steel Workers Union of Trinidad and Tobago. Transport and Industrial Workers Union. Trinidad and Tobago Postal Workers Union.

Concept note-2: -Born out of the Labour Riots of 1937 the Oilfields Workers’ Trade Union is the oldest trade union and best known champion of the working class in Trinidad and Tobago.

Concept note-3: -The Oilfields Workers’ Trade Union (OWTU), widely recognized as the strongest and most militant trade union in Trinidad and Tobago and the Anglophone Caribbean, was born out of the General Strike which started on June 19th, 1937 in the oilfields of south Trinidad.

Concept note-4: -Annual Register of Trade Unions reports that there are 136 trade unions in Trinidad and Tobago.
