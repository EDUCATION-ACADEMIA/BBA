INDUSTRIAL RELATION

[SOURCES]
What is not included in the means of industrial relations is

(A) Employers’ Organization

(B) Company regulations

(C) ** Employment Agreement

(D) Labor laws and regulations

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Parties that are not involved in industrial relations are customers. Industrial relations is concerned with the employer and the employee with their respective organization, at work or arising out of the working situation.

Concept note-2: -Charitable hospitals run by Government or even private associations cannot be included in the definition of industry because they have not embarked upon economic activities analogous to trade or business.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The approach which is not related to the industrial relations is the Employee’s Approach. Hence, the correct answer would be employee’s approach.

Concept note-4: -“Industrial Relations” refers to all types of relations between employers and workers, be they at national, regional or company level; and to all dealings with social and economic issues, such as wage setting, working time and working conditions.
