INDUSTRIAL RELATION

[SOURCES]
Any group of seven or more workers can form a trade union providing they work in different trade, occupation or industry.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to the Section, any seven or more than seven members of a trade union may by application apply for the registration of the trade union subject to the following two conditions: At Least 7 members should be employed in the establishment on the date of the making of the application.

Concept note-2: -In case of any dispute between the unions as to the membership of such members, they would be treated as no members of any union. Therefore, no workman in the establishment can have simultaneous membership in more than one union in the establishment.

Concept note-3: -Supervisory Officers and Managers in an industry can form a trade union and the same can be registered. Under the definition of workmen under the Act, all persons employed in trade or industry are included.

Concept note-4: -Company Union cannot be said to be a trade union since trade union is established for the specific purpose of advancing and protecting the interests of its members or workers in their working relationship.
