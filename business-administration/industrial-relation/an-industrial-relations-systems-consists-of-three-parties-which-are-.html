INDUSTRIAL RELATION

[SOURCES]
An industrial relations systems consists of three parties which are ____

(A) employer, employee and customers

(B) employer, employee and investors

(C) ** employer, employee and government

(D) employer, employee and suppliers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -➢ IR system consists of three agents – management and their associations; workers and their unions; and government agencies. ➢ These actors and their organizations are located within an environment –technological environment, labor and product markets, and political environment.

Concept note-2: -Abstract. We have already identified the parties (actors) in an industrial relations system to be: the workers and their organisations, the employers and their associations, and the government and its agencies.

Concept note-3: -The main parties are employers and their associations, employees and their unions and the government. These three parties interact within economic and social environment to shape the Industrial relations structure.

Concept note-4: -“Industrial Relations” refers to all types of relations between employers and workers, be they at national, regional or company level; and to all dealings with social and economic issues, such as wage setting, working time and working conditions.

Concept note-5: -The main parties are employers and their associations, employees and their unions, and the Government. These three groups interact within the economic and social environment to shape the industrial relations system.
