PRINCIPLES AND PRACTICE OF MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
Basic needs also include the physical conditions in which a person works, such as heating, lighting, and noise.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are five main classes of physical hazard namely Explosive, Flammable, Oxidising, Gases under Pressure and Corrosive to metals.

Concept note-2: -The Industrial Hygiene Principles: Anticipation, Recognition, Evaluation, Control and Confirm.

Concept note-3: -Poor lighting can be a health hazard – too much or too little light strains eyes and may cause eye discomfort (burning, etc.) and headaches.

Concept note-4: -From the workers’ perspective, poor lighting at work can lead to eye-strain, fatigue, headaches, stress and accidents. On the other hand, too much light can also cause safety and health problems such as “glare” headaches and stress. Both can lead to mistakes at work, poor quality and low productivity.
