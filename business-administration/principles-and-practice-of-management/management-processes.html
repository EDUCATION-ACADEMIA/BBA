(C) ** PLANNING, ORGANIZING, LEADING, AND CONTROLLING

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The four functions of management are planning, organizing, leading and controlling. In order to be a successful manager, you must do all four while managing your work and team. These are the foundations of any professional managerial position.

Concept note-2: -The four functions are: Planning involves the planning of decision making. Organizing includes appropriate coordination between planning and resources. Leading involves motivating the employees to achieve organizational goals. Controlling is related to monitoring and evaluation.

Concept note-3: -They were initially identified as five functions by Henri Fayol in the early 1900s. Over the years, Fayolâ€™s functions were combined and reduced to the following four main functions of management: planning, organizing, leading, and controlling.

Concept note-4: -At the most fundamental level, management is a discipline that consists of a set of five general functions: planning, organizing, staffing, leading and controlling.

Concept note-5: -7 Functions of Management: Planning, Organising, Staffing, Directing, Controlling, Co-Ordination and Co-Operation.
