What is the HR strategy employed when it is committed to managing the business ethically in order to make a positive impact on society and the environment?

(A) ** Corporate Social Responsibility

(B) Engagement

(C) Organizational Development

(D) Employee Relations

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Corporate social responsibility, or CSR, is a form of self-regulation that reflects a businessâ€™s accountability and commitment to contributing to the well-being of communities and society through various environmental and social measures.

Concept note-2: -Shaping policy. As a department, HR can advance environmental efforts through creating company policies and guidelines. Consolidating greener changes within business procedures and guidelines is a way that HR teams can make positive, climate-friendly changes.

Concept note-3: -Research to date indicates that sustainable HRM can contribute significantly to the simultaneous attainment of economic, environmental and social goals. The majority of the research has focused on using HR functions such as selection, communication, training and rewards to achieve environmental sustainability.

Concept note-4: -Human Resource Departments play a critical role in ensuring that the company adopts Corporate Social Responsibility programs. Furthermore, HR can manage the CSR plan implementation and monitor its adoption proactively, while documenting (and celebrating) its success throughout the company.
