[SOURCES]
Records of accounts identify all purchases and sales made using credit.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An accounts receivable record identifies the companies from which credit purchases were made and the amount purchased, paid, and owed.

Concept note-2: -Credit sales refer to a sale in which the amount owed will be paid at a later date. In other words, credit sales are purchases made by customers who do not render payment in full, in cash, at the time of purchase.

Concept note-3: -Accounts payable (AP) represents the amount that a company owes to its creditors and suppliers (also referred to as a current liability account). Accounts payable is recorded on the balance sheet under current liabilities.

Concept note-4: -When goods are sold on credit, accounts receivables account is debited and sales account is credited with an equal amount in order to record this transaction.
