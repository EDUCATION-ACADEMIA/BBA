An interview technique that tries to draw deeper and more elaborate explanations from the discussion is called?

(A) Focus group discussion

(B) Observation

(C) ** Probing

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Probing questions ask for more detail on a particular matter. They’re often follow-up questions like, ‘Could you tell me more about that?’ or ‘Please explain what you mean.

Concept note-2: -Often, we want or need more information than we get when we ask a question during an interview. Probing is asking follow-up questions when we do not fully understand a response, when answers are vague or ambiguous or when we want to obtain more specific or in-depth information.

Concept note-3: -Probing questions are typically open-ended, meaning there is more than just one response. Most probing questions begin with ‘what, ’ ‘why’ or ‘how. ‘ If you want the person you’re asking to expand on their response, the use of the word ‘exactly, ’ or the phrase ‘can you explain further’ should get you there.
