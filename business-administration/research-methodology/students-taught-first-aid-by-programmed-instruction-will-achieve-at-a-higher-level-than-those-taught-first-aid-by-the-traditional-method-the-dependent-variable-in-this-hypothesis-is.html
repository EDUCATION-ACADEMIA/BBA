[SOURCES]
“Students taught first aid by programmed instruction will achieve at a higher level than those taught first aid by the traditional method.” The dependent variable in this hypothesis is:

(A) students

(B) ** level of achievement

(C) programmed instruction

(D) method of instruction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -helps to explain a relationship between two other variables. “Students taught first aid by programmed instruction will achieve at a higher level than those taught first aid by the traditional method.” The dependent variable in this hypothesis is: a. the level of achievement.

Concept note-2: -In an experiment, the independent variable is manipulated and the effects observed. These observed effects are called dependent variables. They are often the hypothesized outcome of manipulating the independent variable. A change in the dependent variable depends on the independent variable, hence the name.

Concept note-3: -In any scientific investigation like experiment and scientific research, for the formulation of the hypothesis (tentative answer to the problem) the problem is necessary in first place that has a clear and broad scope of observation and knowledge.

Concept note-4: -The dependent variable is the variable that is being measured or tested in an experiment. 1 For example, in a study looking at how tutoring impacts test scores, the dependent variable would be the participants’ test scores since that is what is being measured.
