Which of the following best describes systematic sampling?

(A) ** Selecting participants at a random starting point and then at a fixed, periodic interval

(B) Dividing the population into clusters and then selecting one cluster group to participate

(C) Dividing the population into various cluster groups and then randomly selecting participants

(D) Selecting individuals at random from the list of volunteers already obtained from the population

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Systematic sampling is a type of probability sampling method in which sample members from a larger population are selected according to a random starting point but with a fixed, periodic interval. This interval, called the sampling interval, is calculated by dividing the population size by the desired sample size.

Concept note-2: -Systematic sampling is a probability sampling method where researchers select members of the population at a regular interval – for example, by selecting every 15th person on a list of the population. If the population is in a random order, this can imitate the benefits of simple random sampling.

Concept note-3: -In systematic sampling, all units of the population have equal chance of being elected in the sample.

Concept note-4: -Systematic sampling: Researchers use the systematic sampling method to choose the sample members of a population at regular intervals. It requires selecting a starting point for the sample and sample size that can be repeated at regular intervals.
