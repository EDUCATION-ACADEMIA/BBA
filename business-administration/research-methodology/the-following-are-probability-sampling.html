The following are probability sampling

(A) ** simple random sampling

(B) quote sampling

(C) purposive sampling

(D) ** cluster random sampling

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Probability sampling means that every member of the target population has a known chance of being included in the sample. Probability sampling methods include simple random sampling, systematic sampling, stratified sampling, and cluster sampling.

Concept note-2: -There are four primary, random (probability) sampling methods – simple random sampling, systematic sampling, stratified sampling, and cluster sampling.

Concept note-3: -Simple random sampling, stratified sampling, cluster sampling, and systematic sampling are all types of probability sampling. But there’s another end of the sampling technique spectrum: non-probability sampling.

Concept note-4: -Cluster sampling is a probability sampling method in which you divide a population into clusters, such as districts or schools, and then randomly select some of these clusters as your sample.

Concept note-5: -Four main methods include: 1) simple random, 2) stratified random, 3) cluster, and 4) systematic. Non-probability sampling – the elements that make up the sample, are selected by nonrandom methods. This type of sampling is less likely than probability sampling to produce representative samples.
