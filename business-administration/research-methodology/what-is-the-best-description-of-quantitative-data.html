What is the best description of quantitative data?

(A) Non-numerical data in the form of opinions and feelings.

(B) Closed questions that are used in questionnaires.

(C) Types of primary data that use observations and interviews.

(D) ** Types of primary data that use numbers and statistics.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Quantitative data is the value of data in the form of counts or numbers where each data set has a unique numerical value. This data is any quantifiable information that researchers can use for mathematical calculations and statistical analysis to make real-life decisions based on these mathematical derivations.

Concept note-2: -Discrete data is information that is collected and can be counted. This type of data only has a certain number of values. Typically, it involves integers-whole numbers, rather than fractions.

Concept note-3: -Quantitative data analysis It is the form of data expressed in the form of numerical. With quantitative data, we usually try to answer the question involving quantity, frequency, size.

Concept note-4: -Quantitative data are used when a researcher is trying to quantify a problem, or address the “what” or “how many” aspects of a research question. It is data that can either be counted or compared on a numeric scale.

Concept note-5: -Quantitative methods Surveys: paper, kiosk, mobile, questionnaires. Longitudinal studies. Polls. Telephone interviews.
