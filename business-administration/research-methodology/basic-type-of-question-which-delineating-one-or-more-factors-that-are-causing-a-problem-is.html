Basic type of question which delineating one or more factors that are causing a problem is?

(A) Exploratory

(B) Descriptive

(C) ** Causal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Correlational Study CORRELATIONAL STUDY: When the researcher is interested in delineating (defining, describing) the important variables associated with the problem, the study is called a co-relational study.

Concept note-2: -In a causal study, the researcher is interested in delineating one or more factors that are causing a problem.

Concept note-3: -Causal: Cause and Effect Questions Designed to determine whether one or more variables causes or affects one or more outcome variables. What is affect of exercise on heart rate? What is the effect hand fatigue on reaction time?

Concept note-4: -Causal Questions Causal research questions aim to find treatment targets, identify factors that increase the risk of getting a condition or injury, or estimate what will happen to people who receive one treatment com-pared to another.
