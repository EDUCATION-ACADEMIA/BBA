RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

It describes the sample of the study.

(A) ** Research Respondents

(B) Research Locale

(C) Research Population

(D) Research Design

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Respondents are those individuals who complete a survey or interview for the researcher, or who provide data to be analyzed for the research study. Respondents can be any age, but determined by the scope of the study, and must agree to informed consent to participate.

Concept note-2: -Sampling means selecting the group that you will actually collect data from in your research. For example, if you are researching the opinions of students in your university, you could survey a sample of 100 students. In statistics, sampling allows you to test a hypothesis about the characteristics of a population.

Concept note-3: -All you have to do is take the number of respondents you need, divide by your expected response rate, and multiple by 100. For example, if you need 500 customers to respond to your survey and you know the response rate is 30%, you should invite about 1, 666 people to your study (500/30*100 = 1, 666).
