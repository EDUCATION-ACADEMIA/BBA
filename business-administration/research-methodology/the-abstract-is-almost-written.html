The abstract is almost written?

(A) ** After completing the research

(B) Before writing the research

(C) all the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The abstract is the very last thing you write. You should only write it after your research is complete, so that you can accurately summarize the entirety of your thesis, dissertation or research paper.

Concept note-2: -The function of the abstract is to outline briefly all parts of the paper. Although it is placed at the beginning of your paper, immediately following the title page, the abstract should be the last thing that you write, once you are sure of the conclusions you will reach.

Concept note-3: -Although you might be tempted to write your abstract first because it will appear as the very first part of your paper, it’s a good idea to wait to write your abstract until after you’ve drafted your full paper, so that you know what you’re summarizing.

Concept note-4: -Although it is the first section of your paper, the abstract should be written last since it will summarize the contents of your entire paper.
