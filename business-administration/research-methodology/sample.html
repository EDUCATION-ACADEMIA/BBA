RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Sample

(A) ** the small group of participants, out of the total number available, that a researcher studies

(B) research method in which the psychologist observes the subject in a natural setting without interferring

(C) research method that involves an intensive investigation of one or more participants

(D) research method in which information is obtained by asking many individuals a fixed set of questions

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The small group is referred to as a sample size, which is a subset of the population used to represent the entire population. A sample may be selected from a population through a number of ways, one of which is the stratified random sampling method.

Concept note-2: -Sampling is the process of selecting a representative group from the population under study. The target population is the total group of individuals from which the sample might be drawn. A sample is the group of people who take part in the investigation. The people who take part are referred to as “participants”.

Concept note-3: -Sample size refers to the number of participants or observations included in a study. This number is usually represented by n.

Concept note-4: -In statistics, a population is the pool of individuals from which a statistical sample is drawn for a study.
