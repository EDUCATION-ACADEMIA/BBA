Which of the following is an advantage of sampling when conducting research?

(A) It guarantees subject participation.

(B) It allows the researcher to collect digital data.

(C) It increases the amount of information collected.

(D) ** It provides the researcher with a cross-section of the population

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Advantages of Sample Surveys compared with Censuses: Reduces cost-both in monetary terms and staffing requirements. Reduces time needed to collect and process the data and produce results as it requires a smaller scale of operation. (Because of the above reasons) enables more detailed questions to be asked.

Concept note-2: -In Sampling, the population is isolated into various parts called examining units. Sampling guarantees accommodation, an assortment of concentrated and thorough data, suitability in restricted assets and better compatibility.

Concept note-3: -The advantages of a simple random sample include its ease of use and its accurate representation of the larger population. In this method, all the units of the sample have equal chance of selection. It is an unbiased method of selection.

Concept note-4: -It is efficient: When a sample is studied, instead of a whole population, it is a much quicker process and is more time efficient. It is practical: Most studies aim to make inferences about large populations. These populations are too large to collect data from each element within them.
