What type of variable describes categorical data?

(A) ordinal

(B) ** nominal

(C) ratio

(D) interval

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A categorical variable (also called qualitative variable) refers to a characteristic that canâ€™t be quantifiable. Categorical variables can be either nominal or ordinal.

Concept note-2: -A categorical variable (sometimes called a nominal variable) is one that has two or more categories, but there is no intrinsic ordering to the categories.

Concept note-3: -Categorical variables represent types of data which may be divided into groups. Examples of categorical variables are race, sex, age group, and educational level.

Concept note-4: -Categorical variables can be further defined as nominal, dichotomous, or ordinal. Nominal variables describe categories that do not have a specific order to them. These include ethnicity or gender.

Concept note-5: -Ordinal categorical data are non-numerical pieces of information with implied order-for example, survey responses on a scale from very dissatisfied to very satisfied. And nominal categorical data are non-numerical pieces of information without any inherent order-for example, colors or states.
