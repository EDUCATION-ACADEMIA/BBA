When you do a checking of tools, what we call the process of validity used?

(A) Criterion validity

(B) Face validity

(C) ** Content validity

(D) Construct validity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Measuring content validity involves assessing individual questions on a test and asking experts whether each one targets characteristics that the instrument is designed to cover. This process compares the test against its goals and the theoretical properties of the construct.

Concept note-2: -In psychometrics, content validity (also known as logical validity) refers to the extent to which a measure represents all facets of a given construct.

Concept note-3: -Common methods to assess construct validity include, but are not limited to, factor analysis, correlation tests, and item response theory models (including Rasch model).

Concept note-4: -Measuring content validity requires input from a judging panel of subject matter experts (SMEs). Here, SMEs are people who are in the best position to evaluate the content of a test.
