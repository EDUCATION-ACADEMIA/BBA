A positive correlation implies that one variable predicts the ____ of another variable.

(A) removal

(B) ** Presence

(C) decrease

(D) elimination

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Key Takeaways. A positive correlation is a relationship between two variables that tend to move in the same direction. A positive correlation exists when one variable tends to decrease as the other variable decreases, or one variable tends to increase when the other increases.

Concept note-2: -A positive correlation exists when two variables move in the same direction as one another. A basic example of positive correlation is height and weight-taller people tend to be heavier, and vice versa. In some cases, positive correlation exists because one variable influences the other.

Concept note-3: -1. Positive Correlation: Correlation between two variables is said to be positive if the values of the variables deviate in the same direction i.e. if the values of one variable increase (or decrease) then the values of another variable also increase (or decrease).

Concept note-4: -Correlation tests for a relationship between two variables. However, seeing two variables moving together does not necessarily mean we know whether one variable causes the other to occur. This is why we commonly say “correlation does not imply causation.”
