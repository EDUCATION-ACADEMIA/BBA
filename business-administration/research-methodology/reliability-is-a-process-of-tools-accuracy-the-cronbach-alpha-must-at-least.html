Reliability is a process of tools accuracy. The Cronbach alpha must at least

(A) 0.5

(B) ** 0.6

(C) ** 0.7

(D) 0.1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Analysts frequently use 0.7 as a benchmark value for Cronbach’s alpha. At this level and higher, the items are sufficiently consistent to indicate the measure is reliable. Typically, values near 0.7 are minimally acceptable but not ideal. However, some fields and industries have different minimum values.

Concept note-2: -Cronbach’s alpha reliability coefficient normally ranges between 0 and 1. However, there is actually no lower limit to the coefficient. The closer Cronbach’s alpha coefficient is to 1.0 the greater the internal consistency of the items in the scale.
