[SOURCES]
Use quotes around a phrase ("We the people") to search for websites where ____

(A) ** those exact words inside the quotes appear in that exact order.

(B) those words are found in any order.

(C) those words are not found.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cite web postings as you would a standard web entry. Provide the author of the work, the title of the posting in quotation marks, the web site name in italics, the publisher, and the posting date. Follow with the date of access.

Concept note-2: -Use double quotation marks (“”) around a direct quote. A direct quote is a word-for-word report of what someone else said or wrote. You use the exact words and punctuation of the original.

Concept note-3: -And in the case of both general and field-specific common knowledge, if you use the exact words of the reference source, you must use quotation marks and credit the source.

Concept note-4: -If the idea is new, or not common knowledge, cite the source. Use quotation marks whenever you use someone else’s words, but use direct quotes sparingly (e.g., to support your point with the words of an authority, or when original wording is unusual, strong, or characteristic of the speaker).
