Data from correlational studies are usually shown as:

(A) ** scatter-plot

(B) pie chart

(C) Bar graph

(D) curve

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Scatterplots provide a visual representation of the correlation, or relationship between the two variables.

Concept note-2: -A scatterplot is a type of data display that shows the relationship between two numerical variables. Each member of the dataset gets plotted as a point whose x-y coordinates relates to its values for the two variables.

Concept note-3: -A scatter plot is a type of data visualization that shows the relationship between different variables. This data is shown by placing various data points between an x-and y-axis.

Concept note-4: -Scatter plots show how much one variable is affected by another. The relationship between two variables is called their correlation. There are three types of correlation: positive, negative, and none (no correlation).
