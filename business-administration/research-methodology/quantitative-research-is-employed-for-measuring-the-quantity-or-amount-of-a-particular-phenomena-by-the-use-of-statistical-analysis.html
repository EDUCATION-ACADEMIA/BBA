RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Quantitative Research is employed for measuring the quantity or amount of a particular phenomena by the use of statistical analysis.

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Quantitative research is based on measurement and is conducted in a systematic, controlled manner. These measures enable researchers to perform statistical tests, analyze differences between groups, and determine the effectiveness of treatments. If something is not measurable, it cannot be tested.

Concept note-2: -Quantitative research methods are designed to collect numerical data that can be used to measure variables. Quantitative data is structured and statistical; its results are objective and conclusive.

Concept note-3: -Quantitative research focuses on gathering numerical data and generalizing it across groups of people or to explain a particular phenomenon.

Concept note-4: -The two most commonly used quantitative data analysis methods are descriptive statistics and inferential statistics.
