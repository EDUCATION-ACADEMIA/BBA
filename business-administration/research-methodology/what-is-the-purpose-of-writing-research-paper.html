What is the purpose of writing research paper?

(A) To show off research skills

(B) To gain popularity

(C) ** To communicate research findings

(D) To get promotions

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Research papers are an excellent platform to disseminate a novel finding to a general or focused audience, which may be interested in replicating the experiments; identifying novel applications for the finding, etc., as well as to popularize your research to the world and prevent duplication of the study.

Concept note-2: -Communicating the findings of your research is a key part of an academic endeavour. This can involve publishing your work for the benefit of the academic community, sharing findings with interested parties or policy and decision makers, or disseminating them to the general public.

Concept note-3: -The goal of a research paper is not to inform the reader what others have to say about a topic, but to draw on what others have to say about a topic and engage the sources in order to thoughtfully offer a unique perspective on the issue at hand. This is accomplished through two major types of research papers.

Concept note-4: -Three of the most influential and common purposes of research are exploration, description and explanation.

Concept note-5: -Having the skills to communicate research in an effective way has a positive impact on researchers themselves. By making the public understand what they are doing, they can prevent misinformation from arising and help to sustain support for their research.
