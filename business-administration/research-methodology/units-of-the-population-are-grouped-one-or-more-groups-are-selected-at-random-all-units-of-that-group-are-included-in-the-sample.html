RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Units of the population are grouped; one or more groups are selected at random. All units of that group are included in the sample.

(A) ** cluster sample

(B) simple random

(C) stratified

(D) proportional

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stratified random sample: The population is first split into groups. The overall sample consists of some members from every group. The members from each group are chosen randomly.

Concept note-2: -Cluster sampling divides the population into groups, then takes a random sample from each cluster. Both systematic and cluster sampling are forms of random sampling, known as probability sampling, which stands in contrast to non-probability sampling.

Concept note-3: -Probability sampling refers to the selection of a sample from a population, when this selection is based on the principle of randomization, that is, random selection or chance.

Concept note-4: -Cluster sampling is defined as a sampling method where the researcher creates multiple clusters of people from a population where they are indicative of homogeneous characteristics and have an equal chance of being a part of the sample.
