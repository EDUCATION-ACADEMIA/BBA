When to Use the Case Study Research Approach

(A) appropriate when the researcher wants to answer a English question

(B) appropriate when the researcher wants to answer a TeacherÂ´s question

(C) ** appropriate when the researcher wants to answer a descriptive question

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A case study is an appropriate research design when you want to gain concrete, contextual, in-depth knowledge about a specific real-world subject. It allows you to explore the key characteristics, meanings, and implications of the case. Case studies are often a good choice in a thesis or dissertation.

Concept note-2: -The case study approach is particularly useful to employ when there is a need to obtain an in-depth appreciation of an issue, event or phenomenon of interest, in its natural real-life context.

Concept note-3: -Case studies capture a range of perspectives, as opposed to the single view of an individual you get with a survey response or interview. This gives the opportunity to gain a greater understanding of the subject in hand and reduces the potential for any bias, by diluting the agenda of a particular individual.

Concept note-4: -The research methods you use depend on the type of data you need to answer your research question. If you want to measure something or test a hypothesis, use quantitative methods. If you want to explore ideas, thoughts and meanings, use qualitative methods.
