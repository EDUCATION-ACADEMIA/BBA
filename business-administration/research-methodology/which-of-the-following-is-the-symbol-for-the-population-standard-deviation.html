Which of the following is the symbol for the population standard deviation?

(A) s

(B) μ

(C) ** σ

(D) s^2

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The symbol ‘’ represents the population standard deviation.

Concept note-2: -The population standard deviation is a number representing the spread of the population distribution and is therefore an example of a population parameter. The Greek letter (sigma) is the most common symbol for the population standard deviation.

Concept note-3: -Standard deviation may be abbreviated SD, and is most commonly represented in mathematical texts and equations by the lower case Greek letter (sigma), for the population standard deviation, or the Latin letter s, for the sample standard deviation.

Concept note-4: -A standard deviation is a measure of how unusual a set of data is if a hypothesis is true. Physicists express standard deviations in units called sigma, . The higher the number of sigma, the more incompatible the data are with the hypothesis.
