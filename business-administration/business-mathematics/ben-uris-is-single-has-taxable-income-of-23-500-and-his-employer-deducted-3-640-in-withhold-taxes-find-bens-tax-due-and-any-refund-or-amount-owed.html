Ben Uris is single, has taxable income of $23, 500, and his employer deducted $3, 640 in withhold taxes. Find Ben’s tax due and any refund or amount owed

(A) $502; $3, 138 refund

(B) $31.38; $5.02 refund

(C) ** $3, 138 ;$502 refund

(D) $5.02; $31.38 refund

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your gross income minus all available deductions is your taxable income. Compare that amount to your tax bracket to estimate the amount you’ll owe before applying any available tax credits.

Concept note-2: -Taxable income or gross income or adjusted gross income includes salaries, wages, bonuses, etc. along with unearned income and investment income. It is the amount that will be used to determine your tax liability.

Concept note-3: -The AGI calculation is relatively straightforward. It is equal to the total income you report that’s subject to income tax-such as earnings from your job, self-employment, dividends and interest from a bank account-minus specific deductions, or “adjustments” that you’re eligible to take.
