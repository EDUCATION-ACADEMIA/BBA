It is the spent on the operations of the business

(A) Buying

(B) ** Expenses

(C) Cost

(D) Capital

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Opex (operational expenditure) is the money a company or organization spends on an ongoing, day-to-day basis to run its business. These expenses can be one-time or recurring. Depending on the industry, these expenses can range from the ink used to print documents to the wages employees are paid.

Concept note-2: -Operating expenses-also known as selling, general and administrative expenses (SG&A)-are the costs of doing business. They include rent and utilities, marketing and advertising, sales and accounting, management and administrative salaries.

Concept note-3: -In accounting terms, expense is the operational cost that is paid to earn business revenues. It means the outflow of cash in return for goods or services. Expenses can also be written as the sum of all the operations that usually bring profit.
