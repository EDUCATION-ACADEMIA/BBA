There are ____ pay periods in a year if you are paid weekly.

(A) 26

(B) 24

(C) ** 52

(D) 12

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Weekly – 52 pay periods per year (53 in leap years) Biweekly – 26 pay periods per year. Monthly payments – 12 pay periods per year. Semi-monthly payments – 24 pay periods per year.

Concept note-2: -There are 26 pay periods in a year when your company runs payroll on a bi-weekly schedule. That is because there are 52 weeks in a year. If one period covers two weeks, 52 weeks divided by two weeks results in 26 two-week pay periods in a year.

Concept note-3: -How do you calculate a pay period? Number of Pay Periods in the Year/Annual Salary = Gross Wages per Pay Period.

Concept note-4: -A pay period is a time frame used to calculate earned wages and determine when employees receive their paychecks. Pay periods are fixed and most often recurring on a weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly or monthly basis.

Concept note-5: -Employees receive 26 paychecks per year with a biweekly pay schedule. Depending on the calendar year, there are sometimes 27 pay periods, which can increase payroll costs. Both hourly and salaried employees may receive biweekly pay.
