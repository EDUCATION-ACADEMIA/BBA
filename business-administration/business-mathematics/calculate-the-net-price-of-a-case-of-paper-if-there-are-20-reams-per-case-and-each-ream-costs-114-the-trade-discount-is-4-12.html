Calculate the net price of a case of paper if there are 20 reams per case and each ream costs $1.14. The trade discount is 4 1/2%.

(A) $0.05

(B) $1.03

(C) ** $21.77

(D) $22.80

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Another way of finding the net price is the complement method. The complement of your trade-discount rate is 100 percent minus the discount rate. This is sometimes called the net-price rate. You can multiply the list price by the complement to find the net price.

Concept note-2: -Net price is what a customer pays. To calculate the net price, start with the list price and add any taxes and other government-mandated charges. Then subtract any discounts, rebates or negotiated prices.

Concept note-3: -Trade discounts are usually based on the list price (catalogue price). Sales are recorded based on net price. Net price = List price â€“ Trade discount. Therefore, trade discounts are not recorded in the books of accounts.

Concept note-4: -5% is a trade discount that rewards a bulk payment. 5% is a cash discount that rewards an early invoice payment within 30 days.
