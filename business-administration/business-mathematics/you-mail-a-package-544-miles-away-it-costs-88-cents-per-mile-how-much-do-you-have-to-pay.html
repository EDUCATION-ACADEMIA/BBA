You mail a package 544 miles away, it costs 88 cents per mile. How much do you have to pay?

(A) ** $478.72

(B) $748.72

(C) $487.27

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To calculate your cost per mile, simply divide your total expenses for the month by the total number of miles you have driven that month. For example, if your expenses totaled $3, 000 and you drove 10, 000 miles, your cost per mile is $0.30.

Concept note-2: -To calculate cost per order, you first need to add up all of your order expenses-everything you spend to acquire, fulfill, package, and ship orders-for a set period of time. Then, you divide your order expenses by the total number of orders you received during the same timeframe.

Concept note-3: -To calculate the cost per load, simply divide the total cost by the number of loads.

Concept note-4: -What is CPM on YouTube? CPM, or cost per mille, shows how much advertisers pay content creators per 1, 000 views (and/or clicks). For instance, if you get 200, 000 views and have a CPM that’s $10.50, the video’s total revenue is $2, 100. Not too shabby.
