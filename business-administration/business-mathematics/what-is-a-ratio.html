What is a Ratio?

(A) It is used to compare the number to one in different units.

(B) It is referred to as an equation.

(C) ** It is used to describe relationships.

(D) Always written as a single number on a per-unit basis.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ratio defines the relationship between the quantities of two or more objects. It is used to compare the quantities of the same kind. If two or more ratios are equal, then it is said to be in proportion.

Concept note-2: -A ratio expresses the relationship between two quantities. Ratio reasoning can be applied to many different types of mathematical and real-life problems. A ratio is a distinct entity, different from the two measures that make it up.

Concept note-3: -In mathematics, a ratio shows how many times one number contains another. For example, if there are eight oranges and six lemons in a bowl of fruit, then the ratio of oranges to lemons is eight to six (that is, 8:6, which is equivalent to the ratio 4:3).

Concept note-4: -Two ratios that describe the same relationship are equivalent ratios.

Concept note-5: -A ratio is a comparison in terms of a quotient of two numbers a and b, usually written as ab or a/b. Science regularly requires us to compare values. Ratios are used to compare the amount of one number to another number.
