Adjusted gross income minus deductions and exemptions is

(A) ** Taxable Income

(B) Net Pay

(C) Witholding Adjustment

(D) Gross Pay

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Taxable Income – This is your AGI minus either the standard deduction or total of itemized deductions-whichever is greater and the qualified business income deduction if applicable. Your taxable income is what you’ll use to determine your tax bracket.

Concept note-2: -Taxable income is a layman’s term that refers to your adjusted gross income (AGI) less any itemized deductions you’re entitled to claim or your standard deduction.

Concept note-3: -It is equal to the total income you report that’s subject to income tax-such as earnings from your job, self-employment, dividends and interest from a bank account-minus specific deductions, or “adjustments” that you’re eligible to take.

Concept note-4: -A parent’s adjusted taxable income is the total of the following components: taxable income for the last relevant year of income (including overseas income if the parent is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction) reportable fringe benefits total for that year of income. target foreign income for that year of income.

Concept note-5: -Key Takeaways. Gross income is the entire amount of money an individual makes, including wages, salaries, bonuses, and capital gains. Adjusted gross income (AGI) is an individual’s taxable income after accounting for deductions and adjustments.
