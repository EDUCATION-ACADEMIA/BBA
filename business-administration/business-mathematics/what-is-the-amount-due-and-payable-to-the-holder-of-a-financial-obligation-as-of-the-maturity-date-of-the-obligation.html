What is the amount due and payable to the holder of a financial obligation as of the maturity date of the obligation?

(A) principal amount

(B) monthly amortization

(C) mortgage

(D) ** maturity value

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Maturity value is the amount due and payable to the holder of a financial obligation as of the maturity date of the obligation. The term usually refers to the remaining principal balance on a loan or bond.

Concept note-2: -At maturity, the amount paid to the bondholders is the face value (or par value) amount, which is also the fair value on that date.

Concept note-3: -The interest that is deducted is called the discount, and the actual amount that is given to the borrower is called the proceeds. The amount the borrower is obligated to repay is called the maturity value.

Concept note-4: -The difference between the principal amount of a note and its maturity value is called interest.

Concept note-5: -The Maturity Value (MV) of a loan is the sum of the principal P plus the interest I. In Example 1, Jo borrowed $2000 at an interest rate of 5%. At the end of one year Jo owed $100 in interest. The maturity value of the loan is MV = P + I where P = $2000 and I = $100.
