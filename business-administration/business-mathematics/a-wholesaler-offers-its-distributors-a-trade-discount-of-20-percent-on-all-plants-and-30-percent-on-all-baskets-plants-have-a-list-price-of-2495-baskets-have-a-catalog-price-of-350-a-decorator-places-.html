A WHOLESALER OFFERS ITS DISTRIBUTORS A TRADE DISCOUNT OF 20 PERCENT ON ALL PLANTS AND 30 PERCENT ON ALL BASKETS. PLANTS HAVE A LIST PRICE OF $24.95. BASKETS HAVE A CATALOG PRICE OF $3.50. A DECORATOR PLACES AN ORDER FOR 50 PLANTS AND 100 BASKETS. WHAT IS THE NET PRICE OF THE ORDER?

(A) $20.27

(B) $1, 118.25

(C) ** $1, 153.25

(D) $1, 278.00

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Get the the discount percentage (for example 20% ) Calculate the savings: 20% of $90 = $18. Subtract the savings from the original price to get the sale price: $90-$18 = $72.

Concept note-2: -In that 20/10/5 split, the 20 percent is taken off the entire purchase amount, while the 10 percent is deducted from what remains, and the five percent deducted from what’s left over after the 10 percent is deducted.

Concept note-3: -Trade discounts are usually based on the list price (catalogue price). Sales are recorded based on net price. Net price = List price – Trade discount. Therefore, trade discounts are not recorded in the books of accounts.

Concept note-4: -List Price = Rs 220. Also, Discount = 10% of List Price or we can write = (10/100) × 220 = Rs 22. Thus we can write: S.P. = List Price – Discount = 220 – 22 = Rs 198.
