The number above the line in a common fraction.

(A) ** NUMERATOR

(B) DENAMINATOR

(C) FRACTIONS

(D) WHOLE NUMBER

(E) IMPROPER FRACTION

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The number on the top of the line is called the numerator. It tells how many equal parts of the whole or collection are taken. The number below the line is called the denominator.

Concept note-2: -A fraction is made up of two integers, one on the top, and the other at the bottom which is separated by a line. The number on top is called the numerator, and the number on the bottom is called the denominator.

Concept note-3: -Numerator: The number above the line in a fraction. It indicates how many parts of the whole are being counted. Denominator: The number below the line in a fraction. It indicates how many total parts are in the whole.

Concept note-4: -A vinculum can indicate the repetend of a repeating decimal value: 1⁄7 = 0.
