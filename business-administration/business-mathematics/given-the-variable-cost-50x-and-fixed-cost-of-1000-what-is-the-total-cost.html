Given the variable cost 50x and fixed cost of 1000, what is the total cost?

(A) 50x-1000

(B) 1000x + 50

(C) 1000x-50

(D) ** 50x + 1000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The total cost of production is divided by the total amount paid in numbers, forming the average total cost formula. A straightforward and easy-to-use procedure, the total-cost formula is calculated by dividing the total production cost by the number of products manufactured.

Concept note-2: -Variable costs are the sum of all labor and materials required to produce a unit of your product. Your total variable cost is equal to the variable cost per unit, multiplied by the number of units produced. Your average variable cost is equal to your total variable cost, divided by the number of units produced.

Concept note-3: -Total Fixed Cost TFC:-The total amount of money spends on fixed factors of production is called fixed cost.It can be obtained by subtracting total variable cost from total costTFC = TC-TVCTotal Variable Cost TVC:-The total amount of money spends on variable factors of production is called total variable cost.
