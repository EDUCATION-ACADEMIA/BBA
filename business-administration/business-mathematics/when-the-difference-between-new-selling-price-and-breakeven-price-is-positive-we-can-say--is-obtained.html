When the difference between New Selling Price and Breakeven Price is positive, we can say ____ is obtained.

(A) Loss

(B) ** Profit

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As illustrated in the graph above, the point at which total fixed and variable costs are equal to total revenues is known as the break even point. At the break even point, a business does not make a profit or loss. Therefore, the break even point is often referred to as the “no-profit” or “no-loss point.”

Concept note-2: -Your break-even point is equal to your fixed costs, divided by your average selling price, minus variable costs. It is the point at which revenue is equal to costs and anything beyond that makes the business profitable.

Concept note-3: -The break-even point does not change when sales change. It remains the point at which revenue covers variable and fixed costs without any profit or loss. A company experiences a profit if sales surpass the break-even point and a loss if sales drop below it.

Concept note-4: -How to calculate break-even. Use the following calculations to find where your profits start. To calculate your break-even (dollar value) before net profit: Break-even ($) = overhead expenses ÷ (1 − (COGS ÷ total sales))
