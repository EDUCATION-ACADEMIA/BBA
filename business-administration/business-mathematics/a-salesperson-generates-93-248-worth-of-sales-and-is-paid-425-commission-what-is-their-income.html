A salesperson generates $93, 248 worth of sales and is paid 4.25% Commission. What is their income?

(A) ** $3 963.04

(B) $4 250.00

(C) $21 940.71

(D) $2 194.07

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To calculate the payable commission, multiply the sales revenue by the sales commission rate. A 10 percent commission rate on a $10, 000 product deal would pay $1, 000 in commission.

Concept note-2: -A commission is a percentage of total sales as determined by the rate of commission. To find the commission on a sale, multiply the rate of commission by the total sales. Just as we did for computing sales tax, remember to first convert the rate of commission from a percent to a decimal.

Concept note-3: -A commission, in its simplest form, is some percentage of revenue. For example, a salesperson may earn 3% of whatever they sell. If a product is sold for $100, the salesperson would earn $3 from that sale.

Concept note-4: -In the simplest definition, the commission is money paid out to a selling agent. For example, if you were selling cars and your selling agent was paid 5%, you would get 95% of the sales price. If you make a sale, you get some portion of the money from the purchaser.
