The following are examples of current Assets except

(A) Cash

(B) Accounts Receivables

(C) Prepaid Expenses

(D) ** Unearned Income

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: Answer: d. Sales Revenue. Assets are defined as the resources owned by the company.

Concept note-2: -Some examples of current assets include cash, cash equivalents, short-term investments, accounts receivable, inventory, supplies, and prepaid expenses.

Concept note-3: -Types of Current Assets: Pre-paid expenses. Account receivable. Marketable securities.

Concept note-4: -Land is not a current asset, because land will NOT turn to cash within one year of the balance sheet date, or within the operating cycle if the operating cycle is longer than one year.

Concept note-5: -Current assets include cash, cash equivalents, accounts receivable, stock inventory, marketable securities, pre-paid liabilities, and other liquid assets. Current assets are important to businesses because they can be used to fund day-to-day business operations and to pay for the ongoing operating expenses.
