What is the time and a half rate for $14?

(A) ** $21

(B) $22

(C) $34

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the typical case of an hourly or non-exempt employee, if you are paying Sandy $14 per hour, you would have to pay her 1.5 x $14 = $21 per hour for every overtime hour. Time and a half pay is due for any hours over the standard 40-hour workweek for employees that qualify for overtime pay under the FLSA.

Concept note-3: -Assume an employee earns $20 hourly during a 40-hour work week. Their time and a half pay would be $20 x 1.5 for a total of $30 an hour.
