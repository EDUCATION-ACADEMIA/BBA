When is payment made on an ordinary annuity?

(A) Beginning of the period

(B) Middle of the period

(C) First 3 days of the period

(D) ** End of the period

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An ordinary annuity is a series of regular payments made at the end of each period, such as monthly or quarterly. In an annuity due, by contrast, payments are made at the beginning of each period.

Concept note-2: -If the periodic payments are made at the end of each period, the annuity is called an immediate annuity or ordinary annuity.

Concept note-3: -If the payments are made at the end of the time periods, so that interest is accumulated before the payment, the annuity is called an annuity-immediate, or ordinary annuity.

Concept note-4: -A life annuity will continue to make payments until the annuitantâ€™s death. A fixed-term annuity will end when the term is up, with the remaining balance paid out in a lump sum or rolled over into another investment.
