What does it mean that you have to “adapt to your audience” during the step of writing business messages?

(A) It means that you should proofread the final draft for typos, spelling errors, and other mechanical problems.

(B) It means that you should distribute your message, using the best combination of personal and technological tools.

(C) ** It means that you should be sensitive to your audience’s needs by adopting the “you” attitude, being polite, emphasizing the positive, and using bias-free language.

(D) It means that you should revise your message by evaluating the content, reviewing readability, and then editing and rewriting until your message comes across concisely and clearly, with correct grammar, proper punctuation, and effective format

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adapting a speech to an audience is not the same thing as simply telling an audience what they want to hear. Audience analysis does not mean ‘grandstanding’ or ‘kowtowing’ to a public. Rather, adaptation guides the stylistic and content choices a speaker makes for a presentation.

Concept note-2: -Audience sensitivity is conceptualized as a tendency to fit one’s communications to the distinctive features of one’s audience, out of a motivation to promote their epistemic goods for its own sake. The relevant features of one’s audience include its distinctive needs, interests, views, abilities, and dispositions.

Concept note-3: -Taking an audience-centered approach will make you a more effective speaker, and being able to adapt your presentation is key. Often, there is a very fine line between over-adapting and under-adapting, but the more you practice it, the easier and more straightforward it will become.
