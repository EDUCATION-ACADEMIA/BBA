Writing a letter in uppercase (B) rather than lowercase (b)

(A) abbreviation

(B) ** capitalization

(C) contraction

(D) exclamation point

(E) question mark

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -English, and other nationalities and languages, are capitalized since they are proper nouns. Even the word “British” to describe nationals or natives of the United Kingdom, including England, is capitalized.

Concept note-2: -In general, you should capitalize the first word, all nouns, all verbs (even short ones, like is), all adjectives, and all proper nouns. That means you should lowercase articles, conjunctions, and prepositions-however, some style guides say to capitalize conjunctions and prepositions that are longer than five letters.

Concept note-3: -Uppercase letters: A-Z. Lowercase letters: a-z. Numbers: 0-9. Symbols:  ‘!
