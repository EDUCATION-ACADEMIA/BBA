When a writer revises a draft, which of these issues should he or she consider?

(A) A specific word choice for each idea

(B) ** The overall flow of ideas and paragraphs

(C) Spelling and mechanics

(D) The structure of each sentence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When we revise our writing, we take the opportunity to step back and re-envision it. We think about the goals of the paper and whether we have accomplished these goals. We ensure that our ideas are clearly expressed and well supported.

Concept note-2: -Whether you are revising a business letter, a research paper, or a short story, a logical first step of the revision process is to look at the overall structure of your draft. Before you begin analyzing word choice and polishing punctuation, make sure that the structure of your writing is sound.
