BUSINESS COMMUNICATION

[SOURCES]
Gossip and rumour are part of ____ communication.

(A) Formal.

(B) ** Informal.

(C) Horizontal.

(D) Vertical.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rumours and gossips are informal communication. The Informal Communication is the casual and unofficial form of communication wherein the information is exchanged spontaneously between two or more persons without conforming the prescribed official rules, processes, system, formalities and chain of command.

Concept note-2: -Gossip is largely a spontaneous form of verbal communication that two or more people use to praise or accuse pe-ople and to share valuable and important information (Kurland and Pelled, 2000; Michelson and Mouly, 2000; Foster, 2004; Atak, 2005; Waddington and Fletcher, 2005; Thomas and Rozell, 2007).

Concept note-3: -An informal communication system is also known as grapevine. Under informal communication, a piece of information flows in all directions without paying any heed to the level or authority. It arises out of social interactions among the employees and then spreads throughout the organisation.

Concept note-4: -Gossip, rumour and genres of communication Gossip can be considered as a genre of communication – as having its own descriptive and prescriptive conventions for production and interpretation, which are understood tacitly by communicators, and which are used to evaluate the success of a particular exchange.

Concept note-5: -1. variable noun. Gossip is informal conversation, often about other people’s private affairs.
