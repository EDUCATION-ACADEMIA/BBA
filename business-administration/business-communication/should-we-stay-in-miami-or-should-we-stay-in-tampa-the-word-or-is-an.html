EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In English, we use just one conjunction to connect two clauses. Incorrect: Because he is intelligent so he gets good marks. Correct: Because he is intelligent he gets good marks. OR He is intelligent so he gets good marks.

Concept note-2: -Subordinating conjunctions introduce the dependent (or subordinate) clause in a complex sentence. The dependent clause tells you about the other part of the sentence and cannot stand alone. Some common subordinating conjunctions are after, before, as, while, until, because, since, unless, although, and if.

Concept note-3: -They can be fixed by adding more information. Remember, if you begin a sentence with a subordinating conjunction, it is considered introductory words and a comma is needed to separate the subordinate clause from the rest of the sentence.

Concept note-4: -A coordinating conjunction is a conjunction that connects words, phrases, and clauses that are coordinate, or equal to each other. There are seven coordinating conjunctions: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so. They can be remembered using the acronym FANBOYS.
