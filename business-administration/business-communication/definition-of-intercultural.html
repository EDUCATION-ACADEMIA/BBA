Definition of Intercultural

(A) ** Refers to business relationships with people from different cultures.

(B) The basic channels are written (hard copy print or digital formats), oral or spoken, and electronic and multimedia.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Intercultural communication refers to the communication between people from two different cultures. Intercultural communication is a symbolic, interpretive, transactional, contextual process in which people from different cultures create shared meanings.

Concept note-2: -Intercultural communication is a field of study that studies how people from different cultural backgrounds communicate, in similar and different ways, and how they try to communicate through the different cultures from which they come.

Concept note-3: -An intercultural couple is defined here as the union between two people of differe. nationalities, which may or may not include differences in race, ethnicity, religion. language. Over the years, many terms have been used to describe culturally diverse co.

Concept note-4: –ˈkəl-chə-variants or less commonly inter-cultural. : occurring between or involving two or more cultures (see culture entry 1 sense 5b) intercultural differences. an intercultural gathering.

Concept note-5: -Cross-cultural means comparing two or more different cultures; so cross-cultural communication examines the varying communication styles of different cultural groups.
