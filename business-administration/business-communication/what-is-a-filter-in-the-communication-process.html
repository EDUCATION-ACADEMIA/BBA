What is a filter in the communication process?

(A) A software program that changes the appearance of an image.

(B) ** Something that modifies, disrupts or distorts a message

(C) When the receiver repeats back your message.

(D) When the sender puts information into a format.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Feedback Loop. A communication process in which the receiver repeats back to the sender what he or she heard. Filter. Modifies, disrupts, or distorts a communication message. Interpersonal Communication.

Concept note-2: -A filter is something that can delete, distort, or generalize the message we’re trying to share. And filters are in place for both the sender and receiver in every single conversation.

Concept note-3: -In communication studies and information theory, noise refers to anything that interferes with the communication process between a speaker and an audience. It’s also called interference.

Concept note-4: -A disruption in the communication process is called noise.

Concept note-5: -Noise essentially is anything that distorts a message by interfering with the communication process. Noise can take many forms, including a radio playing in the background, another person trying to enter your conversation, and any other distractions that prevent the receiver from paying attention.
