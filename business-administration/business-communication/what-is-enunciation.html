What is enunciation?

(A) mumbling words

(B) freedom

(C) speaking quickly

(D) ** speaking clearly and emphasizing each syllable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -enunciation noun [C or U] (PRONOUNCE) the act of pronouncing words or parts of words clearly: Victims of stroke often lack clear enunciation. The participants were chosen based on their enunciation skills and vocabulary.

Concept note-2: -Put the knuckle of your thumb in your mouth and bite down gently. Then, using any reading material you have, read out loud and speak as clearly as you can. Do this for at least a minute a day and you’ll notice straight away after doing it how much clearer you sound.

Concept note-3: -A few examples include: Dropping “g” sounds: Saying things like “He’s goin’ to the store” instead of fully forming the “g” sound at the end of the word “going” Blending words improperly: Combining words in a manner that is not correct, such as saying “gonna” instead of “going to”

Concept note-4: -Proper enunciation is essential for the audience to have any idea of what the actor is saying or singing during a production. Enunciation is the act of pronouncing words. Good enunciation means your words are clear and distinct. Listeners can understand what you’re saying.
