What is COMMUNICATION?

(A) the process of exchanging information between or among individuals through a particular system.

(B) the process of exchanging information and meaning between or among individuals through a particular system.

(C) the process of exchanging and interpreting information between or among individuals through a particular system.

(D) the process of exchanging and interpreting meaning between or among individuals through a particular system.

(E) ** the process of exchanging and interpreting information and meaning between or among individuals through a particular system.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Communication. the process of exchanging and interpreting information and meaning between or among individuals through a system of symbols, signs, and behavior. Interference. a barrier to effective communication. organization communication.

Concept note-2: -Communication is the process of exchanging and interpreting information or meaning through a common system of signs, symbols, and behavior. For example, any time that you write, speak, listen, and converse, you are communicating meaning and information.

Concept note-3: -Communication is a process that involves sending and receiving messages through the verbal and non-verbal methods. Communication is a two-way means of communicating information in the form of thoughts, opinions, and ideas between two or more individuals with the purpose of building an understanding.

Concept note-4: -2) Peter Little defines communication as “the process by which information is transmitted between individuals and or organizations so that an understanding response results.”

Concept note-5: -Interpersonal communication is the process of exchange of information, ideas and feelings between two or more people through verbal or non-verbal methods. It often includes face-to-face exchange of information, in a form of voice, facial expressions, body language and gestures.
