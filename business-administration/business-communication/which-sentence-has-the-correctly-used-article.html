Which sentence has the correctly used article?

(A) That has been an union issue for years.

(B) ** I ate an orange.

(C) The bell rang once a hour.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Since the noun ‘orange’ is non-specific, an indefinite article ‘an’ is used.

Concept note-2: -Still, whether you refer to the color or the fruit, you will use the article “an” in front of the word orange. Is that an orange car? I am eating an orange. You can also use the definite article “the” in front of the word “orange, ” whether the noun or adjective form.

Concept note-3: -an orange is the correct answer. Because the word O in Orange is a vowel word so “ an” used for vowel first word.

Concept note-4: -The definite article is used before singular and plural nouns when the noun is specific or particular. The signals that the noun is definite, that it refers to a particular member of a group. For example: “The dog that bit me ran away."
