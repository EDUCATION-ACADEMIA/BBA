Oral communication is also known as

(A) non-verbal communication

(B) ** verbal communication

(C) face to face communication

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the literal sense, verbal communication is oral communication with words that you or others speak out loud. On the other hand, nonverbal communication is about whatâ€™s not said out loud: gestures, facial expressions, and body language.

Concept note-2: -Oral refers to things that go into or come out of the mouth, while verbal refers to spoken communication. Since spoken communication comes from the mouth and therefore is verbal, writers sometimes get these mixed up. The rule of thumb for these words is this: if you are writing about talking or speaking, use verbal.

Concept note-3: -Oral communication is also known as verbal communication.

Concept note-4: -Verbal communication refers to the use of words for communication purposes. It comprises both oral and written communication.

Concept note-5: -Oral communication is also known as impersonal communication.
