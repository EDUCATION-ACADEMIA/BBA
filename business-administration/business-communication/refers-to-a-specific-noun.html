EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The definite article (the) is used before a noun to indicate that the identity of the noun is known to the reader. The indefinite article (a, an) is used before a noun that is general or when its identity is not known.

Concept note-2: -A definite article is an article that indicates that a noun refers to a specific thing or to something that has been identified previously. In English, there is only one definite article: the word the.

Concept note-3: -Definite Article: the The signals that the noun is definite, that it refers to a particular member of a group. For example: “The dog that bit me ran away.” Here, we’re talking about a specific dog, the dog that bit me.

Concept note-4: -The difference between common and proper nouns is that common nouns refer to general things (like “a city” or “a mountain"), and proper nouns refer to specific, named things (like “Chicago” or “Mt.

Concept note-5: -In Spanish, you have to choose between four definite articles: el, la, los and las. Which one you choose depends on the noun which follows.In Spanish, all nouns (including words for things) are either masculine or feminine – this is called their gender and they can also be either singular or plural.
