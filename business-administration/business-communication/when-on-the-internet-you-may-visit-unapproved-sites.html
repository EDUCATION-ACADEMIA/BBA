When on the internet, you may visit unapproved sites.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These dangerous sites typically resemble legitimate websites, and your computer can be attacked by simply visiting a malicious website. You may be prompted to download software that your computer appears to need. A hazardous installation can compromise your machine, and your sensitive information as well.

Concept note-2: -Section 67 of the IT act makes it illegal to publish or transmit “obscene material” in electronic form. Obscene material according to the IT act is “lascivious or appeals to the prurient interest or if its effect is such as to tend to deprave and corrupt”.

Concept note-3: -A secure URL should begin with “https” rather than “http.” The “s” in “https” stands for secure, which indicates that the site is using a Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) Certificate.

Concept note-4: -Suspicious site: The site you want to visit seems suspicious and may not be safe. The site ahead contains harmful programs: The site you start to visit might try to trick you into installing programs that cause problems when you’re browsing online.
