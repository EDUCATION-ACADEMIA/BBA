To facilitate the understanding of a message, presentation slides should contain as many illustrations as possible.

(A) TRUE

(B) ** FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Key Takeaway Speeches are built by identifying the main points to be communicated and by following five structural elements (attention statement, introduction, body, conclusion, and residual message).

Concept note-2: -You will probably want to divide up the presentation into a number of sections according to the number of slides you consider appropriate. Allow a maximum of two to three minutes per slide – so for a 15 minute presentation, you should have no more than five to seven slides.

Concept note-3: -How do slide presentations help keep the presenter “on message"? The audience will be kept on message because the information is projected overhead. Slides use images that can centralize content. Since slides are sequential, they enable the presenter to always present the intended information.

Concept note-4: -One well-known formulation for PowerPoint presentations is the 10/20/30 rule. This rule dictates that you should use about ten slides for a twenty minute presentation, and each slide should utilize thirty point font. In other words, each slide should be about two minutes in length.
