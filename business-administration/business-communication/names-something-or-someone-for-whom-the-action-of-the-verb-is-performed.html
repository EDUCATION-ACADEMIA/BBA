Names something or someone for whom the action of the verb is performed

(A) clause

(B) gerund

(C) grammar

(D) ** indirect object

(E) verb

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A direct object is the noun that receives the action of the verb. The indirect object, however, is the noun that receives the direct object itself. Embiid passed Simmons the ball. In this sentence, passed is the verb and Embiid is the subject because he’s the one doing the passing.

Concept note-2: -An indirect object is an optional part of a sentence; it’s the recipient of an action. In the sentence “Jake gave me some cereal, ” the word “me” is the indirect object; I’m the person who got cereal from Jake.

Concept note-3: -Whom is objective case like the pronouns him, her, them, me, and us. Use whom as the direct object, indirect object, or object of a preposition in a sentence. Helpful tip: To see whether whom is the correct choice, substitute him for whom. If the sentence sounds correct, then whom is the correct choice.

Concept note-4: -An indirect object is an object that is used with a transitive verb to indicate who benefits from an action or gets something as a result. For example, in ‘She gave him her address. ‘, ‘him’ is the indirect object. Compare direct object.

Concept note-5: -Verbs that take indirect objects include bring, give, make, send, show, teach, tell, and write.
