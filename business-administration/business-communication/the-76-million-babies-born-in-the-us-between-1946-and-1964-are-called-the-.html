The 76 million babies born in the US between 1946 and 1964 are called the: ____

(A) Millennial generation

(B) ** Baby boom generation

(C) Generation X

(D) Generation Y

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There were actually a total of 76 million births in the United States from 1946 to 1964, the 19 years usually called the “baby boom.” Of the 76 million baby boomers born, nearly 11 million had died by 2012, leaving some 65.2 million survivors.

Concept note-2: -The term “baby boomer” is derived from the boom in births that took place after the return of soldiers from WWII.

Concept note-3: -According to Beresford Research, baby boomers are anyone born between 1946 and 1964. Baby boomers are the generation of people born after World War II when the birth rate spiked.

Concept note-4: -Generation Y, sometimes called the “Baby Boomer Echo” because they are the children of the Baby Boomers, represent 25 percent of the workforce.

Concept note-5: -The “silent generation” are those born from 1925 to 1945 – so called because they were raised during a period of war and economic depression. The “baby boomers” came next from 1945 to 1964, the result of an increase in births following the end of World War II.
