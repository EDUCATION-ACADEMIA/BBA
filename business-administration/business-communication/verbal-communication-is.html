Verbal communication is

(A) ** Written and oral communication

(B) Written communication

(C) Oral communication

(D) Communication via Mechanical devices

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Verbal communication is about language, both written and spoken. In general, verbal communication refers to our use of words while nonverbal communication refers to communication that occurs through means other than words, such as body language, gestures, and silence.

Concept note-2: -Verbal Communication Skills Definition In the literal sense, verbal communication is oral communication with words that you or others speak out loud. On the other hand, nonverbal communication is about whatâ€™s not said out loud: gestures, facial expressions, and body language.

Concept note-3: -The two major forms of verbal communication include written and oral communication. Written communication includes traditional pen and paper letters and documents, typed electronic documents, e-mails, text chats, SMS and anything else conveyed through written symbols such as language.

Concept note-4: -Oral communication is the ability to transmit ideas from your brain to either one person or a group of people. Good use of verbal skills means presenting an idea clearly while each thought is articulated in a cohesive manner. It has everything to do with the language that we choose to use.

Concept note-5: -Verbal communication is the exchange of information through the use of language and words. Verbal communication includes speaking, writing, sign language, or symbols. Oral communication is the exchange of information, ideas, and feelings between two or more people through word of mouth.
