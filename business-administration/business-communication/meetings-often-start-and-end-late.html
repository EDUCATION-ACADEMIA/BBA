Meetings often start and end late.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Just as important as starting on time is ending on time. A definitive end time will help ensure that you accomplish what’s on your agenda and get people back to their work promptly.

Concept note-2: -You start meetings late, period Attendees are more likely to be relaxed about arriving on time if the leaders are the people who started late to begin with. If people dutifully show up punctually only to have the meeting host waltz in 10 minutes late, then no one will take meeting start times seriously.

Concept note-3: -Best time of the day for meetings Early Morning (9 – 10 a.m.) Mid-Morning (10 a.m. – 12 p.m.) Lunchtime/Lunch break (12 – 1 p.m.)
