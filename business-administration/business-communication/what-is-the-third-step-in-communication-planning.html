What is the third step in communication planning?

(A) Outline the communication for delivery

(B) Determine the purpose of the message

(C) ** Clarify objectives

(D) Identify the audience

(E) Determine the best channel of communication

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The third step is to specify the context of your message. Now that you know the identity of your audience and communication goals, you can specify the details you want them to hear.

Concept note-2: -Page 10. Step 3: Identify Intended Audiences. Purpose: To identify all potential intended-sometimes called “target”-audience segments for each communication goal; to prioritize those audiences and learn as much as possible about them.

Concept note-3: -The three models of communication we will discuss are the transmission, interaction, and transaction models. Although these models of communication differ, they contain some common elements.

Concept note-4: -A communication plan is a strategic blueprint for delivering consistent, coordinated, and targeted messaging to achieve specified goals. We all know the importance of effective communication. Developing a communication plan is one way to help ensure your communication efforts are strategic, coordinated, and consistent.
