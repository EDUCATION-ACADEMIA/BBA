The writing process is ____

(A) Prewriting, Writing, and Printing

(B) Prewriting, revising and Printing

(C) ** Prewriting, writing, and Revising

(D) Prewriting, Printing and Revising

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Writing is a process that can be divided into three stages: Pre-writing, drafting and the final revising stage which includes editing and proofreading. In the first stage you research your topic and make preparatory work before you enter the drafting stage.

Concept note-2: -The general steps are: discovery, prewriting, drafting, revising, and editing.

Concept note-3: -Prewriting – in this stage, you plan what you are going to write. You choose your topic, identify your audience and purpose, brainstorm ideas, and organize your information.

Concept note-4: -Revision is often defined as the last stage in the writing process (prewriting, writing, and revision). Sommers (1982), on the other hand, sees revision as “a process of making changes throughout the writing of a draft, changes that work to make the draft congruent with a writer’s changing intentions."
