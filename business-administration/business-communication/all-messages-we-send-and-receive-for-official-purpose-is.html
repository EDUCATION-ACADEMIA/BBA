BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

BUSINESS COMMUNICATION

[SOURCES]
All messages we send and receive for official purpose is

(A) External Communication

(B) ** Business communication

(C) Feedback

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term business communication is used for all messages that we send and receive for official purpose like running a business, managing an organization, conducting the formal affairs of a voluntary organization and so on. Business communication is marked by formality as against personal and social communication.

Concept note-2: -Business messaging is a set of channels over which companies and consumers can communicate with each other. The most common messaging channel is SMS or text messaging. Weâ€™ve all had experiences with shipping notifications, marketing offers, promotional campaigns, and appointment reminders.

Concept note-3: -Effective business communication is how employees and management interact with each other to reach organizational goals and be more aligned with the core company values. Its main purpose is to improve organizational practices, eliminate silos, keep employees informed and reduce errors.

Concept note-4: -Assigning tasks to employees, receiving and responding to customer feedback, and publishing a press release are all examples of business communication.

Concept note-5: -Verbal communication encompasses all communication using spoken words, or unspoken words as in the case with sign language.
