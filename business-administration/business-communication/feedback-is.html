Feedback is

(A) Verbal cues

(B) Nonverbal cues

(C) ** Both verbal and nonverbal cues

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In two-way communication, the parties alternate the roles of receiver and sender, exchanging messages through assorted barriers. Feedback enables each party to confirm the other’s meaning and work toward understanding, while nonverbal communication helps lower the barriers to conveying and receiving meaning.

Concept note-2: -Direct feedback is a response that comes from the receiver. Direct feedback can be both verbal and non-verbal, and it may involve signs, symbols, words, or sounds.

Concept note-3: -Nonverbal communication uses body language, facial expressions, tone, and pauses in speech as the medium of communication. Verbal communication uses a single channel of communication, the human voice, which speaks a single word at a time.

Concept note-4: -Non-verbal communication is the collection of body language postures, gestures, etcetera. Feedback is a response that is received from the receiver side to the communicator and provides the idea to the communicator in what manner the message is received and how it will be modified.

Concept note-5: -Negative feedback – corrective comments about past behaviour. Focuses on behaviour that wasn’t successful and shouldn’t be repeated. Positive feedback – affirming comments about past behaviour.
