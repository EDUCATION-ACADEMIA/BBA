The following behaviour “Calling in sick in order to go to the beach” is

(A) legal and ethical

(B) Illegal yet ethical

(C) legal yet unethica

(D) ** illegal and unethical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Some common examples of unethical or illegal activity include: Conflicts of interest. Accounting or auditing irregularities. Theft.

Concept note-2: -Asking for recognition for someone else’s job, calling in sick to go to the hill station, sabotaging someone else’s work, and, in sales, falsifying the product or service to fulfill the target are all examples of unethical behavior in the workplace.

Concept note-3: -The ERC reported that employees most often observe the following five unethical behaviors in the workplace: 1) employees misusing company time, 2) supervisors abusing subordinates, 3) employees stealing from their employers, 4) employees lying to their employers, and 5) employees violating company internet policies.

Concept note-4: -Unethical behavior in the workforce is not restricted to overt acts. It includes workers acting out and aggressively confronting supervisors, supervisees, and colleagues, and it includes more subtle behaviors that compromise people, productivity, and organizations.
