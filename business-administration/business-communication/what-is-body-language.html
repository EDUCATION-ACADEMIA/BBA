What is Body Language?

(A) ** Postures, gestures, and facial expressions.

(B) The exchange of information through the use of words.

(C) Your words say one thing but your body language say another.

(D) Speak from your point of view.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Body language is a range of nonverbal signals that you can use to communicate your feelings and intentions. These include your posture, facial expressions, and hand gestures. Your ability to understand and interpret other people’s body language can help you to pick up on unspoken issues or feelings.

Concept note-2: -Gesture refers to a movement of a body part, especially a hand or the head whereas posture refers to the way in which your body is positioned when you are sitting or standing. This is the main difference between gesture and posture.

Concept note-3: -All people use one of four movements to communicate their body language: light and bouncy, soft and fluid, dynamic and determined, or precise and bold. Each of the movements corresponds to one of the four energy types and has a unique meaning.

Concept note-4: -5 of the most common body language signs There are five common body language signs you can use today. They are raised eyebrows, mirroring, head tilt, crossed arms, and fidgeting with objects. Let’s dive into these, so you can better read people and have more confident body language.

Concept note-5: -Body language is made up of facial expressions, gestures, posture, and other non-verbal cues that convey a lot of meaning, either consciously or subconsciously.
