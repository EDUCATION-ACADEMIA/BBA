You should not look the other person in the eyes because it can mean you are mad at them.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, when a person avoids eye contact, it means they don’t want to engage with what’s in front of them or that they want to engage with something else. This ‘desire for a lack of engagement’ with an object is the core reason behind almost all the reasons for avoiding eye contact.

Concept note-2: -Eye Contact and Social Anxiety Disorder Often, people with social anxiety disorder (SAD) describe looking someone in the eyes as anxiety-provoking and uncomfortable. This is likely due, in part, to genetic wiring. Research has shown that people diagnosed with SAD have a pronounced fear of direct eye contact.

Concept note-3: -If you avoid making eye contact with a person, you might be considered to be distracted or even insulting. Sometimes Americans might think you lack confidence or in some cases, that you could be untrustworthy. Therefore, please make good eye contact with your friends and show them your interest!

Concept note-4: -Tell the person exactly why you are upset. Use the word “I, ” not “you.” Say, “I felt hurt when you did .” Don’t say: “You did wrong.” Find out why. “I know you probably didn’t mean to hurt me.
