Identification with and understanding of another’s situation, feelings, and motives.

(A) ** Empathy

(B) Growth Mindset

(C) Self Confidence

(D) Hard Skills

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“Cognitive empathy, ” sometimes called “perspective taking, ” refers to our ability to identify and understand other people’s emotions.

Concept note-2: -Empathy is the ability or practice of imagining or trying to deeply understand what someone else is feeling or what it’s like to be in their situation. Empathy is often described as the ability to feel what others are feeling as if you are feeling it yourself.

Concept note-3: -Cognitive empathy, also known as empathic accuracy, involves “having more complete and accurate knowledge about the contents of another person’s mind, including how the person feels, ” Hodges and Myers say.

Concept note-4: -The three types of empathy that psychologists have defined are: Cognitive, Emotional, and Compassionate. As an aside, it’s worth noting that empathy is a relatively new idea and is still being defined by social and cognitive psychologists.
