Interpersonal communication is also known as Dyadic.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -True. Interpersonal communication occurs when two people form a dyad, also known as two people communicating with one another.

Concept note-2: -Interpersonal communication is also known as dyadic communication. Chatting with a friend or having a conference with a professor are examples.

Concept note-3: -“Dyadic communication” is bafflegab jargon that refers to a dialogue, or an exchange of information between two people. As such, it would be a specific subset of interpersonal communications. Interpersonal communication is the process of exchanging information between two people or among a larger group of people.

Concept note-4: -Interpersonal communication, also known as people communication, is the process of expressing your ideas and opinions to other people.

Concept note-5: -Dyadic and Interpersonal Communication Dyadic communication-described by social scientists-is the communication between two people. Interpersonal communication is a two-way interaction between people who are part of a close and irreplaceable relationship.
