A device to reduce the shock or pain of bad news is called a(n)

(A) explanation

(B) ** buffer

(C) adjustment

(D) deflector

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A buffer is a device to reduce shock or pain. To buffer the pain of bad news, begin with a neutral but meaningful statement that makes the reader continue reading. The buffer should be relevant and concise and provide a natural transition to the explanation that follows.

Concept note-2: -What is the purpose of a strategic buffer? to give readers a chance to prepare for bad news.

Concept note-3: -An explanation of the bad news is the most important part of a negative message because it prepares the reader for the refusal or denial – but it does NOT explicitly state the bad news yet.

Concept note-4: -The most important part of a bad-news letter is the section that explains why a negative decision is necessary. Without sound reasons for denying a request or refusing a claim, a letter will fail, no matter how cleverly it is organized or written.

Concept note-5: -Which of the following will help you set up your reasons for bad news effectively? Citing the expert opinion of authorities whom both you and your reader respect.
