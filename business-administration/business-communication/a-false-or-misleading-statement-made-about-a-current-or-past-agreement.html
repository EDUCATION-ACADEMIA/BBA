A false or misleading statement made about a current or past agreement.

(A) Fraud

(B) Copyright

(C) ** Misrepresentation

(D) Record Keeping

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A misrepresentation is a false statement of a material fact made by one party which affects the other partyâ€™s decision in agreeing to a contract. If the misrepresentation is discovered, the contract can be declared void. Depending on the situation, the adversely impacted party may seek damages.

Concept note-2: -In addition to the prohibition against misleading or deceptive conduct, it is unlawful for a business to make false or misleading claims about goods or services. A misrepresentation is a claim or statement that is false or misleading made by one party to another.

Concept note-3: -(1) The defendant made a false representation of a past or existing material fact susceptible of knowledge. (2) The defendant did so knowing the representation was false, or without knowing whether it was true or false. (3) The defendant intended to induce the plaintiff to act in reliance on that representation.
