The communication process is a set of steps in which a message is sent and received from one person to another.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The communication process is made up of four key components. Those components include encoding, medium of transmission, decoding, and feedback. There are also two other factors in the process, and those two factors are present in the form of the sender and the receiver.

Concept note-2: -Communication is the process of sending and receiving messages through verbal or nonverbal means, including speech, or oral communication; writing and graphical representations (such as infographics, maps, and charts); and signs, signals, and behavior.

Concept note-3: -Decoding: Decoding is the process by which the receiver interprets the message and translates it into meaningful information. Decoding involves two things: one is technically receiving the message as it has been sent, and the other is interpreting the message the way sender wants receiver to understand.
