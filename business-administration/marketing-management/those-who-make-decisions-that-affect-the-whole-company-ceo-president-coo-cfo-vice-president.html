BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

MARKETING MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
Those who make decisions that affect the whole company (CEO, president, COO, CFO, vice president).

(A) empowerment

(B) middle management

(C) ** top management

(D) supervisory-level management

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is the CEO’s responsibility to take all big decisions for the entire company and set goals along with the board of directors. The COO’s responsibilities vary greatly depending on the CEO and the needs of the organization. The COO’s responsibilities are in relation to the CEO and what they need.

Concept note-2: -The CEO is first in command, while the CFO and COO are second in command. The CEO is hired or appointed by the board of directors as the topmost position in the corporate hierarchy at any company. They report only to the board and no one else. The COO is second in command to the CEO and works very closely with them.

Concept note-3: -A CEO is the highest position in a company. Depending on the company’s organisational structure, a CEO may report to the board of directors. CEOs may also report to investors or the founders of a company. In contrast, a COO usually reports directly to the CEO.

Concept note-4: -What is a CEO vs. CFO? People outside the business world often get confused with the roles played by the CEO vs. CFO. The Chief Executive Officer (CEO) and the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) take on different but equally important responsibilities in an organization.

Concept note-5: -As the top executive at large companies, CEOs receive guidance from the board of directors as to the vision and goals of the organization. In the case of private companies, CEOs take direction from the owner(s) of the company.
