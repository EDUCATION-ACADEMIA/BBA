Service organizations rely heavily on traditional advertising such as billboards and televisioncommercials.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Service organizations rely heavily on traditional advertising such as billboards and television commercials. This is a branding strategy that occurs when a business creates multiple brand names to appeal to different market segments.

Concept note-2: -Advertising is the paid presentation and promotion of ideas, goods, or services by an identified sponsor in a mass medium. Examples include print ads, radio, television, billboard, direct mail, brochures and catalogs, signs, in-store displays, posters, mobile apps, motion pictures, web pages, banner ads, emails.

Concept note-3: -Industrial goods are bought and used for industrial and business use. Consumer goods are ready for the consumption and satisfaction of human wants. While industrial goods are made up of machinery, plants, and raw materials, consumer goods are commodities purchased by a buyer like clothing, food, and drinks.

Concept note-4: -Brand advertising helps companies generate awareness, expand reach, and make their brand name more recognizable to customers. One way it does that is by showcasing their brand story.

Concept note-5: -A brand is a name, term, design, symbol or any other feature that identifies one sellerâ€™s goods or service as distinct from those of other sellers.
