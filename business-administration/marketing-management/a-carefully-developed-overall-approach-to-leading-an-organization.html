A carefully developed overall approach to leading an organization:

(A) ** management strategy

(B) management science

(C) Industrial Revolution

(D) business competition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A management strategy is a carefully developed overall approach to leading an organization.

Concept note-2: -Middle managers spend most of their time leading and monitoring results of their work team. All managers perform some of the same activities. The manager who knows how to plan, organize, staff, lead, and control is ready to make the decisions needed to operate a business successfully.

Concept note-3: -There are three broad categories of management styles: Autocratic, democratic and laissez-faire.

Concept note-4: -Lean Six Sigma seeks to improve employee and company performance by eliminating the waste of resources and process/product defects.
