Foreign goods exported in the same state as previously imported, to the rest of the world.

(A) Exports

(B) ** Re exports

(C) Illegal

(D) Immoral

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Re-export means foreign goods exported in the same state as previously imported, from the free circulation area, premises for inward processing or industrial free zones, directly to the rest of the world and from premises for customs warehousing or commercial-free zones, to the rest of the world.

Concept note-2: -When the goods are imported from one country with the purpose of exporting it to another country, then such type of trade is called entrepot trade.

Concept note-3: -Re-exportation, also called entrepot trade, is a form of international trade in which a country exports goods which it previously imported without altering them.

Concept note-4: -Re-export is sending back goods imported for specific purposes like jobbing, execution of a contract, servicing/repairing of machineries, display in fair/exhibition etc. It also happens when indigenously manufactured goods were returned back after export and re-imported for repairing/reprocessing etc.

Concept note-5: -Balance of trade vs. The difference between exports and imports is called the balance of trade.
