According to the Federal Trade Commission, a “new product” must:

(A) ** be entirely new or changed in an important and noticeable way.

(B) not have a direct competitor with a similar product for sale.

(C) not use the term “new” for more than 90 days.

(D) change the product’s packaging to meet FTC guidelines for the use of the word “new".

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The short answer according to longstanding FTC guidance is 6 months from launch, with a little extra time built in if you do a limited test launch first. This rule has been on the books a long time but not actively enforced.

Concept note-2: -Launch: The process by which a new product is introduced into the market for initial sale. Commercialization: The process of taking a new product from development to market.

Concept note-3: -According to Entrepreneur, there are several critical factors to consider when marketing a new product, including the competition, the ideal customer, the unique selling proposition (USP), testing, media campaigns and understanding the life cycle of the product.

Concept note-4: -This is used when an existing product is updated or changed or a brand-new product is introduced that would cause a target consumer to choose the changed or new product over one they had routinely chosen in the past.
