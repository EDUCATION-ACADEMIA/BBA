An organization directs its marketing efforts at two or more segments by developing a marketing mix for each segment. Identify the target marketing strategy adopted here?

(A) ** Multi-segment approach

(B) Concentration approach

(C) Total market approach

(D) Differentiated market approach

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A differentiated marketing strategy is one in which the company decides to provide separate offerings to each different market segment that it targets. It is also called multisegment marketing. Each segment is targeted in a particular way, as the company provides unique benefits to different segments.

Concept note-2: -Single-Market Strategy Examples Companies using a single-market strategy focus on just one segment within the market. The segment can be defined geographically or demographically. For instance, a local brewpub would be an example of a brewer targeting a single geographically defined market segment.

Concept note-3: -Undifferentiated Approach (Total Market Approach) Single Marketing Mix for the entire market. All consumers have similar needs for a specific kind of product.

Concept note-4: -The marketing mix in marketing strategy: Product, price, place and promotion. The marketing mix is the set of controllable, tactical marketing tools that a company uses to produce a desired response from its target market. It consists of everything that a company can do to influence demand for its product.
