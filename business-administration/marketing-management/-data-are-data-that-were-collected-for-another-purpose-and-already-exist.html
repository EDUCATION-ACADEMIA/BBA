[SOURCES]
.... data are data that were collected for another purpose and already exist.

(A) ** Secondary

(B) Primary

(C) Primitive

(D) Cross-sectional

(E) Ordinate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Secondary data is data that has already been collected by someone else, or data you have already collected for another purpose. Collecting primary data is more time consuming, work intensive, and expensive than collecting secondary data.

Concept note-2: -Secondary data refers to data that is collected by someone other than the primary user. Common sources of secondary data for social science include censuses, information collected by government departments, organizational records and data that was originally collected for other research purposes.

Concept note-3: -Four common types of market research techniques include surveys, interviews, focus groups, and customer observation.
