MARKETING MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
An informational tag, wrapper or printed message that is attached to a product or package

(A) Packaging

(B) ** label

(C) FDA

(D) UPC

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A label * is an information tag, wrapper, seal, or imprinted message that is attached to a product or its package. Its main function is to inform customers about the product’s contents and give directions for its use.

Concept note-2: -There are four major types of labels that companies and small businesses are using for their products and operations: brand labels, informative labels, descriptive labels, and grade labels.

Concept note-3: -Generally speaking, labels for food products must advise consumers of the product ingredients (including potential allergens), the “best before” or “use by” date, country of origin, and nutrition information. The address of the manufacturer or distributor is also required.

Concept note-4: -The primary function of packaging is to ensure the product is safe to transport, store and sell, but labeling provides necessary information about the product using printed text, logos, artwork or other designs.

Concept note-5: -They come in an unlimited array of shapes and sizes but typically feature a brand name, product name, product description, ingredient list, directions for use, and more. They’re different from point-of-sale barcode labels and ecommerce shipping labels for which a dedicated thermal label printer is sufficient.
