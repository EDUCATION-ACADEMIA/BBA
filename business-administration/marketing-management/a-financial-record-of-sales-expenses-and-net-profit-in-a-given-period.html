A financial record of sales expenses and net profit in a given period

(A) mission statement

(B) budget

(C) ** income statement

(D) financial statement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The income statement shows a company’s expense, income, gains, and losses, which can be put into a mathematical equation to arrive at the net profit or loss for that time period.

Concept note-2: -The income statement focuses on the revenue, expenses, gains, and losses of a company during a particular period. An income statement provides valuable insights into a company’s operations, the efficiency of its management, underperforming sectors, and its performance relative to industry peers.

Concept note-3: -An income statement shows a company’s revenues, expenses and profitability over a period of time. It is also sometimes called a profit-and-loss (P&L) statement or an earnings statement. It shows your: revenue from selling products or services.

Concept note-4: -An income statement is a report that shows how much revenue a company earned over a specific time period (usually for a year or some portion of a year). An income statement also shows the costs and expenses associated with earning that revenue.
