MARKETING MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
Suppliers are other business organizations and individuals who provide the organization with raw materials, parts, components, supplies, or services required to produce and supply products to customers.

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is a Supplier in a Business? A supplier is a person or business that provides a product or service to another entity. The role of a supplier in a business is to provide high-quality products from a manufacturer at a good price to a distributor or retailer for resale.

Concept note-2: -The supply chain is itself defined as: “…a channel of goods distribution, which starts with the supplier of raw materials or components, moves through an operational process to the distributor and retailer, and finally to the consumer.”

Concept note-3: -A vendor, also known as a supplier, is a person or a business entity that sells something.

Concept note-4: -The upstream portion of the supply chain includes the organization’s suppliers and the processes for managing relationships with them. The downstream portion consists of the organizations and processes for distributing and delivering products to the final customers.
