Putting out “Wet Floor” signs is one way to handle risk and is known as which of the following?

(A) Avoid

(B) Shift

(C) Retain

(D) ** Reduce

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Wet floor signs alert and remind people of the presence of a slippery substance on the walking surface. This could be due to cleaning or many other reasons, including accidental spills, product leaks and bad weather. Wet floor signs, like any other safety sign, are incredibly important to use in all workplaces.

Concept note-2: -Signs should be placed on both sides of a wet floor/spill, with a triangle of signs around the hazard area being ideal. If the wet floor is near a blind corner, staff should place a sign on the other side of the turn, as it will warn customers and employees of the hazard.

Concept note-3: -Wet, slippery floors are one of, if not the most, common causes of slip and fall incidents in facilities. When people slip and fall, they can seriously hurt themselves and sustain injuries like sprains and broken bones.
