Define Promotion

(A) ** Any from of communication a business or organization uses to inform, persuade, or remind.

(B) The number of different product lines a manufactures or sells

(C) The process of creating, growing, and manufacturing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Promotion is any communication used to inform, persuade, and/or remind people about an organization’s or individual’s goods, services, image, ideas, community involvement, or impact on society.

Concept note-2: -Promotion is any form of communication a business uses to inform, persuade, or remind people about its products and its image. Public relations fosters a favorable image about a business, its products, or its policies. Publicity tries to place positive information in the media.

Concept note-3: -In business, promotion is any communication that attempts to influence people to buy products or services. Businesses generally promote their brand, products, and services by identifying a target audience and finding ways to bring their message to that audience.

Concept note-4: -Promotion refers to the various types of communication that marketers use to inform, persuade, or remind customers about their products.

Concept note-5: -Promotion is the element in an organisation’s marketing mix that serves to inform, persuade and remind the market of a product and/or the organisation selling it, in hopes of influencing the recipients’ feelings, beliefs or behaviour.
