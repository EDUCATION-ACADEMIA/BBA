MARKETING MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
The packaging of bread you purchase from a local bakery is an example of:

(A) ** primary packaging

(B) Secondary packaging

(C) Transportation Packaging

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A major portion of the total packaging material consumption in bakeries goes to bread bags. Packaging bread in low density polyethylene plastic (LDPE) bags is an almost universal practice.

Concept note-2: -The recommended packaging materials are preferably heat-sealable waxed paper with high water-vapour permeability, semi moisture-proof cellophane or 15 gauge HDPE etc. Apart from waxed paper, plastic films used to pack them are LDPE and Polypropylene (PP).

Concept note-3: -What Are Some Examples Of Primary Packaging Materials? Primary packaging materials include glass, shrink film, flexible pouches, aluminum cans, and various other packaging materials.

Concept note-4: -Low-density polyethylene (LDPE) It is most commonly found in plastic bags but is also widely used for squeezable food bottles, bread and frozen food packaging, and flexible lids.
