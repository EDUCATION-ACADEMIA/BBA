This is the legal protection of the words or symbols for use by a company.

(A) ** trademark

(B) brand character

(C) patent

(D) brand invention

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Copyright is the exclusive right that the owner of an intellectual property has. It protects the creator’s work from unauthorized duplication or use.

Concept note-2: -(1) A trademark shall protect a sign which may be represented graphically and which is capable of distinguishing the goods or services of one undertaking from those of other undertakings.

Concept note-3: -TM SYMBOLS Principally, there are three symbols that are most commonly associated with signifying trademark rights: Circle R (®), TM, and SM. Depending on the nature of the goods/services sold and the status of the trademark application with the USPTO, one or more of these marks may be appropriate.

Concept note-4: -The Act gives protection to goods or services represented through such marks and assigning such marks in its actual sense is called trademark making. Trademark Marking is a commonly used practice to assign a status symbol to signify a word, logo, slogan, etc. as a Trademark.

Concept note-5: -A trademark is a symbol, word, or words legally registered or established by use as representing a company, product or service.
