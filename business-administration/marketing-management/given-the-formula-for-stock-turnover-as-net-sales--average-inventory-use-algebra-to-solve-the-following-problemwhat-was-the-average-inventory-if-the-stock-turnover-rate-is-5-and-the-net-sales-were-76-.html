Given the formula for stock turnover as Net Sales / Average inventory. Use algebra to solve the following problem:What was the average inventory if the stock turnover rate is 5 and the net sales were $76, 850?

(A) ** 15370

(B) 14370

(C) 13370

(D) 16370

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In this example, inventory turnover ratio = 1 / (73/365) = 5. This means the company can sell and replace its stock of goods five times a year.

Concept note-2: -Inventory turnover ratio = Cost of goods sold * 2 / (Beginning inventory + Final inventory) The inventory turnover ratio is a measure of how many times your average inventory is “turned” or sold in a certain period of time.

Concept note-3: -The stock turnover ratio formula is the cost of goods sold divided by average inventory.

Concept note-4: -The average inventory formula is: Average inventory = (Beginning inventory + Ending inventory) / 2.
