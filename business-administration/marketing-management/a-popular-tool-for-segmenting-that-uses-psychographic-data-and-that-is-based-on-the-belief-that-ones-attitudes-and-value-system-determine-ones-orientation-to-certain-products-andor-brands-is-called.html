MARKETING MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
A popular tool for segmenting that uses psychographic data, and that is based on the belief that one’s attitudes and value system determine one’s orientation to certain products and/or brands is called

(A) Experian.

(B) ** VALS.

(C) Mals.

(D) Prizm.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -VALS (Values and Lifestyle Survey) is a proprietary research methodology used for psychographic market segmentation. Market segmentation is designed to guide companies in tailoring their products and services in order to appeal to the people most likely to purchase them.

Concept note-2: -Psychographic segmentation is used by marketers to group people by their values, attitudes, personality, and lifestyle. The marketing mix refers to a group of segments the company wishes to appeal to, design products for, and tailor its marketing activities toward.

Concept note-3: -Psychographic segmentation divides buyers into different groups based on social class, lifestyle, or personality traits. Behavioral segmentation divides buyers into groups based on their knowledge, attitudes, uses, or responses to a product.

Concept note-4: -Demographic, psychographic, behavioral and geographic segmentation are considered the four main types of market segmentation, but there are also many other strategies you can use, including numerous variations on the four main types.

Concept note-5: -There are five psychographic segmentation variables on the basis of which homogeneous segments can be prepared for proper research – Personality, Lifestyle, Social Status, AIO (Activities, Interests, Opinions), and Attitudes.
