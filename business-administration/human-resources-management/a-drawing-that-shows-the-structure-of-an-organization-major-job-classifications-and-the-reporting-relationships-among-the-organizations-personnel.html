A drawing that shows the structure of an organization, major job classifications, and the reporting relationships among the organization’s personnel.

(A) Unity of Command

(B) ** Organization Chart

(C) Employee Empowerment

(D) Accountability

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An organizational chart is a diagram that visually conveys a company’s internal structure by detailing the roles, responsibilities, and relationships between individuals within an entity. It is one way to visualize a bureaucracy.

Concept note-2: -An organizational chart shows the internal structure of an organization or company. The employees and positions are represented by boxes or other shapes, sometimes including photos, contact information, email and page links, icons and illustrations. Straight or elbowed lines link the levels together.

Concept note-3: -The four types of organizational structures are functional, multi-divisional, flat, and matrix structures.

Concept note-4: -The organizational charts shall indicate reporting relationships. A solid line connecting a specific position or unit to another higher level position or unit typically indicates both administrative and functional reporting to the same higher level individual or position.
