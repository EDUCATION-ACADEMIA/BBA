Room that cannot be sold and currently not been assigned to any guest is known as

(A) ** Vacant/Out of order

(B) Occupied/Out of order

(C) Vacant/Dirty

(D) Occupied/Dirty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -VD-Vacant and Dirty-Room is Vacant and Dirty. VR-Vacant and Ready-Room is Vacant and Ready for Check-in. OR-Occupied and Ready. OC-Occupied and Clean-Room is Occupied and Cleaned by the Housekeeping. OD-Occupied and Dirty-Room is Occupied and yet to be cleaned by the housekeeping.

Concept note-2: -Out of Order means that the room is not for sale because it needs repairs. The Out-Of-Service status is used if you want to block a floor or wing in the hotel due to low season, cleaning, etc. The out-of-service rooms stay in the availability, of course, because an Out of Service room can be sold if the need arises.

Concept note-3: -Due out: The room is expected to become vacant after the following dayâ€™s checkout time. Do Not Paid: The guest is going to check out from the hotel today. Checkout: The guest has settled his or her account, returned the room keys, and left the hotel.

Concept note-4: -Out-of-order. The room cannot be assigned to a guest. A room may be out-of-order for a variety of reasons, including the need for maintenance, refurbishing, and extensive cleaning.
