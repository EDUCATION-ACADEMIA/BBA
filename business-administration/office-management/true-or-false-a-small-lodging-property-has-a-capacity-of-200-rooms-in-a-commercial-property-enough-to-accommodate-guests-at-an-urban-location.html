True or False? A Small Lodging property has a capacity of 200 rooms in a commercial property. Enough to accommodate guests at an Urban Location.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An inn is usually much smaller than a standard or chain hotel. Often individually or family-owned, inns tend to offer a cozy, homey feel for their guests. Typically offering fewer in-room amenities than a larger hotel chain, you may find in-room coffee, Wi-Fi, or small breakfast options available at an inn.

Concept note-2: -Lodging accommodations fall into 3 distinct categories: hotels, vacation rentals, or outdoor lodging.

Concept note-3: -Bed and Breakfast – A Bed and Breakfast (B&B) is an intimate, independently run lodging establishment, where breakfast is included in the room rate. Inn – A small establishment offering overnight accommodation, food, and drink to travelers.
