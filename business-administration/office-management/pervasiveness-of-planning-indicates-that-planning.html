Pervasiveness of planning indicates that planning

(A) ** extends through out the organisation

(B) is a first element of management process.

(C) is a top management function

(D) is future-oriended activity.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ans. False; there is uncertainty in business environment. 1. Pervasiveness of planning indicates that planning (a) is a top management function.

Concept note-2: -Planning is called a pervasive function of management because planning occurs at all levels of management in a business organisation directing all managers in different business units to work towards achieving the business goals and objectives.

Concept note-3: -Answer» B. extends throughout the organisation.

Concept note-4: -Planning is Pervasive: For example, top managers plan for the organisation as a whole, while middle level managers propose departmental plans and lower level manager formulate day-to-day operational planes.

Concept note-5: -Management is considered pervasive as it is required in all types of organizations whether it is profit-making, non-profit making, business or non-business, at all levels whether it is top-level management or middle-level management, or lower-level management. Management is considered a universal phenomenon.
