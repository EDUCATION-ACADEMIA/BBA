Unstructured decisions require judgement, evaluation, and insight to solve the problem.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Unstructured decisions are those in which the decision maker must provide judgment, evaluation, and insights into the problem definition. Structured decisions, by contrast, are repetitive and routine, and decision makers can follow a definite procedure for handling them to be efficient.

Concept note-2: -What are Unstructured Problems? Unstructured problems are novel and infrequent in nature. These types of problems may be difficult to recognize upon initial occurrence. Further, they may require specific analysis and research to fully understand.

Concept note-3: -Unstructured decisions: These decisions require judgment, evaluation, and insight to solve the problem. Unstructured means “decision processes that have not been encountered in quite the same form and for which no predetermined and explicit set of ordered responses exists in the organization”.

Concept note-4: -Structured problems are the straight forward problems which can be determined and solved by repeating examination and testing on the problems. Unstructured problems are the difficulties introduced to an organization when facing unusual situation and their solution are different and sometimes unique.
