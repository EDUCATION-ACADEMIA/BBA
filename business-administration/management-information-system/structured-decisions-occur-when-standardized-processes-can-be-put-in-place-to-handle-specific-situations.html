Structured decisions occur when standardized processes can be put in place to handle specific situations.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Structured decision making is an approach for careful and organized analysis of natural resource management decisions. Based in decision theory and risk analysis, SDM encompasses a simple set of concepts and helpful steps, rather than a rigidly-prescribed approach for problem solving.

Concept note-2: -Process standardization refers to the organization of different tasks, approaches, and administrative steps in a business, as well as their formalization and documentation. A process consists of input, transformation steps, and output (delivery).

Concept note-3: -The standardization process “cleans” or transforms member data into a format that makes attribute comparison easier. Normally, standardization of data means capitalization of all alphabetic characters, removal of punctuation, anonymous value checks, and ordering and validating data.

Concept note-4: -Answer» B. semi-structured decision.
