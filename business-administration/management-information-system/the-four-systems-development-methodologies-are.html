The four systems development methodologies are:

(A) ** SDLC, RAD, Agile, Lean

(B) SDLC, RAD, Iterative, and Lean

(C) SDLC, BAD, Agile, and Lean

(D) SDLC, RAD, Iterative, and Phased

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition. The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a structured process that enables the production of high-quality, low-cost software, in the shortest possible production time. The goal of the SDLC is to produce superior software that meets and exceeds all customer expectations and demands.

Concept note-2: -It is a methodology for systematically organizing the best ways to develop systems efficiently. It includes, for example, descriptions of work to be performed at each stage of the development process and drafted documents. Multiple methodologies-which differ according to viewpoint-are available.
