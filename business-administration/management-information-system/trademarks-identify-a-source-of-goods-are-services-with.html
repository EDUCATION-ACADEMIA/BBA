Trademarks identify a source of goods are services with:

(A) Words, Phrases, Logos, Shapes, or Surnames

(B) Words, Phrases, Geographic terms, Shapes, or Slogans

(C) ** Words, Phrases, Logos, Shapes, or Slogans

(D) Words, Phrases, Logos, Ornamental, or Slogans

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A trademark is a distinguishable and recognizable sign, design, or expression that identifies specific products or services of a particular source as distinct from others. A logo is a graphic sign, mark, or symbol used to identify a particular source. It is fair to say that a logo is a type of trademark.

Concept note-2: -Trademark registration can be obtained for slogans if it is distinctive and is used to represent goods or services from a unique source. Slogans are different from logos or wordmark, in that slogans consists of one or more words.

Concept note-3: -A distinctive shape of goods or its packaging or any three dimensional object capable of being represented graphically, can be a trademarked in India. A famous case involving shape trademark is the fight between Nestle and Cadbury.
