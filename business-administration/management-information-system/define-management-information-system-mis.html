Define Management Information System (MIS)

(A) *Identifying the system that is needed for effective conclusion in organizations

(B) *Identifying the management that is needed for efficient decision making in organizations

(C) ** Identifying the information that is needed for effective decision making in organizations

(D) *Identifying the information that is needed for user acceptance in organizations

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Abstract. Management Information System is flow-processing procedures based on computer data, and integrated with other procedures in order to provide information in a timely and effective manner to support decision-making and other management functions.

Concept note-2: -MIS is an organization – wide effort to provide decision making process information. The system is a formal commitment by executive to make the computer available to all managers. MIS sets the stage for accomplishments in the other area, which is DSS, the virtual office and knowledge based systems.

Concept note-3: -MIS stands for management information systems, which is the department that oversees hardware and software solutions dedicated to making critical business decisions. These information systems are designed to gather and analyze data and produce business reports that help management with critical decision-making.

Concept note-4: -MIS provides accurate and timely information necessary to facilitate the decision-making process and enable the organizations planning, control, and operational functions to be carried out effectively.
