Financial analysis becomes significant because it:

(A) Ignores price level changes

(B) ** Measures the efficiency of business

(C) Lacks qualitative analysis

(D) Is effected by personal bias

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Financial analysis is significant because it facilitates intra firm comparison. Explanation: Intra-firm comparison is a strength of the financial statement since it allows you to compare the company’s financial status not only to previous years, but also to competitors’.

Concept note-2: -The goal of financial analysis is to analyze whether an entity is stable, solvent, liquid, or profitable enough to warrant a monetary investment. It is used to evaluate economic trends, set financial policy, build long-term plans for business activity, and identify projects or companies for investment.

Concept note-3: -Financial planning and analysis (FP&A) is a process that helps companies plan and analyze their finances, and it is an essential part of business management. Companies typically use FP&A to determine how much money they need to spend on operations and how much revenue they will generate in the coming months.

Concept note-4: -Performing a financial analysis involves evaluating projects, budgets, and other finance-related entities within a business or asset. It allows you to understand and examine the business’s performance and make strategic decisions about your company’s future growth and opportunities.

Concept note-5: -Financial statements provide a snapshot of a corporation’s financial health, giving insight into its performance, operations, and cash flow. Financial statements are essential since they provide information about a company’s revenue, expenses, profitability, and debt.
