BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
Increase in current assets or decrease in current liabilities increases the current ratio

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An equal increase in both current assets and current liabilities generally does not effect current ratio.

Concept note-2: -Since there is no effect on current liabilities and an increase in current assets, there would be an increase in the current ratio.

Concept note-3: -A decrease in the current assets and increase in the current liabilities will bring a fall in the ratio. Changes in the value of current assets and current liabilities leading to a decrease in the former and an increase in the latter will definitely change the current ratio.

Concept note-4: -Repaying or restructuring debt will raise the current ratio. Explore whether you can reamortize existing term loans and change how the lender charges you interest, effectively delaying debt payments so they drop off your current ratio. Negotiate longer payment cycles whenever possible.

Concept note-5: -If a transaction increases current assets and current liabilities by the same amount, there would be no change in working capital. For example, if a company received cash from short-term debt to be paid in 60 days, there would be an increase in the cash flow statement.
