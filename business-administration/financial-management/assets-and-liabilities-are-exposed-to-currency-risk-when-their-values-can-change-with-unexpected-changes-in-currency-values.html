Assets and liabilities are exposed to currency risk when their values can change with unexpected changes in currency values.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Currency risk, or exchange rate risk, refers to the exposure faced by investors or companies that operate across different countries, in regard to unpredictable gains or losses due to changes in the value of one currency in relation to another currency.

Concept note-2: -What Is Currency Risk? Currency risk, commonly referred to as exchange-rate risk, arises from the change in price of one currency in relation to another. Investors or companies that have assets or business operations across national borders are exposed to currency risk that may create unpredictable profits and losses.

Concept note-3: -Asset exposure refers to the possible risk of a change in the value of an asset held by an entity due to a change in foreign exchange rates. This is an inherent risk that can not be avoided by an entity that indulges in foreign currency transactions.
