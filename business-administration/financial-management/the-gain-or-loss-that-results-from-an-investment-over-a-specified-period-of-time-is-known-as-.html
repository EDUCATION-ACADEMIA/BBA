The gain or loss that results from an investment over a specified period of time is known as ____

(A) risk

(B) ** return

(C) uncertainty

(D) expected value

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A rate of return (RoR) is the net gain or loss of an investment over a specified time period, expressed as a percentage of the investment’s initial cost.1 When calculating the rate of return, you are determining the percentage change from the beginning of the period until the end.

Concept note-2: -return. The gain or loss that results from an investment over a specified period of time is known as the return on the investment. Return on investment is a measure of investment efficiency, as well as the basis of comparison between two or more investment alternatives.

Concept note-3: -In simple terms, the difference between the selling price and cost/purchase price of an investment can be described as capital gain/loss. If the selling price is higher than cost price, it results in a capital gain and when the selling price is lower than the cost price, it leads to capital loss.

Concept note-4: -ROI stands for Return on Investment and is a measure of how much money is earned relative to the amount of money spent on an investment. It is usually expressed as a percentage and calculated by dividing the net profit from an investment by the cost of the investment.

Concept note-5: -ROI measures the return of an investment relative to the cost of the investment.
