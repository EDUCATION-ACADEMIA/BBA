If you borrow $100, 000 at an annual rate of 8.00% for a 10-year period and repay the total amount of principal and interest due of $215, 892.50 at the end of 10 years, what type of loan did you have?

(A) ** Discount loan

(B) Interest-only loan

(C) Discount Decline loan

(D) Discount and Compound loan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Example 1: A $10, 000 loan with a 5-year term at 13% Annual Percentage Rate (APR) would be repayable in 60 monthly installments of $228 each.

Concept note-2: -Divide your interest rate by the number of payments you’ll make that year. If you have a 6 percent interest rate and you make monthly payments, you would divide 0.06 by 12 to get 0.005. Multiply that number by your remaining loan balance to find out how much you’ll pay in interest that month.

Concept note-3: -The formula and calculations are as follows: Effective annual interest rate = (1 + (nominal rate ÷ number of compounding periods)) ^ (number of compounding periods)-1. For investment A, this would be: 10.47% = (1 + (10% ÷ 12)) ^ 12-1.

Concept note-4: -The principal–the money that you borrow. The interest–this is like paying rent on the money you borrow.
