Net Working Capital =

(A) ** Current Assets ____ Current Liabilities

(B) All Assets ____ All Liabilities

(C) Current Liabilities ____ Current Assets

(D) All Liabilities ____ All Asset

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Simply put, Net Working Capital (NWC) is the difference between a company’s current assets and current liabilities on its balance sheet. It is a measure of a company’s liquidity and its ability to meet short-term obligations, as well as fund operations of the business.

Concept note-2: -Working capital is calculated by taking a company’s current assets and deducting current liabilities. For instance, if a company has current assets of $100, 000 and current liabilities of $80, 000, then its working capital would be $20, 000.

Concept note-3: -Working capital is calculated as current assets minus current liabilities, as detailed on the balance sheet.
