What is the future value of your money?

(A) ** the amount of money you will have at a specified date in the future

(B) the number of investments you will make at a specified date in the future

(C) the items you will buy with your money at a specified date in the future

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The time value of money (TVM) is the concept that a sum of money is worth more now than the same sum will be at a future date due to its earnings potential in the interim. The time value of money is a core principle of finance.

Concept note-2: -Future Value (FV) = PV × (1 + r) ^ n Where: PV = Present Value. r = Interest Rate (%) n = Number of Compounding Periods.

Concept note-3: -Future Value is the value at some future time of a present amount of money, or a series of payments, evaluated at a given interest rate.

Concept note-4: -What is the future value of $1, 000 after five years at 8% per year? If compounding monthly, $1, 489.85 is the total compound interest value after five years.

Concept note-5: -The future sum of money is $ 6, 357.38 Substituting the values we have: r (rate) = 9.50% / 2 = 0.0475.
