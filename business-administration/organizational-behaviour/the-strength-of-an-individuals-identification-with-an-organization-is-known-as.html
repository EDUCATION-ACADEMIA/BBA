The strength of an individual’s identification with an organization is known as:

(A) normative commitment

(B) ** organizational commitment

(C) continuance commitment

(D) behavioral commitment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -organizational commitments. the strength of an individual’s identification with an organization. affective commitment. organizational commitment based on an individual’s desire to remain in an organization.

Concept note-2: -Commitment was most commonly defined in the organization behavior literature as “the relative strength of an individual’s identification with and involvement in a particular organization” (Mowday et al., 1982, p.

Concept note-3: -Organizational commitment refers to the level of engagement and dedication team members feel toward their individual jobs and the organization. It also describes the different reasons professionals remain with an employer rather than seek opportunities elsewhere.

Concept note-4: -Affective commitment (AC) refers to “an emotional attachment to, identification with, and involvement in the organization” [7 p67].
