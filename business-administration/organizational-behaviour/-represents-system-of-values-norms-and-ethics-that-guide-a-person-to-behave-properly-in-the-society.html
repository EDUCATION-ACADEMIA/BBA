[SOURCES]
____ represents system of values, norms and ethics that guide a person to behave properly in the society

(A) Id

(B) Ego

(C) ** Super Ego

(D) Super Id

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The superego is the ethical component of the personality and provides the moral standards by which the ego operates. The superego’s criticisms, prohibitions, and inhibitions form a person’s conscience, and its positive aspirations and ideals represent one’s idealized self-image, or “ego ideal.”

Concept note-2: -Freud’s personality theory (1923) saw the psyche structured into three parts (i.e., tripartite), the id, ego and superego, all developing at different stages in our lives.

Concept note-3: -The superego is part of the psyche that is partly conscious. It represents the rules of society, functions to reward and punish through a system of moral attitudes, and has a sense of guilt.

Concept note-4: -The three ego states are called-Parent, Adult and Child ego states. These three ego states comprise individual personality. Each ego state is an entire system of thoughts, feelings, and behaviours from which we interact with one another. Understanding our ego states helps us to understand Who am I?

Concept note-5: -The ego operates based on the reality principle, which strives to satisfy the id’s desires in realistic and socially appropriate ways. The reality principle weighs the costs and benefits of an action before deciding to act upon or abandon impulses.
