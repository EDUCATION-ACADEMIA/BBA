In social learning, the family, peer group, religious organizations, and culture shape an individual’s attitudes in a(n):

(A) direct manner

(B) ** indirect manner

(C) reciprocal manner

(D) parallel manner

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Social Learning: the process of deriving attitudes from family, peer groups, religious organizations, and culture.

Concept note-2: -Acting in ways consistent with one’s personal values and the commonly held values of the organization and society is called: a. emotional behavior.

Concept note-3: -An attitude has been defined as “a psychological tendency that is expressed by evaluating a particular entity with some degree of favor or disfavor.” Attitudes are, thus, a person’s evaluation of something else. Attitudes have three components: Cognitive, affective, and behavioral intentions.
