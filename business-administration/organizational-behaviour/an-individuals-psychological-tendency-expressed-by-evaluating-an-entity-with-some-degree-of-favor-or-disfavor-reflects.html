An individual’s psychological tendency expressed by evaluating an entity with some degree of favor or disfavor reflects:

(A) ** an attitude

(B) one’s mood

(C) Values

(D) Beliefs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An attitude has been defined as “a psychological tendency that is expressed by evaluating a particular entity with some degree of favor or disfavor.” Attitudes are, thus, a person’s evaluation of something else. Attitudes have three components: Cognitive, affective, and behavioral intentions.

Concept note-2: -Presentation on theme: “Attitude Attitude is a psychological tendency which is expressed through evaluative statements or judgments regarding people, objects or events.

Concept note-3: -Acting in ways consistent with one’s personal values and the commonly held values of the organization and society is called: a. emotional behavior.

Concept note-4: -Attributional Bias The first is called the fundamental attribution error. This error is a tendency to underestimate the effects of external or situational causes of behavior and to overestimate the effects of internal or personal causes.
