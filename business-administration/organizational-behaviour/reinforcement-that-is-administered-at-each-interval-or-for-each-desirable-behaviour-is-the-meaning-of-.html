Reinforcement that is administered at each interval or for each desirable behaviour is the meaning of ____

(A) ** Fixed reinforcement

(B) Interval reinforcement

(C) Ratio reinforcement

(D) Variable reinforcement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A fixed-interval reinforcement is when the reward only happens after a certain amount of time. For example, every 5 minutes, a reward will be given when the right action occurs; participants do not get a reward between intervals.

Concept note-2: -Fixed refers to the number of responses between reinforcements, or the amount of time between reinforcements, which is set and unchanging. Variable refers to the number of responses or amount of time between reinforcements, which varies or changes.

Concept note-3: -fixed interval reinforcement schedule: behavior is rewarded after a set amount of time. fixed ratio reinforcement schedule: set number of responses must occur before a behavior is rewarded.

Concept note-4: -Fixed-interval schedules are those where the first response is rewarded only after a specified amount of time has elapsed. This schedule causes high amounts of responding near the end of the interval but slower responding immediately after the delivery of the reinforcer.
