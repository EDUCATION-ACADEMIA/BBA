The following are true about needs for achievement propose by David McClelland ____

(A) ** the desire to accomplish challenging tasks

(B) the desire to influence and control one’s environment

(C) the desire to be friendly and have close interpersonal relationship

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -McClelland’s Human Motivation Theory states that every person has one of three main driving motivators: the needs for achievement, affiliation, or power. These motivators are not inherent; we develop them through our culture and life experiences. Achievers like to solve problems and achieve goals.

Concept note-2: -The theory adopts an interactional model and considers that achievement motivation results from the interaction of five components: personality factors, situational factors: resultant or behavioural tendencies, emotional reactions, and achievement related behaviour.

Concept note-3: –Psychologist David McClelland (1958) proposed a measure, called “need for achievement” (n Ach), defined as the desire to attain a difficult, but desired, goal.

Concept note-4: -McClelland’s Acquired Needs Motivation Theory says that humans have three types of emotional needs: achievement, power and affiliation. Individuals can have any mix of these needs. Their motivations and behaviors are shaped by the strength and blend of their specific needs.
