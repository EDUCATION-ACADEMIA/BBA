ORGANIZATIONAL BEHAVIOUR

[SOURCES]
OB comes from which behavioural sciences:

(A) Psychology, sociology and anthropology.

(B) Psychology, social psychology and anthropology.

(C) ** Psychology, sociology, social psychology and anthropology.

(D) Psychology, sociology and social psychology.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -OB is an applied behavioral science built on contributions from a number of behavioral disciplines, mainly psychology and social psychology, sociology, and anthropology.

Concept note-2: -The field of organizational behavior (OB) deals with the behavior and impact of the interaction of individuals, groups, and structures within an organization, in order to apply the knowledge to improve the effectiveness of the organization. Effectively an applied science, OB, draws from various social sciences.

Concept note-3: -Organizational Behavior (OB) is a discipline of social science that seeks explanations for human behavior in organizations.

Concept note-4: -THE BEHAVIORAL SCIENCES Organizational behavior scientists study four primary areas of behavioral science: individual behavior, group behavior, organizational structure, and organizational processes.

Concept note-5: -The major behavioral science disciplines that contributed to the development of organizational behavior are psychology, sociology, anthropology, management and medicine.
