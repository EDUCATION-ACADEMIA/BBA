Which one is the example of Positive Reinforcement

(A) Employee may be transferred to another place which is not to his/her liking

(B) ** Professor receives high teaching evaluations when a student gets a good grade

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An example of positive reinforcement is providing a sticker to a student once they’ve completed an assignment. An example of negative reinforcement is allowing the student to leave circle time for a five-minute break after they use a break card.

Concept note-2: -Nonverbal cues (thumbs up, jazz hands, clapping) Verbal praise (“thank you for participating, ” “excellent question”) Tangible rewards (bite-sized candies for class participation) Activity rewards (five minutes of free time for those who stay on task)

Concept note-3: -Verbal praise Verbal reinforcement is one of the most effective types of positive reinforcement that school staff can use. Describe the positive behaviour while giving praise. The comments should focus on what the student did right and be stated in positive language.

Concept note-4: -Clapping and cheering. Giving a high five. Giving a hug or pat on the back. Giving a thumbs-up.
