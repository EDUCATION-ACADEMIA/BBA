Sampling Error is the difference between sample and the population that exist only because of observations that happened to be selected for the sample.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A sampling error occurs when the sample used in the study is not representative of the whole population. Sampling is an analysis performed by selecting a number of observations from a larger population.

Concept note-2: -Sampling error refers to the difference between the sample and the population that exists only because of the observations that happened to be selected for the sample.

Concept note-3: -The total error of the survey estimate results from the two types of error: sampling error, which arises when only a part of the population is used to represent the whole population; and. non-sampling error which can occur at any stage of a sample survey and can also occur with censuses.

Concept note-4: -Sampling error This is because random samples are not identical to the population in terms of numerical measures like means and standard deviations.

Concept note-5: -Expert-Verified Answer option a will be the right answer. it decreases as sample size increases.
