In Excel, Columns are labelled as ____

(A) ** A, B, C, etc

(B) 1, 2, 3 etc

(C) A1, A2, etc.

(D) $A$1, $A$2, etc.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -By default, Excel uses the A1 reference style, which refers to columns as letters (A through IV, for a total of 256 columns), and refers to rows as numbers (1 through 65, 536). These letters and numbers are called row and column headings.

Concept note-2: -Columns in Spreadsheet are labelled by letters of alphabet. The cell address in excel is the combination of rows and column number like A1, C5, B15 etc.

Concept note-3: -In Microsoft Excel, columns are labelled as A, B, C, D, E and more. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-4: -Go to the Table tab on the Ribbon. In the Table Style Options group, select the Header Row check box to hide or display the table headers.
