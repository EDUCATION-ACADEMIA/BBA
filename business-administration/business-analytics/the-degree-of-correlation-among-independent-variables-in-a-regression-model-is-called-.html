The degree of correlation among independent variables in a regression model is called ____

(A) ** multicollinearity

(B) interaction

(C) the coefficient of determination

(D) the sum of squared errors (SSE)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Multicollinearity is the occurrence of high intercorrelations among two or more independent variables in a multiple regression model.

Concept note-2: -Multicollinearity refers to a situation in which more than two explanatory variables in a multiple regression model are highly linearly related. Concept note-3: -Multicollinearity occurs when independent variables in a regression model are correlated. This correlation is a problem because independent variables should be independent.

Concept note-4: -Multicollinearity exists when two or more of the predictors in a regression model are moderately or highly correlated with one another. Unfortunately, when it exists, it can wreak havoc on our analysis and thereby limit the research conclusions we can draw.

Concept note-5: -Multicollinearity is a situation where two or more predictors are highly linearly related. In general, an absolute correlation coefficient of >0.7 among two or more predictors indicates the presence of multicollinearity.
