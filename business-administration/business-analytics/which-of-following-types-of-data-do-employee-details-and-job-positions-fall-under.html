Which of following types of data do employee details and job positions fall under?

(A) Semi-structured Data

(B) Unstructured Data

(C) To-be Structured Data

(D) ** Structured Data

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Structured Data includes information on employees and their employment.

Concept note-2: -Common examples of structured data are Excel files or SQL databases. Each of these have structured rows and columns that can be sorted.

Concept note-3: -Examples of structured data include names, dates, addresses, credit card numbers, stock information, geolocation, and more. Structured data is highly organized and easily understood by machine language.

Concept note-4: -Sources of Structured Data: SQL Databases. Spreadsheets such as Excel. OLTP Systems.

Concept note-5: -Structured data is when data is in a standardized format, has a well-defined structure, complies to a data model, follows a persistent order, and is easily accessed by humans and programs. This data type is generally stored in a database.
