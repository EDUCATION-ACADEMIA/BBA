Bayes’ theorem is a method used to compute ____ probabilities.

(A) ** posterior

(B) prior

(C) empirical

(D) conditional

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bayes’ Theorem formula is an important method for calculating conditional probabilities. It is used to calculate posterior probabilities. Bayes’s theorem describes the probability of an event, based on conditions that might be related to the event.

Concept note-2: -Posterior probability is calculated by updating the prior probability using Bayes’ theorem. In statistical terms, the posterior probability is the probability of event A occurring given that event B has occurred.

Concept note-3: -Bayes theorem provides a way to calculate the probability of a hypothesis based on its prior probability, the probabilities of observing various data given the hypothesis, and the observed data itself.

Concept note-4: -In statistics and probability theory, the Bayes’ theorem (also known as the Bayes’ rule) is a mathematical formula used to determine the conditional probability of events.

Concept note-5: -Posterior probability = prior probability + new evidence (called likelihood).
