In Excel, Rows are labelled as ____

(A) A, B, C, etc

(B) ** 1, 2, 3 etc

(C) A1, A2, etc.

(D) D.$A$1, $A$2, etc.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -By default, Excel uses the A1 reference style, which refers to columns as letters (A through IV, for a total of 256 columns), and refers to rows as numbers (1 through 65, 536). These letters and numbers are called row and column headings.

Concept note-2: -In all spreadsheet programs including Microsoft Excel, rows are labeled using numbers (e.g., 1 to 1, 048, 576).

Concept note-3: -Row header or Row heading is the gray-colored column located on the left side of column 1 in the worksheet, which contains the numbers (1, 2, 3, etc.) where it helps out to identify each row in the worksheet.

Concept note-4: -In the modern versions of Excel there are 1, 048, 576 rows. In older versions of Excel (2003 and prior) there were 65, 536 rows.
