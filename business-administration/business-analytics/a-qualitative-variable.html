A qualitative variable

(A) Always refers to a sample

(B) ** Is not numeric

(C) Has only two possible outcomes

(D) All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A qualitative variable, also called a categorical variable, is a variable that isn’t numerical. It describes data that fits into categories.

Concept note-2: -Qualitative variables are often descriptive but can sometimes be given a numeric value. For example, a dataset may represent the color of a person’s eyes by numbers such as 1, 2, and 3, where 1 = blue, 2 = brown, 3 = green.

Concept note-3: -A numeric variable (also called quantitative variable) is a quantifiable characteristic whose values are numbers (except numbers which are codes standing up for categories). Numeric variables may be either continuous or discrete.

Concept note-4: -Qualitative data can be observed and recorded. This data type is non-numerical in nature. This type of data is collected through methods of observations, one-to-one interviews, conducting focus groups, and similar methods.

Concept note-5: -Also known as categorical variables, qualitative variables are variables with no natural sense of ordering. They are therefore measured on a nominal scale.
