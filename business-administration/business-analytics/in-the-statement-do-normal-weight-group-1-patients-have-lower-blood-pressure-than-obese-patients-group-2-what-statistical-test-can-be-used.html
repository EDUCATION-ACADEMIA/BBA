In the statement, “Do normal weight (group 1) patients have lower blood pressure than obese patients (group 2)?” what statistical test can be used?

(A) ** t-test / Anova

(B) Regression

(C) Correlation

(D) chi-square

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is this test for? The one-way analysis of variance (ANOVA) is used to determine whether there are any statistically significant differences between the means of three or more independent (unrelated) groups.

Concept note-2: -What Is a T-Test? A t-test is an inferential statistic used to determine if there is a significant difference between the means of two groups and how they are related.

Concept note-3: -In ANOVA, the null hypothesis is that there is no difference among group means. If any group differs significantly from the overall group mean, then the ANOVA will report a statistically significant result.

Concept note-4: -The only difference between one-way and two-way ANOVA is the number of independent variables. A one-way ANOVA has one independent variable, while a two-way ANOVA has two.
