BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

BUSINESS ANALYTICS

[SOURCES]
Includes the people, processes and technologies needed to manage and protect the company’s data assets in order to guarantee generally understandable, correct, complete, trustworthy, secure and discoverable corporate data.

(A) Collaborative business intelligence

(B) Security

(C) Data discovery

(D) ** Data governance and trust

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Data stewards take care of data assets, making certain that the actual data is consistent with the data governance plan, linked with other data assets, and in control in terms of data quality, compliance, or security.

Concept note-2: -Data governance (DG) is the process of managing the availability, usability, integrity and security of the data in enterprise systems, based on internal data standards and policies that also control data usage. Effective data governance ensures that data is consistent and trustworthy and doesn’t get misused.

Concept note-3: -Specifically, there are four major pillars to keep in mind for good data management: Strategy and Governance, Standards, Integration, and Quality. Most importantly, in order to be data-driven, an organization must embrace data as a corporate asset.

Concept note-4: -Data governance defined It includes the actions people must take, the processes they must follow, and the technology that supports them throughout the data life cycle. Data governance means setting internal standards-data policies-that apply to how data is gathered, stored, processed, and disposed of.
