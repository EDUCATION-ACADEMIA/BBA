The number of individual networking addresses required for the Internet is currently ____

(A) ** more than 4.3 billion

(B) more than 19 billion

(C) unknowable

(D) one per country

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A 32-bit IPv4 (the “v” stands for “version") address is big enough to support 232 computers. That’s about four billion (4 · 109), but there are more than seven billion people on Earth, so there aren’t enough IP addresses to go around.

Concept note-2: -In addition, since there are more than 2 32 connected Ethernet devices on the planet, IP addresses can be reused across multiple smaller networks while those machines are still accessible via a process called Network Address Translation (NAT).

Concept note-3: -The internet is a global network connecting millions of computers.

Concept note-4: -A computer network comprises two or more computers that are connected-either by cables (wired) or WiFi (wireless)-with the purpose of transmitting, exchanging, or sharing data and resources.
