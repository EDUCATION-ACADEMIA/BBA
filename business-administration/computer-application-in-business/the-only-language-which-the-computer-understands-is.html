The only language which the computer understands is

(A) Assembly Language

(B) ** Binary Language

(C) BASIC

(D) C Language

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Machine language gives instructions as 0’s and 1’s and is the only language that the computer understands.

Concept note-2: -Machine code, also known as machine language, is the elemental language of computers. It is read by the computer’s central processing unit (CPU), is composed of digital binary numbers and looks like a very long sequence of zeros and ones.

Concept note-3: -To make sense of complicated data, your computer has to encode it in binary. Binary is a base 2 number system. Base 2 means there are only two digits-1 and 0-which correspond to the on and off states your computer can understand.

Concept note-4: -Binary literals don’t exist in C. The closest you have are hexadecimal, as they follow the binary bitpattern closely.
