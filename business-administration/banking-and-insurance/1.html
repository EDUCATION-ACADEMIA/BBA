Legally, in case of fire insurance, reserve for expired risk is ____ % of net premium.

(A) 30

(B) ** 50

(C) 100

(D) 90

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There is an unexpired liability under various policies which may occur during the remaining term of the policy beyond the year and therefore, a provision for unexpired risks is made at normally 50% in case of Fire Insurance and 100% of in case of Marine Insurance.

Concept note-2: -Unexpired Risk Reserve is the present value of loss and expense payments to be provided for by premiums covering the period from the valuation date to expiry on all contracts in force on the valuation date. A loss reserve is a provision for an insurerâ€™s liability for claims.

Concept note-4: -Unexpired Risk Reserve (URR) = Unearned Premium Reserve (UPR) + Additional Reserve for Unexpired Risks (AURR).
