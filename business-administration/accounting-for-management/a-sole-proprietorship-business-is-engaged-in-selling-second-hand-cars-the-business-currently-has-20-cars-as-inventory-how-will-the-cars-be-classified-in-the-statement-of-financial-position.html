A sole proprietorship business is engaged in selling second hand cars. The business currently has 20 cars as inventory. How will the cars be classified in the Statement of Financial Position?

(A) ** current asset

(B) non-current assets

(C) current liabilities

(D) non-current liabilities

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In accounting terms, inventory is considered an asset. On the balance sheet, it is recorded as a current asset because businesses typically use, sell or replenish it in less than 12 months.

Concept note-2: -Some examples of current assets include cash, cash equivalents, short-term investments, accounts receivable, inventory, supplies, and prepaid expenses.

Concept note-3: -Current assets include cash, cash equivalents, accounts receivable, stock inventory, marketable securities, pre-paid liabilities, and other liquid assets.

Concept note-4: -Some examples of long-term assets include: Fixed assets like property, plant, and equipment, which can include land, machinery, buildings, fixtures, and vehicles. Long-term investments such as stocks and bonds or real estate, or investments made in other companies.
