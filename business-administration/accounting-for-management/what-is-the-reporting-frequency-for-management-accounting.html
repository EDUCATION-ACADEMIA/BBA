What is the reporting frequency for management accounting?

(A) Once at the end of accounting period

(B) ** As frequently as information needed for decision making purposes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Specific to certain decision points like inventory valuation, cost control, cost accounting, etc. Most companies have a statutory filing of Quarterly, half-yearly and annual reports.

Concept note-2: -Management accounting reports are usually prepared on a weekly or monthly basis by managers or business analysts. Financial accounting reports are filed annually.

Concept note-3: -Monthly management reports are the financial statements and business performance reports prepared by internal stakeholders or external experts on a monthly basis.

Concept note-4: -The accounting system which provides relevant information to the managers to make policies, plans and strategies for running the business effectively is known as Management Accounting. Financial statements are prepared for a fixed period, i.e. one year. Management Reports are prepared whenever needed.

Concept note-5: -Management accounting helps managers strategize, course correct and make informed decisions based on the analysis and interpretation of the financial data related to the internal operations of the company. Management accounting is a virtual tool to help the managers of an organization steer it towards their goals.
