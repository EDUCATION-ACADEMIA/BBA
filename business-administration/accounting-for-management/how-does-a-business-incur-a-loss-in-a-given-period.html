How does a business incur a loss in a given period?

(A) when total expenses for the period is equal to total income earned

(B) ** when total income earned is lesser than total expenses incurred

(C) when total expenses for the period is greater than beginning equity

(D) when there are no expenses for the period

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A net loss occurs when the sum total of expenses exceeds the total income or revenue generated by a business, project, transaction, or investment. Businesses would report a net loss on the income statement, effectively as a negative net profit.

Concept note-2: -In business, we say one makes a profit when the business makes money or experiences a return of investment (ROI). A business experiences a loss when it doesnâ€™t make money on a product or service but loses part of more than what the owner initially invested.

Concept note-3: -When income is less than expenses, you have a budget deficit.-too little cash to provide for your wants or needs. A budget deficit is not sustainable; it is not financially viable.

Concept note-4: -The difference between the revenue and cost (found by subtracting the cost from the revenue) is called the profitThe difference between revenue and cost when revenue exceeds the cost incurred in operating the business..
