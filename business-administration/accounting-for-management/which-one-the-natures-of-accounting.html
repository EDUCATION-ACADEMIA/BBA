Which one the Natures of Accounting?

(A) Classifying, Measuring, Communicating, Controlling, Recording

(B) Identifying, Classifying, Measuring, Controlling, Recording

(C) ** Identifying, Measuring, Classifying, Communicating, Recording

(D) Identifying, Controlling, Measuring, Communicating, Recording

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Financial Accounting is that branch of accounting which involves identifying, measuring, recording, classifying, summarising the business transactions, i.e. it involves the steps from Identifying, Recording of transactions to Summarisation, and communicating the financial data.

Concept note-2: -Nature of Accounting Accounting can be defined as the act of classifying and summarising money-related matters in a detailed manner that can be easily interpreted. This definition highlights both the nature and scope of financial accounting.

Concept note-3: -The 3 types of accounting include cost, managerial, and financial accounting.

Concept note-4: -Identification: It means to determine what transactions to record. Measurement: It means quantification (including estimates) of business transactions into financial terms by using monetary unit.
